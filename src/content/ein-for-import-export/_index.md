An import/export business moves goods across US borders. Customs clearance, importer-of-record filings, customs bonds, freight forwarder accounts, and international wire transfers all require a single business identifier. ## Do Import/Export Businesses <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

Import/export businesses operating as a US LLC or corporation need an EIN. CBP requires an importer of record to carry an identification number, and the EIN fills that role for a US entity. Sole proprietors can sometimes use an SSN, but an EIN protects personal data on customs and banking forms.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Import/export LLC | Yes (required) |
| Import/export corporation | Yes (required) |
| Sole proprietor importer with SSN | Recommended |
| Non-US resident with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Business with US employees | Yes (required) |
| Personal one-time shipment | No |

### Why Import/Export Businesses Get an EIN

1. **Customs clearance** — CBP and customs brokers require an importer identification number
2. **Customs bonds** — surety companies reference your EIN on continuous and single-entry bonds
3. **Business banking** — open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) to pay duties and suppliers
4. **Freight forwarder accounts** — forwarders and 3PLs set up accounts under your EIN
5. **Privacy** — keep your SSN off invoices, W-9s, and commercial documents
6. **Tax compliance** — required on US business tax and information returns

See [who needs an EIN](/who-needs-ein/) for the complete eligibility breakdown.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>for an Import/Export Business?</Accent>

You get an EIN by filing IRS Form SS-4 with the IRS. US residents with an SSN use the instant online tool. Non-US residents fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS charges $0, and most correct filings return an EIN within 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Required |
|--------|-------|------|--------------|
| [IRS online](/how-to-get-ein/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |

| [ein.so Standard](/apply/) | 4-7 days | $49 | No |
| [ein.so Express](/apply/) | 2-3 days | $97 | No |

The online tool blocks applicants without an SSN. That blocks most non-resident importers and exporters. The fax method is the working path. ein.so prepares the SS-4, faxes it, and emails your EIN assignment letter (CP 575). See the full [EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/).

### Best Business Structure for an Import/Export Business

Most import/export operators choose one of two structures:

- **[Single-member LLC](/ein-for-non-residents/)** — liability protection, US business identity, enables banking and customs accounts
- **C-corporation** — used when raising US capital or holding US investors

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an <Accent>Import/Export EIN?</Accent>

Non-residents apply by faxing IRS Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627, using a passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for non-resident importers and exporters. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in a US state through a registered agent. This creates a US business address and a legal entity to hold the EIN, the customs bond, and the bank account. You complete formation online from your home country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home address. No foreign business registration number is required for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. List your import or export activity on Line 16. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) for use with CBP and banks.</Step>
</Steps>

### Import/Export SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Wholesale," "Import," or "Export" |
| Line 17 (Products) | Describe the goods you trade |

## How Does an Import/Export Business Use an EIN for <Accent>Customs Clearance?</Accent>

An import/export business uses its EIN as the importer of record identification number with US Customs and Border Protection. CBP and customs brokers reference the EIN on entry summaries, customs bonds, and ACE filings. Without an EIN, shipments face clearance delays at the port of entry.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Importer of Record ID" index={1}>CBP requires an importer of record to carry an identification number for entry filings. A US business entity uses its EIN for this purpose. Your customs broker requests the EIN before filing the entry summary (CBP Form 7501).</Card>
<Card title="Customs Bond" index={2}>A continuous customs bond and single-entry bonds list your EIN as the importer identification. Surety companies ask for the EIN during underwriting. The bond guarantees payment of duties and taxes to CBP on your shipments.</Card>
<Card title="Customs Broker Accounts" index={3}>Licensed customs brokers set up your account under your EIN before clearing goods. The broker files entries through the Automated Commercial Environment (ACE) using your business identification.</Card>
<Card title="Freight Forwarders and 3PL" index={4}>Freight forwarders, NVOCCs, and third-party logistics providers open accounts under your EIN. The EIN ties your shipments, duty payments, and carrier billing to one business identity.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Customs filing rules are specific and change. Confirm importer of record, bond amounts, and ACE requirements with a licensed customs broker. ein.so files your EIN and does not provide customs or legal advice.

## What Should an Import/Export Business Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Customs duties are separate from income tax and are paid to CBP on imported goods. ein.so handles the EIN only and does not give tax advice.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return reporting transactions between the LLC and its foreign owner.</Card>
<Card title="Customs Duties and Tariffs" index={2}>Imported goods carry duties and tariffs set by the Harmonized Tariff Schedule. CBP collects these at entry, separate from income tax. A licensed customs broker classifies your goods and calculates the duty rate.</Card>

## How Does an Import/Export EIN Compare to <Accent>Other IDs?</Accent>

An EIN identifies your US business to the IRS, CBP, and banks. It differs from a customs bond, an ITIN, and a foreign business registration number. The EIN is the federal tax ID; the others serve customs, individual tax, and home-country roles.

| Identifier | Issued By | Purpose | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| EIN | IRS | US business tax ID and importer ID | $0 (ein.so $49) |
| ITIN | IRS | Individual tax ID for non-residents | $0 (ein.so $197) |
| Customs bond | Surety company | Guarantees duty payment to CBP | Varies |
| Foreign company number | Home country | Home-country registration | Varies |

An [ITIN](/ein-without-ssn/) identifies a person, not a business. ein.so files an ITIN for $197 Standard or $297 Express when an owner needs personal US tax identification. The EIN identifies the entity that imports or exports the goods. Most import/export operators need the EIN first.
