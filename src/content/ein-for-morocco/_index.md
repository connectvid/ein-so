## How Do Moroccan Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Moroccan citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Morocco passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Moroccan applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Moroccan founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Morocco.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Morocco passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Morocco address. No IF, ICE, or Morocco registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Morocco address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Morocco passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Moroccan Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Morocco Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Morocco resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. Reporting duties still apply. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file information returns each year.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Morocco Reporting" index={2}>Morocco residents declare income to the DGI under Morocco rules. A US LLC does not remove your Morocco obligations. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Morocco accountant.</Card>
<Card title="US-Morocco Tax Treaty" index={3}>A US-Morocco income tax treaty exists and can affect withholding and double-taxation relief. Treaty application depends on income type and residency. Confirm any treaty benefit with a CPA before relying on it.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi and separate from the EIN.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a Morocco accountant and a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters. Your EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance, not the end of it. See the [Form 5472 guide](/form-5472-guide/) and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for next steps.
