## How Do Sri Lankan Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Sri Lankan citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Sri Lanka passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Sri Lankan founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Sri Lanka.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Sri Lanka passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Sri Lanka address. No TIN or local registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Sri Lanka address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Sri Lanka passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Sri Lankan Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Sri Lanka Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Sri Lankan resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still file annual US information returns to stay compliant. Confirm your specific tax position with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>

## How Does a US EIN Compare to a <Accent>Sri Lanka TIN?</Accent>

A US EIN and a Sri Lanka TIN are different identifiers issued by different authorities, and neither replaces the other. The Sri Lanka TIN has no standing in the United States. You need a US EIN to run a US entity, regardless of your Sri Lanka registration.

<InfoBox title="Key differences at a glance">
<ul>
<li>The EIN is a 9-digit federal tax ID issued by the IRS for US entities.</li>
<li>The Sri Lanka TIN is a 9-digit ID issued by the IRD for local taxpayers.</li>
<li>The IRS does not recognize your Sri Lanka TIN for US filings.</li>
<li>You can hold both: a Sri Lanka TIN for local business and a US EIN for your US LLC.</li>
<li>Getting an EIN does not register you for taxes in Sri Lanka.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

The practical takeaway is simple. If you own or operate a US LLC, you apply for a US EIN through [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/), and you keep your Sri Lanka registrations for local activity. The two systems run in parallel.
