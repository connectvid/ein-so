## Do Trucking and Transportation Companies <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A trucking company needs an EIN when it operates as an LLC or corporation, hires drivers, or registers with the FMCSA for a USDOT number and operating authority. Sole-proprietor owner-operators can use an SSN, but an EIN keeps that SSN off broker and insurance paperwork.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Trucking LLC or corporation | Yes (required) |
| Owner-operator sole proprietor | Recommended |
| Carrier with employee drivers | Yes (required) |
| Non-US owner with a US trucking LLC | Yes (required) |
| Registering for a USDOT or MC number as an entity | Yes |
| Hobby hauling with no business entity | No |

### Why Trucking Companies Get an EIN

1. **FMCSA registration** — the EIN serves as the business tax ID for a USDOT number and MC operating authority
2. **IFTA fuel tax** — most states list the EIN on the IFTA license for a trucking LLC or corporation
3. **Fleet insurance** — underwriters identify the carrier by EIN on commercial auto and cargo policies
4. **Business banking** — open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) for settlements, fuel cards, and maintenance
5. **SSN privacy** — brokers and shippers request a W-9; an EIN keeps your SSN off it
6. **Payroll** — required to run [employee driver payroll](/ein-for-hiring-employees/)

See [who needs an EIN](/who-needs-ein/) for full eligibility details.

## How Do Trucking Companies <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

US owners apply online at irs.gov for free and receive the EIN the same session, because the online tool requires an SSN. Non-US owners fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days. The IRS charges $0 by every method.

Most carriers choose one of two structures:

- **[LLC](/ein-for-llc/)** — limits personal liability, which matters with high accident and cargo-claim exposure, and enables clean business banking

## How Does a Non-US Owner Get an EIN for a <Accent>US Trucking LLC?</Accent>

A non-US owner of a US trucking LLC gets an EIN with no SSN and no ITIN by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. You enter your passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool blocks applicants without an SSN, so fax is the path.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Trucking LLC">Register the LLC in a US state through a registered agent. This gives you a US business entity and address to attach the EIN to. You complete this online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US presence is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Set Line 16 principal activity to "trucking" or "transportation." ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Trucking SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US trucking LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Trucking" or "Transportation" |

The most common rejection cause for non-US applicants is address formatting that does not map to the SS-4 layout. ## What Tax and Compliance Steps Follow the EIN for a <Accent>Trucking Business?</Accent>

After the EIN, a trucking company files federal and state returns, IFTA fuel taxes, and the Heavy Highway Vehicle Use Tax for qualifying trucks. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC also files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, with a $25,000 penalty for not filing.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120 (Foreign-Owned LLC)" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, separate from income tax. Confirm your filings with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Heavy Highway Vehicle Use Tax" index={2}>Trucks at or above 55,000 pounds taxable gross weight may owe the Heavy Highway Vehicle Use Tax on IRS Form 2290. The EIN is required on Form 2290. Confirm your truck's weight class and due date with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="IFTA Quarterly Returns" index={3}>Interstate carriers file quarterly IFTA fuel tax returns with the base jurisdiction. The EIN identifies the carrier across member states and provinces. Confirm your state's IFTA rules with that state's tax office.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties on your trucking entity.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

<Alert type="warning" title="Trucking taxes are detailed - confirm with a CPA">
<ol>
<li>Form 2290 weight thresholds and due dates vary by when the truck is first used.</li>
<li>IFTA registration and rates are set by your base jurisdiction, not by the IRS.</li>
<li>A foreign-owned LLC has a $25,000 Form 5472 penalty for non-filing.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with motor carrier and non-resident matters. Your EIN is the starting point for trucking tax compliance.
