<Section>
<Capsule>**Form 5472 is due April 15 of the year following your tax year (same as your corporate tax return).** A 6-month automatic extension is available by filing Form 7004, extending the deadline to October 15. For new LLCs, the first filing is due by the 15th day of the 4th month after the tax year ends.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>Deadlines</Label>

## Form 5472 <Accent>Deadlines</Accent>

| Situation | Deadline |
|-----------|----------|
| Calendar year LLC | April 15 |
| With extension (Form 7004) | October 15 |
| First year (new LLC) | 15th day of 4th month after tax year end |
| Fiscal year LLC | 15th day of 4th month after fiscal year end |

### How to Get an Extension
1. File Form 7004 (Application for Automatic Extension) by April 15
2. This gives you an automatic 6-month extension to October 15
3. No reason required — the extension is automatic
4. The extension is for filing, not for payment of any taxes due
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>First Year</Label>

## First Year <Accent>Filing</Accent>

If your LLC was formed during the tax year:
- Your first Form 5472 covers from formation date to December 31
- Due by April 15 of the following year
- Report ALL transactions from formation date onward
- Even if LLC had no activity, you must file

**Example:** LLC formed July 15, 2026 → First Form 5472 covers July 15 - December 31, 2026 → Due April 15, 2027
</Section>

## Late Filing <Accent>Consequences</Accent>

| Action | Result |
|--------|--------|
| File on time | No penalty |
| File with extension | No penalty |
| File 1 day late | $25,000 penalty |
| Don't file at all | $25,000 + additional $25,000 per 30 days |
