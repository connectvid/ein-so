<Section>
<Capsule>**To get a Tax ID number: businesses apply for an EIN using Form SS-4, US citizens get an SSN using Form SS-5, and non-residents get an ITIN using Form W-7.** The type of Tax ID you need depends on whether you are an individual or business and whether you are a US citizen or non-resident.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Label>By Type</Label>
## How to Get Each <Accent>Tax ID</Accent>

| Tax ID | Form | Method | Speed |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) | SS-4 | Online/fax/phone | Instant-7 days |
| SSN | SS-5 | Social Security office | 2-4 weeks |
| [ITIN](/how-to-get-itin/) | W-7 | Mail/CAA | 7-11 weeks |
| [PTIN](/preparer-tax-id/) | Online | irs.gov/ptin | Instant |
</Section>

## Which Tax ID <Accent>Do You Need?</Accent>

| You Are | You Need |
|---------|----------|
| US citizen starting a business | SSN (already have) + EIN |
| Non-resident with US LLC | EIN + possibly ITIN |
| Non-resident with US income | ITIN |
| Tax preparer | PTIN |
| Employer hiring workers | EIN |
