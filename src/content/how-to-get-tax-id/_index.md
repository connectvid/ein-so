A Tax Identification Number is the number the IRS uses to track a person or business for federal tax purposes. The United States issues four main types: the EIN for businesses, the SSN for citizens and authorized residents, the ITIN for individuals who cannot get an SSN, and the PTIN for paid tax preparers. The right Tax ID depends on whether you are an individual or a business and whether you are a US citizen or a non-resident. This guide explains every type, the exact IRS form for each, the timeline, and the cost. It then walks non-residents through the EIN process step by step.

## What Are the Four Types of <Accent>US Tax ID Number?</Accent>

The four US Tax ID types are the EIN, the SSN, the ITIN, and the PTIN. Each serves a different person or purpose. The EIN identifies a business. The SSN identifies a US citizen. The ITIN identifies an individual who is not eligible for an SSN. The PTIN identifies a paid tax preparer.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="EIN — Employer Identification Number" index={1}>The EIN is a 9-digit tax ID formatted XX-XXXXXXX. Every US LLC, corporation, and partnership needs one to open a bank account, hire workers, and file federal returns. The IRS issues it on Form SS-4. The fee is $0. See the [full EIN guide](/how-to-get-ein/).</Card>
<Card title="SSN — Social Security Number" index={2}>The SSN is a 9-digit personal tax ID formatted 9XX-XX-XXXX for US citizens and work-authorized residents. The Social Security Administration issues it on Form SS-5. The fee is $0. Non-residents abroad cannot get an SSN, so they use an EIN or ITIN instead.</Card>
<Card title="ITIN — Individual Taxpayer Identification Number" index={3}>The ITIN is a 9-digit personal tax ID for people who must file US taxes but cannot get an SSN. The IRS issues it on Form W-7. The fee is $0. See the [ITIN guide](/how-to-get-itin/). ein.so files ITIN applications for $197 Standard or $297 Express.</Card>
<Card title="PTIN — Preparer Tax Identification Number" index={4}>The PTIN identifies a paid US tax preparer on returns they sign. The IRS issues it online at irs.gov/ptin. The fee is $19.75 per year. Business owners filing only their own returns do not need a PTIN. See [preparer tax ID](/preparer-tax-id/).</Card>

## Which Tax ID Number <Accent>Do You Need?</Accent>

You need an EIN if you run a US business entity, an SSN if you are a US citizen, and an ITIN if you must file US taxes without SSN eligibility. A non-resident who owns a US LLC needs an EIN for the company. The same non-resident may also need an ITIN for personal US tax filings.

| You Are | Tax ID You Need | Form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| US citizen starting a business | SSN (already held) + EIN | SS-5 + SS-4 |
| Non-resident with a US LLC | EIN | SS-4 |
| Non-resident with US-source personal income | ITIN | W-7 |
| Employer hiring US workers | EIN | SS-4 |
| Paid tax preparer | PTIN | Online |
| Non-resident selling on a US platform | EIN | SS-4 |

Most non-residents who contact ein.so need an EIN, not an SSN or ITIN. The EIN attaches to the US LLC and unlocks banking, payment processors, and federal filings. An ITIN is a separate personal number that you only need if you must file a personal US return or claim a treaty benefit. Confirm your exact personal filing requirement with a CPA. ## What Is the Difference Between an EIN and an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

An EIN is a tax ID for a business entity, while an ITIN is a tax ID for an individual person. A non-resident uses an EIN for the US LLC and an ITIN for personal US tax matters. The two numbers do not replace each other. The IRS charges $0 for both.

| Feature | EIN | ITIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies | A business entity | An individual person |
| Format | XX-XXXXXXX | 9XX-XX-XXXX |
| IRS form | SS-4 | W-7 |
| Government fee | $0 | $0 |
| Non-resident timeline | 4-7 business days by fax | 7-11 weeks |
| ein.so service fee | $49 / $97 | $197 / $297 |
| Needed for US bank account | Yes | No |
| Needed for personal US return | No | Often |

A common mistake is applying for an ITIN when you only need an EIN. If your goal is to open a US bank account, accept Stripe payments, or sell on Amazon through a US LLC, the EIN does that work. You file for an ITIN only when you personally owe a US filing, such as a partner in a US partnership or an owner claiming a treaty rate. Confirm with a CPA before paying for an ITIN you do not need. ## How Do Non-Residents Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Non-residents get an EIN by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for applicants without one. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register a US LLC in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a legal entity and a US business presence to attach the EIN to. You can complete the formation online from any country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, your formation date, and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or home-country business number is required to apply for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes every field to prevent the errors that cause silent IRS rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The completed SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Non-Residents

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign home or business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started a new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is a foreign address that does not map to the SS-4 layout. The IRS silently rejects malformed international addresses without explaining why. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. The biggest mistakes that slow a Tax ID application are using the online EIN tool without an SSN, entering a foreign address in the wrong format, and applying for the wrong number entirely. Each error costs days or weeks. ein.so prevents these on the EIN application.

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these Tax ID application errors">
<ol>
<li>Using the online EIN tool without an SSN. The tool blocks non-residents. Use the fax method to 855-215-1627 instead.</li>
<li>Formatting a foreign address incorrectly on Form SS-4 Line 4. The IRS rejects malformed international addresses without notice.</li>
<li>Applying for an ITIN when you only need an EIN for a US LLC. This wastes time and a $197 service fee.</li>
<li>Leaving Line 7b blank. Non-residents must enter a passport number as the responsible party identification.</li>
<li>Choosing the wrong reason on Line 10. "Started a new business" fits most new LLC applicants.</li>
