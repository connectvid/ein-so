## How Do Myanmar Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Myanmar citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Myanmar passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Myanmar applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Myanmar founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Myanmar.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Myanmar passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Myanmar address. No Myanmar TIN or local registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Myanmar address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Myanmar passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### Myanmar-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Myanmar Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Myanmar address (street, township, region) |

| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Myanmar passport number |

| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Myanmar Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Myanmar resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, which means the LLC itself often pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. A foreign-owned single-member LLC still must file information returns each year. Confirm specifics with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Myanmar Reporting" index={2}>Myanmar residents follow local IRD rules for foreign company interests and income. Confirm your Myanmar reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a qualified Myanmar tax advisor.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={3}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is free to file at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Annual Deadlines" index={4}>Form 5472 is due with the LLC's annual return, generally April 15, with an extension available. Set reminders so you never miss the filing window and trigger the $25,000 penalty.</Card>

## Do Myanmar Applicants Need an ITIN as Well as an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Most Myanmar applicants need only an EIN to run a US LLC, and an ITIN is a separate personal tax ID for individuals with US filing obligations. An EIN identifies your business; an ITIN identifies you as a person. Many founders never need an ITIN.

<InfoBox title="EIN vs ITIN at a glance">
<ul>
<li>**EIN** identifies a business entity and is required to open US bank accounts, sell on Amazon, and file Form 5472.</li>
<li>**ITIN** identifies an individual for personal US tax filing and is requested by some payment platforms for treaty benefits.</li>
<li>ein.so files your EIN for $49 Standard or $97 Express.</li>
<li>ein.so files your ITIN for $197 Standard or $297 Express when you need one.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

Start with the EIN, because it unlocks your US LLC, banking, and payments. Add an ITIN later only if a tax filing or platform specifically requires your personal taxpayer number. A CPA confirms whether your situation needs both.
