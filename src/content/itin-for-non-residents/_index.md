<Section>
<Capsule>**Non-resident aliens who earn US-source income need an ITIN to file US tax returns.** Common scenarios: rental income from US property, investment income from US sources, scholarship income, and tax treaty benefit claims. Apply by submitting Form W-7 with Form 1040-NR (non-resident tax return) and identity documents.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>Who Needs</Label>
## Which Non-Residents <Accent>Need an ITIN?</Accent>

1. **Rental property owners** — US rental income requires tax filing
2. **Investors** — US dividends, interest, capital gains
3. **Students** — US scholarship income
4. **Spouses** — Filing jointly with US citizen spouse
5. **Tax treaty claimants** — Claiming reduced withholding
6. **Freelancers** — US-source service income

| Situation | Need ITIN? | Need EIN? |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| US rental income (individual) | Yes | No |
| US LLC ownership | Maybe | Yes |
| US investments | Yes | No |
| US business entity | No (use EIN) | Yes |

See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for business tax IDs.
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>Difference</Label>
## ITIN vs EIN for <Accent>Non-Residents</Accent>

| | ITIN | EIN |
|-|------|-----|
| For | Individuals | Businesses |
| Format | 9XX-XX-XXXX | XX-XXXXXXX |
| Use | Personal tax returns | Business tax returns |
| Application | Form W-7 | [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) |
| Processing | 7-11 weeks | 4-7 days (fax) |

Many non-residents need BOTH: an EIN for their LLC and an ITIN for personal tax filing. See [EIN vs ITIN](/ein-vs-itin/).
</Section>
