## Which Non-Residents <Accent>Need an ITIN?</Accent>

Non-residents need an ITIN when they receive US-source income that requires a US tax return and they cannot get an SSN. The most common triggers are US rental property income, US investment income, scholarship income, and tax treaty benefit claims. An ITIN is for individuals, not for companies.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Rental Property Owners" index={1}>Foreign owners of US real estate must report rental income on Form 1040-NR. The ITIN identifies the owner on the return. Without it, the property manager often withholds 30% of gross rent under default rules. Confirm your withholding position with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Investors in US Assets" index={2}>Non-residents earning US dividends, interest, or capital gains use an ITIN to file and to claim treaty-reduced withholding. Brokers request a tax ID on Form W-8BEN. The ITIN lets you complete that form correctly.</Card>
<Card title="Tax Treaty Claimants" index={3}>People claiming reduced US withholding under an income tax treaty enter their ITIN on Form W-8BEN. The treaty can lower the default 30% rate on certain passive income. The exact rate depends on your country and income type.</Card>
<Card title="Spouses and Dependents" index={4}>A foreign spouse or dependent listed on a US tax return needs an ITIN to be claimed. This applies when a US citizen or resident files jointly or claims a dependent who has no SSN.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## What Is the Difference Between an <Accent>ITIN and an EIN?</Accent>

An ITIN identifies an individual, and an EIN identifies a business. The ITIN goes on Form 1040-NR for personal tax filing. The EIN goes on business returns and on banking applications for a US LLC. The two numbers serve different purposes and are not interchangeable.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Purpose" index={1}>The ITIN exists so individuals without an SSN can file US tax returns. The EIN exists so a business can file taxes, open a bank account, and hire. A US LLC uses an EIN, never an ITIN, for its own filings.</Card>
<Card title="Application Path" index={2}>You apply for an ITIN with Form W-7 plus a tax return, mailed to the IRS. You apply for an EIN with [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/), faxed to 855-215-1627. The EIN fax path is far faster than the ITIN mail path.</Card>
<Card title="Speed" index={3}>An EIN arrives in 4-7 business days by fax through ein.so. An ITIN takes 7-11 weeks. There is no expedited or instant ITIN. See [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for the business timeline.</Card>
<Card title="Who Needs Both" index={4}>A non-resident who owns a US LLC and personally collects US rent needs both. The LLC gets an EIN. The owner gets an ITIN for the personal Form 1040-NR. Get the EIN first, since it is faster and unblocks banking.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an <Accent>ITIN with Form W-7?</Accent>

Non-residents apply for an ITIN by submitting IRS Form W-7 with a federal tax return and proof of foreign identity. You mail the package to the IRS in Austin, Texas, or use a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA). The IRS charges $0 and issues the ITIN in 7 to 11 weeks.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm You Need an ITIN">Verify you have US-source income that requires a Form 1040-NR and that you cannot get an SSN. The ITIN is only for tax filing. If your need is a business tax ID, apply for an EIN instead with [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form W-7">Enter your full legal name, foreign address, and the reason you need an ITIN. Most non-residents select the reason for a non-resident filing a US tax return or claiming a treaty benefit. Enter your country of citizenship and your foreign tax ID if you have one.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Attach Your Tax Return">Attach Form 1040-NR to your W-7 unless you qualify for an exception. The IRS processes the ITIN and the return together. First-time applicants almost always file the return at the same time as the W-7.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Prove Your Identity">Provide a valid passport, which the IRS accepts as a single stand-alone document, or two other approved documents. Use a CAA to certify copies so you keep your original passport. Mailing your original passport is the alternative but carries shipping risk.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Submit and Wait">Mail the package to the IRS ITIN unit in Austin, or submit through your CAA. The IRS mails a CP 565 assignment letter in 7 to 11 weeks. Track the calendar; tax-season filings run longer.</Step>
</Steps>

## How Long Does an <Accent>ITIN Take and What Does It Cost?</Accent>

An ITIN takes 7 to 11 weeks from the date the IRS receives your complete Form W-7 package. The IRS charges $0 for the ITIN itself. There is no instant or fax option for an ITIN, which is the main reason it is slower than an EIN.

The 7-to-11-week range stretches during the January-to-April filing peak, when the IRS processes the highest volume of returns. The IRS mails a CP 565 assignment letter to the address on your W-7. Keep that letter; it is your proof of the ITIN.

## Do US LLC Owners Need an <Accent>ITIN or an EIN?</Accent>

US LLC owners need an EIN for the company and an ITIN only if they personally receive US-source income. The LLC files with its EIN. The individual files Form 1040-NR with an ITIN when personal US income exists. Owning an LLC alone does not require an ITIN.

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. These filings use the LLC's EIN, not the owner's ITIN. Most LLC owners get the EIN first because it is faster and unblocks banking.

<Card title="Step 3: File Annual Returns" index={3}>Submit [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) and the pro-forma Form 1120 each year, plus your [BOI report](/boi-filing/). The $25,000 Form 5472 penalty makes on-time filing essential. Confirm details with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Step 4: Add an ITIN If Needed" index={4}>Apply for an ITIN with Form W-7 only when you personally earn US-source income that requires Form 1040-NR. This is separate from the LLC's filings. Many owners never need an ITIN at all.</Card>

## What Causes <Accent>ITIN Rejections?</Accent>

ITIN rejections most often come from incomplete document certification, a missing tax return, an incorrect reason code on Form W-7, or expired identity documents. The IRS rejects the entire package when any required piece is wrong, which restarts the 7-to-11-week clock.

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these ITIN application mistakes">
<ol>
<li>Submitting Form W-7 without an attached Form 1040-NR when one is required.</li>
<li>Sending uncertified passport copies instead of using a CAA or originals.</li>
<li>Selecting the wrong reason-for-applying box on the W-7.</li>
<li>Using an expired passport or a document the IRS does not accept.</li>
<li>Entering a name on the W-7 that does not match the passport exactly.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>
