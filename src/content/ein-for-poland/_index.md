## How Do Polish Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Polish citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Polish passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Polish founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Poland.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Polish passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Polish address. No NIP, REGON, or Polish registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Polish address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Polish passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Poland-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Polish Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Polish address (ulica, kod pocztowy, miasto) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Polish passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Polish Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Polish Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Polish resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still carry US information-filing duties, and you continue to declare income in Poland.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-Poland Tax Matters" index={2}>The United States and Poland have an income tax treaty framework that addresses double taxation. Treaty treatment depends on your facts. Confirm withholding and relief with a US CPA and a Polish doradca podatkowy before you rely on any rate.</Card>
<Card title="Polish Reporting" index={3}>Polish residents generally must report foreign company interests and foreign accounts to KAS. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Polish doradca podatkowy.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>

## What Mistakes Do Polish Applicants Make on <Accent>Form SS-4?</Accent>

Polish applicants most often fail because of a malformed address, a blank Line 7b, or a wrong entity type. Each error causes a silent IRS rejection with no notification, so you wait weeks before realizing the application never processed.

<ol>
<li>Leaving Line 7b blank because you have no SSN — enter your Polish passport number instead.</li>
<li>Writing the kod pocztowy in the wrong field so the IRS reads the address as malformed.</li>
<li>Selecting the wrong entity type on Line 9a for a single-member LLC.</li>
<li>Using a Polish phone format the IRS fax cover sheet cannot parse.</li>
