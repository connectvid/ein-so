## Why Do Shopify Sellers Need <Accent>an EIN?</Accent>

Shopify reports seller income to the IRS once gross sales exceed $600 per year using Form 1099-K. Over 4 million Shopify stores operate worldwide, and every US-facing store needs a TIN for tax compliance. Shopify is a payment facilitator, which means it processes transactions on behalf of sellers.

### Shopify Tax Requirements by Seller Location

| Factor | US-Based Seller | Non-US Seller (with US LLC) |
|---|---|---|
| TIN Required | SSN or EIN | EIN (mandatory) |
| 1099-K Threshold | $600 in gross sales | $600 in gross sales |
| Shopify Payments Access | Available (lowest fees) | Requires US bank account + EIN |
| Sales Tax Collection | Required in nexus states | Required if economic nexus met ($100K/200 transactions) |
| Transaction Fee (Shopify Payments) | 2.4% + $0.30 (Advanced plan) | 2.4% + $0.30 (Advanced plan) |

Beyond tax reporting, Shopify Payments (the built-in payment processor) requires a TIN during activation. Without Shopify Payments, you are limited to third-party payment gateways that charge additional transaction fees. Activating Shopify Payments with your EIN gives you the lowest transaction rates and the smoothest checkout experience for your customers.

For **non-US sellers**, the EIN is your gateway to the US e-commerce market. You cannot activate Shopify Payments, handle US tax obligations, or open a US bank account for payouts without one. The IRS online EIN application requires an SSN, so non-US sellers must apply by fax using Form SS-4 -- or use a service like ein.so that handles the process for $49.

For **US sellers**, using an EIN instead of your SSN keeps your most sensitive personal identifier off Shopify's systems and the 1099-K forms sent to the IRS. This is a simple but effective identity protection measure. Learn more about [who needs an EIN](/who-needs-ein/).

## How Do You Set Up Shopify Payments <Accent>With Your EIN?</Accent>

Shopify Payments is the built-in payment processor that gives you the lowest transaction fees and the most seamless checkout experience. Setting it up with your EIN takes just a few minutes once you have your EIN confirmation letter from the IRS.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Access Payment Settings">In your Shopify admin dashboard, navigate to **Settings > Payments**. Click **"Activate Shopify Payments"** or **"Manage"** if already partially set up.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Enter Business Information">Enter your legal business name exactly as it appears on your EIN confirmation letter. Select your business type (sole proprietor, LLC, corporation). Enter your business address and phone number.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Provide Your EIN">When prompted for a Tax Identification Number, enter your 9-digit EIN in the format XX-XXXXXXX. Double-check every digit against your IRS confirmation letter. A single incorrect digit causes validation failure.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Connect Your Bank Account">Link a US bank account to receive Shopify payouts. The bank account should be in your business name (matching your EIN). Non-US sellers can use Mercury, Relay, or similar banks that accept non-resident LLC owners. See our [EIN for bank account guide](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Verify and Activate">Shopify validates your EIN with the IRS, which takes 24-48 hours. Once verified, Shopify Payments is fully active. If you have a new EIN, allow 2 weeks for IRS database propagation before activating.</Step>
</Steps>

## How Do You Configure Shopify Tax Settings <Accent>With Your EIN?</Accent>

45 US states plus DC impose sales tax, and Shopify sellers with economic nexus must collect in each. Beyond Shopify Payments, your EIN is used in Shopify's tax settings to handle sales tax collection and income tax reporting. Proper tax configuration prevents compliance issues and ensures smooth operations as your store grows.

### Shopify Tax Settings Configuration

In your Shopify admin, go to **Settings > Taxes and Duties**. Here you configure which regions you collect sales tax in, your tax registration numbers for each state, and your federal EIN. If you use Shopify Tax (the automated tax calculation feature), your EIN is used to register with state tax authorities.

**Sales tax nexus** is an important concept for Shopify sellers. If you have economic nexus in a state ($100,000 in sales or 200 transactions), you must collect and remit sales tax in that state. Shopify Tax automates this calculation, but you need to register with each state's tax authority using your EIN.

For non-US sellers, sales tax can be complex. Many non-US Shopify sellers use services like TaxJar or Avalara (both integrate with Shopify) to automate sales tax compliance. Your EIN is required to set up these services and to register with state tax authorities.

At year end, Shopify generates a 1099-K if your gross sales exceed $600. This form is sent to the IRS with your EIN. You use this information when filing your business tax return. If you operate through an LLC, see our [EIN for LLC guide](/ein-for-llc/) for more on tax filing requirements.
