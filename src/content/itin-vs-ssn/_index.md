<Label>Key Difference</Label>
## The Core <Accent>Difference</Accent>

An SSN is a multi-purpose identifier: tax filing, work authorization, Social Security benefits, credit history, identity verification. It is available only to US citizens and authorized residents.

An ITIN is a single-purpose identifier: federal tax filing only. It is available to anyone who needs a US tax ID but cannot get an SSN.

If you later become eligible for an SSN, the IRS rescinds your ITIN and transfers your tax records to your new SSN.
