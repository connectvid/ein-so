## Do Consultants and Coaches <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

Consultants and coaches need an EIN if they run an LLC, a corporation, or hire employees. Sole proprietors can use an SSN, but an EIN keeps it off client W-9s and separates business from personal finances. Non-residents with a US LLC always need one.

The decision depends on your structure and whether you bill US clients. The table below maps common consulting setups to the EIN requirement.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Consulting or coaching LLC | Yes (required) |
| Sole proprietor consultant | Recommended |
| Consulting practice with employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US consultant with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Hobby or one-off side income | No |

### Why Consultants and Coaches Get an EIN

1. **W-9 compliance** — Every US client paying you over $600 a year requests Form W-9 with a tax ID
2. **SSN privacy** — An EIN keeps your Social Security Number off many client systems
3. **Business banking** — Open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) under the practice name
4. **Tax separation** — Report business income apart from personal income
5. **Hiring** — Required for payroll and contractor reporting as you scale

## How Do Consultants Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Consultants apply for a US EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN use the IRS online tool for an instant EIN. Non-residents fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b, and the IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Choose Your Structure">US consultants can stay sole proprietors or form an LLC. Non-residents register a US LLC in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives the EIN a legal entity and a US business address.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your name as the responsible party and a tax ID for Line 7b. US residents use an SSN. Non-residents use a passport number. Have your LLC name, state, and formation date ready.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your consulting business name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name on Line 7a, and your SSN or passport number on Line 7b. List "consulting" or "coaching" on Line 16. ein.so completes this to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">US residents get an instant EIN online. Non-residents fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. ein.so delivers your EIN by email in 4-7 business days (Standard) or 2-3 business days (Express). The IRS returns the CP 575 assignment letter.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Consultants

| SS-4 Field | Consultant Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your consulting LLC or your full name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your business or home address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | SSN (US) or passport number (non-resident) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Consulting," "coaching," or "professional services" |

## What Should Consultants Know About <Accent>EIN Taxes and Compliance?</Accent>

A consultant's EIN does not change the tax owed; it identifies the business to the IRS. A single-member US LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. Foreign-owned single-member LLCs still file information returns each year. Confirm your specifics with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US consulting LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="W-9 and 1099 Reporting" index={2}>US clients collect your EIN on Form W-9 and may issue a 1099 for payments over $600 a year. Using an EIN instead of an SSN keeps your personal number off client records.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={3}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand who must file and when. Filing is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Home-Country Reporting" index={4}>Non-resident consultants often must declare a foreign-held US LLC in their home country. Tax rules vary by country. Confirm your reporting duties with a local accountant and a US CPA.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## Do Consultants Need an EIN or an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

Consultants need an EIN for the business and may also need an ITIN for personal US tax filing. The EIN identifies your LLC or practice. An ITIN is a personal taxpayer number for individuals without an SSN. They serve different purposes and are not interchangeable.

| Number | Who It Identifies | Used For | ein.so Price |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| EIN | The business entity | W-9s, banking, business returns | $49 / $97 Express |
| ITIN | The individual | Personal US tax filing | $197 / $297 Express |

Most non-resident consultants start with an EIN for the LLC. You add an ITIN later if you must file a personal US return. The two numbers can coexist. Confirm whether you need an ITIN with a CPA, since it depends on your income type and treaty position.
