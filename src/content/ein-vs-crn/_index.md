## What Is an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is a 9-digit federal tax ID issued by the IRS to businesses operating in the United States. The format is XX-XXXXXXX, for example 12-3456789. The IRS assigns the number permanently once it issues it.

Every US business entity uses the EIN for federal tax filing, opening a US bank account, hiring staff, and accepting payments. The EIN costs $0 from the IRS. ein.so charges $49 to file [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) for non-residents who cannot use the IRS online tool. For a full explanation, read [what is an EIN](/what-is-ein/).

## What Is a <Accent>CRN?</Accent>

A CRN (Company Registration Number) is an 8-character identifier issued by Companies House when a company incorporates in the United Kingdom. England and Wales companies use 8 digits, such as 12345678. Scotland companies use SC plus 6 digits. Northern Ireland companies use NI plus 6 digits.

The CRN is a company registration identifier, not a tax number. Companies House assigns it at incorporation and never changes it. The number appears on the public register, on Companies House filings, and on official company documents. It identifies the company entity for corporate filings such as annual confirmation statements and accounts.

UK companies also hold a UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference) from HMRC for tax. The CRN and the UTR split two jobs: the CRN registers the company, the UTR handles tax. In the US, the EIN performs both jobs in a single number.

<InfoBox title="Key CRN Facts">
<ul>
<li>**Issued by:** Companies House</li>
<li>**Format:** 8 digits, or 2 letters + 6 digits (12345678, SC123456, NI123456)</li>
<li>**Country:** United Kingdom only</li>
<li>**Purpose:** company registration and corporate identification</li>
<li>**Tax counterpart:** the HMRC UTR handles UK tax, not the CRN</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

## EIN vs CRN: <Accent>Side-by-Side</Accent>

An EIN identifies your business to the US government for tax. A CRN identifies your company to the UK government for the register. The table below sets out every practical difference between the two identifiers.

Both numbers are permanent. Both identify a legal entity. Both unlock a domestic bank account and payment onboarding in their own country. The overlap stops there. The EIN reports to the IRS. The CRN sits on the Companies House register. One does not substitute for the other in any scenario.

## How Do UK Entrepreneurs <Accent>Get a US EIN?</Accent>

UK entrepreneurs get a US EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 and entering their UK passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so UK applicants use the fax method. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register an LLC or corporation with a US state. Many UK founders choose Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. You complete this online from the UK.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the US entity name on Line 1, your UK address on Line 4, your full name on Line 7a, and your UK passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is needed.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Submit by Fax to the IRS">Fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. ein.so prepares and faxes it for you to avoid the common errors that trigger silent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Receive Your EIN">The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575). ein.so delivers your EIN by email in 4-7 business days (Standard, $49) or 2-3 business days (Express, $97).</Step>
</Steps>

Your CRN plays no part in this application. The IRS does not ask for or verify a UK CRN, UTR, or VAT number. Your passport is sufficient identification on Line 7b.

## Do You Need <Accent>Both Numbers?</Accent>

You need an EIN if you run a US entity, and you need a CRN if you run a UK company. You need both if you operate in both countries. The lists below show which identifier each situation requires.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="You Need an EIN" index={1}>You formed a US LLC or corporation. You sell on Amazon.com or a US Shopify store. You need a US [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/). You hire US contractors. You file US tax returns.</Card>
<Card title="You Need a CRN" index={2}>You incorporated a limited company at Companies House. You file UK confirmation statements and accounts. You operate and employ in the United Kingdom. You hold a UK business bank account.</Card>
<Card title="You Need Both" index={3}>You run companies in both the US and the UK. You are a UK resident with a US LLC. You earn income in both jurisdictions. Each identifier serves its own country independently.</Card>
<Card title="Order of Acquisition" index={4}>You get the CRN when you incorporate the UK company. You get the EIN after you form the US entity and file Form SS-4. Most UK founders already hold a CRN and add the EIN later.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The two numbers coexist without conflict. A UK resident keeps the CRN for the UK company and uses the EIN for the US LLC. Neither filing affects the other.

## How Does the EIN Affect <Accent>US Banking and Tax?</Accent>

The EIN unlocks a US business bank account and US payment processors, which a CRN cannot do in the United States. Mercury, Relay, and Wise approve most non-resident LLC owners who hold an EIN and a passport. The EIN also drives US federal tax compliance.

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.
