## How Do Non-Residents Apply for a <Accent>US EIN for Payoneer?</Accent>

Non-residents submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number in place of an SSN on Line 7b. The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for non-residents. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days and charges $0.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register a US LLC through a registered agent if you do not already have one. This gives you a US business entity and address to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from your home country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC formation details (legal name, state, formation date), and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or local business registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Payoneer SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "consulting," "e-commerce," "software" |

The most common rejection cause is a malformed international address that does not map to the SS-4 layout. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Should Payoneer Users Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, and the penalty for not filing is $25,000. Receiving payments through Payoneer does not remove this filing duty. The EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance, not the end of it.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties.</Card>
<Card title="Income Sourcing" index={3}>Whether your Payoneer income is US-sourced affects your US tax position. Sourcing rules are complex for non-residents. Confirm your situation with a CPA before assuming any income is exempt.</Card>
<Card title="Home-Country Reporting" index={4}>You usually still declare US LLC income under your home country's rules. A US EIN does not change your local tax obligations. Confirm with a local tax professional.</Card>
