## How Do Peruvian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Peruvian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Peru passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Peruvian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Peru.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Peru passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Peru address. No RUC, DNI, or SUNAT registration is required for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Peru address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Peru passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Peru-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Peruvian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Peru address (calle, distrito, código postal) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Peru passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |

## What Should Peruvian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Peru Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Peru resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. The LLC files an annual information return, not necessarily a US tax bill. You still declare income to SUNAT in Peru under Peruvian rules.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return that reports transactions between you and your LLC.</Card>
<Card title="Peru Reporting to SUNAT" index={2}>Peru residents declare worldwide income to SUNAT under their RUC. A foreign-held US LLC does not remove your Peru reporting duties. Confirm your obligations with a Peruvian contador before you file.</Card>
<Card title="US-Peru Tax Position" index={3}>The United States and Peru do not have a comprehensive income tax treaty as of 2026. Withholding and double-taxation relief depend on your specific facts. Confirm your position with a CPA experienced in non-resident matters.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand your deadline and avoid penalties. The BOI report is free to file.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
