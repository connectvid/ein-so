## Is a Pennsylvania EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Pennsylvania EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a federal number issued only by the IRS. It is the same 9-digit number in Pennsylvania and every other state. Pennsylvania does not issue its own EIN.

Two different identifiers cause confusion. The federal EIN comes from the IRS and is used for federal tax filings, banking, and payroll. The Pennsylvania state tax account comes from the Department of Revenue and is used for state-level obligations like sales tax and employer withholding. You may need both, and the EIN does not replace state registration.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form 5472 |
| PA state tax account | PA Department of Revenue | PA sales tax, employer withholding |
| PA entity number | PA Department of State | LLC formation records |

The federal EIN process never changes by state. The SS-4 you file for a Pennsylvania LLC is identical to one for a Wyoming or Delaware LLC. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how the state-level account fits in.

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Pennsylvania?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Pennsylvania through the IRS by one of four methods: online, fax, phone, or mail. The fastest is online (instant) but it requires an SSN. Non-residents without an SSN use fax to 855-215-1627, which the IRS processes in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Online (irs.gov) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| Fax (855-215-1627) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | No |
| Phone (800-829-4933) | Same day | $0 | Restricted |
| Mail | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The online tool is the simplest route for US residents with an SSN. Non-residents are blocked from it and rely on the fax method. The phone line works for international applicants in limited cases but requires calling during US business hours. Mail is the slowest at 4-6 weeks. ## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an EIN for a <Accent>Pennsylvania LLC?</Accent>

Non-residents submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is needed. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the working path. Correctly filed EINs arrive in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Pennsylvania LLC">Register your LLC with the Pennsylvania Department of State, naming a registered office in Pennsylvania. Non-residents typically use a commercial registered office provider. This creates the legal entity the EIN attaches to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to apply for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Mark "Started new business" on Line 10. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Pennsylvania SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Pennsylvania LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is a malformed international address that the IRS silently rejects. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/).

After getting your EIN, register with Pennsylvania for state taxes, file your federal information returns, and report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000.
