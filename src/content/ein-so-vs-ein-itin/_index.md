## What Is the Difference Between an EIN and an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

An EIN is a business tax ID for an entity. An ITIN is a personal tax ID for an individual. Most non-resident LLC owners need only an EIN to open a bank account and file Form 5472. ein.so files EINs at $49 and ITINs at $197 Standard or $297 Express.

The two IDs serve different purposes, and many applicants confuse them. You attach an EIN to your US LLC for banking, payments, and federal filings. You need an ITIN only when you personally owe US tax filing duties and cannot get an SSN.

| Feature | EIN | ITIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Holder | Business entity | Individual person |
| Format | XX-XXXXXXX (9 digits) | 9XX-XX-XXXX (9 digits) |
| IRS form | Form SS-4 | Form W-7 |
| ein.so price | $49 / $97 | $197 / $297 |
| Primary use | Banking, Form 5472, payments | Personal US tax filing |
| Needed for most LLC owners | Yes | Often no |

Most non-resident entrepreneurs forming a US LLC need the EIN, not the ITIN. Confirm whether your situation requires an ITIN with a CPA, since personal tax obligations vary. ein.so does not provide tax advice. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) for the banking use case.
