## Do Forex and Stock Traders <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

Forex and stock traders need an EIN whenever they trade through an LLC, a corporation, or a partnership, or whenever they hire staff. A US LLC cannot open a business bank account or an entity brokerage account without one. Sole-proprietor traders may use an SSN, but an EIN keeps that SSN off broker paperwork.

| Trader Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Forex or stock trading LLC | Yes (required) |
| Multi-member trading partnership | Yes (required) |
| Trading firm with employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US trader with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Sole-proprietor trader (individual) | Recommended |
| Hobby trading, no entity | Not required |

### Why Forex and Stock Traders Get an EIN

1. **Separate trading from personal taxes** — The EIN gives the LLC its own tax identity.
2. **Business banking** — Open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) in the LLC's name.
3. **Entity brokerage accounts** — Brokers that accept LLC accounts require an EIN.
4. **Privacy** — Use the EIN, not your SSN, on W-9s and broker forms.
5. **Liability protection** — The LLC shields personal assets from business liabilities.

A non-resident trader who forms a US LLC gets an EIN with no SSN required. See [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the full eligibility rules.

## How Do Traders Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Traders apply by submitting IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN use the IRS online tool for an instant EIN. Non-resident traders fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Trading LLC">Register a US LLC through a registered agent in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico. This creates the legal entity that will hold your trading accounts. You can form the LLC online from anywhere.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name, formation state, formation date, and your home address. No SSN and no ITIN are required to apply for the EIN as a non-resident.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. List "securities trading" on Line 16. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by fax, then ein.so emails it to you.</Step>
</Steps>

### Trader SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Trader Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your trading LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home or business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Securities trading" or "investing" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting that does not map to the SS-4 layout. The IRS rejects malformed international addresses without explanation. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style.
