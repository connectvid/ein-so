## How Do Russian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Russian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Russian passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Russian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Russian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Russia.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Russian passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Russian address. No INN, OGRN, or Russian registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Russian address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Russian passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### Russia-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Russian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Russian address (region, city, street, postal index) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Russian passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Russian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Compliance?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, and the penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return that reports transactions between the LLC and its Russian owner. It is not necessarily a tax bill.
