## How Do Trinidadian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Trinidadian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Trinidad and Tobago passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Trinidadian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Trinidad and Tobago.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Trinidad and Tobago passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home address. No BIR File Number or local company registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Trinidad and Tobago address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Trinidad and Tobago-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Trinidadian Entry |
| --- | --- |

## What Should Trinidadian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Filing?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Trinidad and Tobago resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The LLC still has annual US information-filing duties that carry steep penalties.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand your deadline and avoid penalties. The report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Trinidad and Tobago Reporting" index={3}>Trinidad and Tobago residents declare worldwide income to the Board of Inland Revenue under local rules. Confirm how a foreign-held US LLC affects your BIR filing with a local accountant.</Card>
<Card title="Confirm Specifics With a CPA" index={4}>Tax outcomes depend on your activity, residency, and whether income is US-sourced. Confirm your exact obligations with a CPA experienced in non-resident US matters before you rely on any treatment.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
