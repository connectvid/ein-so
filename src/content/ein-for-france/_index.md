## How Do French Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

French citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a French passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for French applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most French founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from France.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your French passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your French address. No SIREN, Codice, or French registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your French address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your French passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

| SS-4 Field | French Entry |

## What Should French Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and French Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a French resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The US-France income tax treaty helps prevent double taxation and can reduce US withholding on certain passive income.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-France Tax Treaty" index={2}>The treaty allocates taxing rights and provides relief from double taxation. It can lower US withholding on dividends, interest, and royalties. It does not remove your obligation to declare income in France.</Card>
<Card title="French Reporting" index={3}>French residents generally must declare foreign company interests and foreign accounts to the DGFiP. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a French expert-comptable.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and [deadlines](/boi-filing-deadline/) to avoid penalties.</Card>
