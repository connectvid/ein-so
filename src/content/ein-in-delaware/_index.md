## Is a Delaware EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Delaware EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a single federal number issued by the IRS, and it is identical whether your LLC is in Delaware, Wyoming, or any other state. Delaware does not issue its own version of an EIN.

Your federal EIN and any Delaware state tax registration are two separate things. The EIN covers federal IRS reporting. State registration, if required for your business activities, comes from the Delaware Division of Revenue.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payment processors |
| Delaware state tax registration | Delaware Division of Revenue | State-level taxes (if applicable) |
| Delaware LLC entity number | Division of Corporations | State entity recordkeeping |

You may need both the federal EIN and state registration. Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for details, and confirm your specific Delaware obligations with a CPA.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Delaware?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Delaware through the IRS, and the method depends on whether you have an SSN. US residents with an SSN apply online for free instant approval. Non-residents file Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | Free | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | Free / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | Free | Yes |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | Free | No |

The fax method is the practical path for non-residents because the IRS online tool requires an SSN. The phone line (800-829-4933) is restricted to applicants with a US SSN or ITIN. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for full detail.

## How Does a Non-Resident Apply for a <Accent>Delaware EIN?</Accent>

A non-resident applies by forming a Delaware LLC, completing Form SS-4 with a passport number on Line 7b, and faxing it to the IRS at 855-215-1627. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days. No SSN or ITIN is needed.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Delaware LLC">Register your LLC with the Delaware Division of Corporations through a registered agent. This gives you a US legal entity and a registered agent address. You complete the entire formation online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name and Delaware formation date, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or foreign business registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Delaware LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Select "Started new business" on Line 10. ein.so completes this to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by fax or mail.</Step>
</Steps>

### Delaware SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Delaware LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Must a Foreign-Owned Delaware LLC <Accent>File Each Year?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Delaware LLC must file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for failing to file is $25,000. These are information returns, not necessarily a tax bill. A single-member LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax.
