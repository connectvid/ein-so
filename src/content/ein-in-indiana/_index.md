## Is an Indiana EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. An Indiana EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the IRS is the only authority that issues EINs. The number works in all 50 states. Indiana does not issue its own EIN. You apply once and use the same 9-digit number nationwide.

Indiana businesses confuse two separate numbers. The federal EIN comes from the IRS for federal taxes. The Indiana state tax ID comes from the Indiana Department of Revenue for state taxes. You may need both.

| Number | Issuer | Purpose | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 | $0 (IRS) |
| Indiana state tax ID | Department of Revenue | State income tax, sales tax | Registration via INBiz |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. Register with the Indiana Department of Revenue when you have employees, collect Indiana sales tax, or owe state withholding. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction. The federal [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) is always $0 from the IRS.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Indiana?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Indiana through the IRS, not the state. Four methods exist. Residents with an SSN apply online for an instant number. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Mail and phone are also available. The process is identical in every state.

| Method | Speed | SSN Required | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | Yes | $0 |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | No | $0 / $49 |
| Phone (800-829-4933) | Same day | No | $0 |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | No | $0 |

The online tool is the fastest path, but it requires an SSN or ITIN. A non-US resident without an SSN cannot use it. That applicant uses the fax method with a passport number instead. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full walkthrough.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for an <Accent>Indiana Business?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for an Indiana LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The IRS issues most correct filings in 4-7 business days.

Indiana does not require US residency or citizenship to own an LLC. A foreign founder registers an Indiana LLC through a registered agent, then applies for the federal EIN. Many non-residents instead choose Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico for lower fees and simpler annual compliance.

## How Do You Complete Form SS-4 for an <Accent>Indiana EIN?</Accent>

You complete Form SS-4 with your LLC name, your address, your name as responsible party, and your passport number on Line 7b. Non-residents leave SSN blank and enter the passport number. Then you fax the form to 855-215-1627. ein.so prepares the form to prevent rejection.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Indiana LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home country or US address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC or your entity type |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting that does not map to the SS-4 fields. The IRS rejects malformed entries without explanation. ein.so reformats international addresses to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Taxes Apply to an Indiana LLC With an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An Indiana LLC faces federal taxes filed under the EIN plus Indiana's 3.05% flat state income tax. A single-member US LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. Indiana also has county income taxes that vary by county. Confirm exact obligations with a CPA.

<Card title="Indiana State Income Tax" index={2}>Indiana applies a 3.05% flat state income tax. This is separate from federal tax. Register with the Department of Revenue through INBiz. County income taxes may also apply.</Card>
