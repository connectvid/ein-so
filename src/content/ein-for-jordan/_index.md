## How Do Jordanian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Jordanian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Jordan passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Jordanian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Jordan.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Jordan passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Jordan address. No Tax Number or local Jordan registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Jordan address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Jordan passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Jordanian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Jordanian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The owner still files annual information returns with the IRS, and penalties for missing them are steep.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is filed free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
