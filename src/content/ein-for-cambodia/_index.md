## How Do Cambodian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Cambodian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Cambodia passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Cambodian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Cambodian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Cambodia.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Cambodia passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Cambodia address. No Cambodia TIN or local registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Cambodia address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Cambodia passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at [855-215-1627](/irs-fax-number-for-ein/). Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Cambodia-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Cambodian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Cambodia address (street, sangkat, khan, province) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Cambodia passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

## What Should Cambodian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes and Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Cambodian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The owner still files annual information returns to stay compliant.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties. The report is free to file.</Card>
<Card title="Cambodia Reporting" index={3}>Cambodian residents may have local reporting duties for foreign company interests and foreign income. Confirm your obligations with a Cambodian tax adviser or GDT.</Card>
