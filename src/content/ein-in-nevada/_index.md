## Is a Nevada EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Nevada EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a federal number issued by the IRS, identical in all 50 states. A Nevada LLC and a Delaware LLC apply through the same Form SS-4 and receive the same 9-digit format (XX-XXXXXXX).

Founders often confuse the federal EIN with Nevada's state-level business registration. They are two different identifiers from two different authorities. You may need both, depending on what your business does inside Nevada.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, payroll, hiring |
| Nevada state registration | Nevada Department of Taxation | Nevada state business obligations |
| Nevada entity record | Nevada Secretary of State | Legal existence of the LLC or corporation |

Your EIN does not replace state registration. State registration does not replace your EIN. Form the entity with the [Secretary of State](/ein-by-state/) first, then apply for the federal EIN. Confirm your exact Nevada state registration duties with a Nevada CPA, since they depend on your activity, employees, and sales.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Nevada LLC?</Accent>

A non-US resident gets an EIN for a Nevada LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS online tool is blocked without an SSN, so non-residents fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 instead.

Nevada is one of the most popular states for non-resident LLCs because of its zero state income tax and privacy protections. The EIN process for these owners is identical to the process for US citizens, except for the identification line and the filing method.

<Card title="No SSN Required" index={1}>You do not need a Social Security Number. The SSN requirement applies only to the IRS online tool. Your passport number serves as your identification on Line 7b. See the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/).</Card>
<Card title="No ITIN Required" index={2}>You do not need an ITIN to get an EIN. The two numbers are separate. If you later need an ITIN for personal US tax filing, ein.so offers an ITIN service at $197 Standard or $297 Express.</Card>
<Card title="Passport on Line 7b" index={3}>Enter your full legal name as the responsible party on Line 7a and your passport number on Line 7b. The IRS accepts a foreign passport number in place of a US tax ID. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Card>
<Card title="Filed by Fax" index={4}>The completed Form SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. The IRS issues the EIN within 4-7 business days for an error-free form. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).</Card>

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Nevada?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Nevada through one of four IRS methods, and all four issue the identical federal number. The online tool is instant but requires an SSN. The fax method to 855-215-1627 takes 4-7 business days and works for non-residents.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 (DIY) / $49 (ein.so) | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day for some callers | $0 | International call |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The process does not change by state. A Nevada applicant uses the same form, the same fax number, and the same IRS office as an applicant in any other state. See the full [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) walkthrough.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Nevada Entity">Register your LLC or corporation with the Nevada Secretary of State through a registered agent. This creates the legal entity and gives you a US business name to attach the EIN to. Confirm current state filing and business license fees on the Secretary of State site.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your Nevada entity name, your formation date, and your business address. No SSN, ITIN, or prior US filing is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Nevada entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive the EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address or responsible-party formatting. A single malformed line restarts the wait. [Apply through ein.so](/apply/) to have the form prepared and faxed for you.

## What Are the Federal Filing Duties for a <Accent>Nevada LLC?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Nevada LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. This is a federal information return, not necessarily a tax bill, and it applies regardless of Nevada's zero state income tax.

Nevada's lack of state income tax does not remove federal obligations. The IRS treats a Nevada LLC like an LLC in any other state for these information returns.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>Foreign-owned single-member LLCs file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. File on time even if the LLC owes no tax.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) for your deadline and the information FinCEN requires.</Card>
<Card title="Nevada State Registration" index={3}>Register with the Nevada Department of Taxation if your activity requires it, and keep your Secretary of State entity in good standing. Confirm your exact duties with a Nevada CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Annual Maintenance" index={4}>Keep your registered agent active and file any required annual reports with the state. Lapsing these can void the LLC, which strands the EIN attached to it.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters to confirm your Form 5472, Form 1120, and Nevada state positions. The EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance, not the end of it.

## How Does Nevada Compare to <Accent>Other LLC States?</Accent>

Nevada competes with Wyoming, Delaware, and New Mexico as a home state for non-resident LLCs. The EIN process is federal and identical across all of them. The differences lie in state fees, taxes, and privacy, not in the EIN.

| State | State income tax | Common appeal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nevada | None | No state income tax, privacy |
| [Wyoming](/ein-in-wyoming/) | None | Low fees, privacy |
| [Delaware](/ein-in-delaware/) | None on out-of-state income | Corporate law, investor familiarity |
| [Washington](/ein-in-washington/) | None | West Coast presence |

Whichever state you choose, the EIN comes from the same IRS process. Form the entity with that state's Secretary of State, then file Form SS-4 by fax. See the full [EIN by State](/ein-by-state/) directory and the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) for non-resident specifics.
