## How Much Does the IRS Charge for an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

The IRS charges $0 for an EIN through every application method. There is no filing fee, processing fee, or renewal fee. The EIN is free whether you apply online, by fax, by phone, or by mail.

The IRS has never charged for an EIN. The number is free regardless of your entity type, your location, or how many EINs you hold. Here is every method and its true cost:

| Method | IRS Cost | Processing Time | Requirements |
|--------|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Online (EIN Assistant) | $0 | Instant | SSN or ITIN required |
| Fax (Form SS-4) | $0 | 4-7 business days | Passport number only |
| Phone (267-941-1099) | $0 | Same day | International call charges |
| Mail (Form SS-4) | $0 | 4-6 weeks | None |

Non-residents face one barrier: the online EIN Assistant blocks anyone without an SSN. That removes the instant, free path. The fax method to 855-215-1627 remains free, but it requires an error-free Form SS-4 and a working US fax line. ## How Does EIN Cost Compare to <Accent>ITIN Cost?</Accent>

An EIN and an ITIN are different numbers with different costs. The IRS issues an EIN for $0 and ein.so files it for $49. An ITIN requires Form W-7 and supporting documents; ein.so handles ITIN applications for $197 Standard or $297 Express.

A business needs an EIN. An individual non-resident sometimes needs an ITIN for personal US tax obligations. The two are not interchangeable, and confusing them leads to paying for the wrong service.

| Item | EIN | ITIN |
|------|-----|------|
| Purpose | Business tax ID | Individual tax ID |
| IRS form | Form SS-4 | Form W-7 |
| IRS fee | $0 | $0 |
| ein.so Standard | $49 | $197 |
| ein.so Express | $97 | $297 |
| Needs passport | Yes (Line 7b) | Yes, plus W-7 documents |
| Used for | Bank account, Stripe, taxes | Personal US tax filing |

For a US LLC, the EIN is the number you need. The ITIN matters only for specific personal filing situations. Confirm which number applies to your situation with a CPA. ein.so does not provide tax advice. Read more on [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).

## What Other Costs Surround a US <Accent>LLC and EIN?</Accent>

The EIN itself costs $0 from the IRS and $49 through ein.so. Separate costs surround the wider US business setup: state LLC formation fees, annual report fees, and federal compliance filings. These are not EIN costs, but founders often confuse them.

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 every year. The penalty for failing to file is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill, but the deadline is firm.
