<Capsule>**Search for an EIN by company name using the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search (nonprofits, free), SEC EDGAR (public companies, free), or state Secretary of State databases.** Private company EINs are not publicly searchable. The standard method is to request IRS Form W-9 directly from the business.</Capsule>
</Section>

| Method | Works For | Cost |
|--------|-----------|------|

| SEC EDGAR | Public companies | Free |
| State SOS | Registered businesses | Free |

| Dun & Bradstreet | Any (limited) | Paid |

### How to Use IRS Tax Exempt Search
1. Go to apps.irs.gov/app/eos
2. Enter organization name
3. Filter by city/state
4. Results show EIN, name, status

<Label>Private Companies</Label>
## Finding Private <Accent>Company EINs</Accent>

Private company EINs are confidential. Your options:

1. **Request W-9** — Standard business practice. Companies must provide W-9 for $600+ transactions
2. **State databases** — Many states list registered businesses with EINs
3. **Google search** — Some orgs publish EINs in public documents
4. **Commercial services** — D&B, LexisNexis
</Section>
