## Is an EIN in Alaska Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. An EIN in Alaska is the exact same federal number the IRS issues everywhere. The EIN is national, formatted XX-XXXXXXX, and tied to your business entity, not to Alaska. Alaska does not assign its own version of an EIN.

The EIN, or Employer Identification Number, is a 9-digit tax ID from the Internal Revenue Service. The IRS issues it under Form SS-4. The process does not change from state to state. An EIN you obtain for an Anchorage business is identical to one you obtain for a New York or Wyoming business.

Alaska business obligations run separately from your federal EIN. Your EIN handles IRS reporting. State and local registration handles Alaska duties. These are two different tracks.

<InfoBox title="Federal EIN vs Alaska state registration">
<ul>
<li>**Federal EIN** — issued by the IRS under Form SS-4, used for federal taxes, banking, and payroll.</li>
<li>**Alaska state registration** — handled through the state, separate from the EIN, depending on your activity and borough.</li>
<li>The EIN does not replace state or local registration. You may need both.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for an <Accent>Alaska Business?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 by entering your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN, ITIN, or passport number on Line 7b. Non-residents use a passport number. The reason goes on Line 10.

Form SS-4 is the single IRS form that creates an EIN. The fields are the same for an Alaska business as for any other state. The most common rejection cause for non-residents is a malformed foreign address that the IRS silently rejects.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your address (US or foreign) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Passport number for non-residents |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC or relevant entity type |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "fishing" |

<Alert type="warning" title="Common SS-4 rejection causes">
<ol>
<li>Malformed foreign address that the IRS cannot read.</li>
<li>Responsible party name not matching the passport.</li>
<li>Entity type and reason fields left blank or inconsistent.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>
