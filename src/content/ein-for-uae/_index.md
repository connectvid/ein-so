## How Do UAE Residents Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

UAE residents submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most UAE founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This is separate from your UAE free-zone or mainland company and gives the EIN a US entity to attach to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your UAE address. No Emirates ID, trade licence, or TRN is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your UAE address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### UAE-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | UAE Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your UAE address (villa/office, area, emirate, P.O. Box) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "trading," "software" |

UAE addresses often use P.O. Box numbers and area names rather than street numbers, which the SS-4 does not anticipate. Malformed international addresses are silently rejected by the IRS. ein.so reformats your UAE address to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Should UAE Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

There is no comprehensive US-UAE income tax treaty, so UAE owners of US LLCs cannot rely on treaty relief to reduce US withholding. This makes correct entity structuring and accurate annual reporting more important than for treaty countries.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="No US-UAE Treaty" index={1}>Without a treaty, US-sourced income paid to a foreign owner may face standard US withholding. Plan your structure and contracts with a US CPA before assuming income is tax-free in the US.</Card>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={2}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return.</Card>
<Card title="UAE Corporate Tax" index={3}>The UAE introduced a 9% federal corporate tax on business profits above AED 375,000. How your US LLC income interacts with UAE corporate tax depends on your structure; confirm with a UAE tax adviser.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and [deadlines](/boi-filing-deadline/) to avoid penalties.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
