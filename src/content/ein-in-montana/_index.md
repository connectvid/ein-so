## Is a Montana EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Montana EIN is a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit number (XX-XXXXXXX) that works in all 50 states. Montana does not issue its own EIN. The state does issue a separate Montana state tax registration through the Department of Revenue.

Your federal EIN and your Montana state tax ID serve different purposes. Mixing them up causes filing errors.

| Identifier | Issuer | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, payment processors |
| Montana state tax ID | Montana Department of Revenue | State withholding, state-level filings |

The EIN does not replace state registration. The state tax ID does not replace the EIN. A business with Montana employees or in-state activity may need both. Confirm your specific obligations with a CPA. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction and [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for a full price breakdown.

## How Do Non-Residents Complete <Accent>Form SS-4?</Accent>

Non-residents complete Form SS-4 by entering the US LLC name on Line 1, the foreign address on Line 4, the responsible party name on Line 7a, and the passport number on Line 7b. No SSN goes on the form. Address formatting errors are the top cause of IRS rejection.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Montana LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your foreign passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

The IRS rejects malformed international addresses without explanation. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style so your application is not silently dropped. Read the complete [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for line-by-line detail and the [EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/).
