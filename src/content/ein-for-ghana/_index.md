## How Do Ghanaian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Ghanaian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Ghana passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Ghanaian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Ghanaian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Ghana.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Ghana passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Ghana address. No Ghana TIN or local registration is required for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Ghana address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Ghana passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### Ghana-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Ghanaian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Ghana address (street, city, region) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Ghana passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |
| Line 18 (Prior EIN) | No |

## What Should Ghanaian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Ghana resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The owner still files an annual information return: Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your filing obligation and deadline and avoid penalties.</Card>
<Card title="US-Ghana Tax Position" index={3}>Confirm your US withholding and any treaty position with a US CPA. Do not assume a treaty rate applies. ein.so does not give tax advice, so verify the exact figures with a qualified professional.</Card>
