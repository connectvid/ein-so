## How Do Romanian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Romanian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Romanian passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Romanian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Romania.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Romanian passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Romanian address. No CUI, CIF, or ANAF registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Romanian address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Romanian passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Romanian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Romanian Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Romanian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still file an annual US information return, and you still declare income in Romania.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Romanian Reporting (ANAF)" index={2}>Romanian residents declare foreign company interests and foreign accounts to ANAF. Confirm your exact reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Romanian accountant before you file.</Card>
<Card title="US-Romania Tax Treaty" index={3}>A US-Romania income tax treaty exists to reduce double taxation. Treaty rates and eligibility depend on your facts. Confirm any treaty benefit with a CPA before claiming it on a US return.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is filed free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>

## Do Romanian Entrepreneurs Need an ITIN <Accent>As Well As an EIN?</Accent>

Most Romanian entrepreneurs need only an EIN, not an ITIN. The EIN identifies your US LLC. An ITIN identifies you as an individual for personal US tax filing, which applies only in specific situations.

You need an EIN to form a US business, open US accounts, and file Form 5472. You may need an ITIN if you personally must file a US individual tax return, claim a treaty benefit on personal income, or appear on a US tax document that requires an individual tax number. The two numbers serve different purposes.

| Number | Identifies | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EIN | Your US LLC | Banking, payments, Form 5472, Amazon |
| ITIN | You as an individual | Personal US tax returns, treaty claims |

ein.so files both. The EIN service costs $49 Standard or $97 Express. The ITIN service costs $197 Standard or $297 Express. Start with the EIN, since the LLC needs it first. Add the ITIN only when a US individual filing requirement applies. Confirm whether you need an ITIN with a CPA before ordering one.
