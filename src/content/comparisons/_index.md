## What Is the Difference Between an EIN, an ITIN, and an <Accent>SSN?</Accent>

An EIN identifies a business, while an ITIN and an SSN identify individuals. The IRS issues an EIN to a company. The IRS issues an ITIN to a person who needs a tax ID but has no SSN. The Social Security Administration issues an SSN to a US person.

Non-residents mix these up because the words "tax ID" cover all three. The distinction matters for your application. You attach an EIN to your US LLC for banking and tax filing. You attach an ITIN to yourself for personal filings, such as claiming a treaty benefit. Many non-residents need both numbers for different reasons.

| Feature | EIN | ITIN | SSN |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies | A business | An individual | A US individual |
| Issued by | IRS | IRS | Social Security Admin |
| Format | XX-XXXXXXX | 9XX-XX-XXXX | XXX-XX-XXXX |
| Needs SSN to apply? | No | No | N/A |
| ein.so price | $49 / $97 | $197 / $297 | Not offered |

The EIN is the number a US LLC needs first. A foreign owner applies for the EIN with a passport, not an SSN. Read the full [EIN vs ITIN](/ein-vs-itin/) and [EIN vs SSN](/ein-vs-ssn/) breakdowns, then start your [EIN application](/apply/).

## How Does an EIN Relate to TIN and Federal <Accent>Tax ID?</Accent>

TIN is the umbrella term, and an EIN is the TIN that the IRS assigns to a business. "Federal Tax ID" is a plain-English name for the same EIN. These are not competing numbers. They describe the same 9-digit business identifier from different angles.

The IRS uses "Taxpayer Identification Number" (TIN) as a category. Inside that category sit several specific numbers: the EIN for businesses, the SSN for individuals, and the ITIN for individuals without an SSN. When a US bank or platform asks for your "federal tax ID" or "business TIN," it wants your EIN.

<Card title="Federal Tax ID = EIN" index={2}>"Federal Tax ID Number" is marketing language for the EIN. There is no separate application. See [EIN vs Federal Tax ID](/ein-vs-federal-tax-id/).</Card>

## Does a Foreign Business Number Replace a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

No. A foreign business number never replaces a US EIN. A UK UTR, Australian ABN, Indian PAN, Canadian CRN, or EU VAT number identifies you to a foreign tax authority. None of them have standing with the IRS or with US banks and platforms.

If you form a US LLC, you need a US EIN even when you already hold a foreign business ID. The two systems do not connect. Your VAT number proves nothing to a US bank. Your EIN proves nothing to the French DGFiP. You keep both, and you use each in its own country.

| Foreign ID | Country | US EIN still required? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| VAT number | European Union | Yes |
| ABN | Australia | Yes |
| UTR | United Kingdom | Yes |
| PAN | India | Yes |
| CRN / BN | Canada | Yes |
| GST number | Various | Yes |

## How Does a Non-Resident Apply for an EIN After <Accent>Comparing Options?</Accent>

A non-resident applies by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool stays blocked without an SSN, so the fax method is the route. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm You Need an EIN, Not an ITIN">An EIN is for your US LLC. An ITIN is for you as an individual. If you need both, ein.so files the [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and the ITIN separately.</Step>

## What Do You Do With an EIN After You <Accent>Get It?</Accent>

You use the EIN to open a US bank account, accept US payments, and file required US returns. The EIN is the federal tax ID that connects your foreign-owned LLC to the US financial and tax systems. It unlocks banking, payment processing, and platform onboarding.

A US business bank account needs the EIN, the formation documents, and a passport. Mercury and Relay approve many non-resident LLC owners remotely. Tax compliance starts with the EIN too. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Most LLCs also file a [BOI report](/boi-filing/) with FinCEN.
