## How Do Costa Rican Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Costa Rican citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Costa Rica passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Costa Rican applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Costa Rican founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Costa Rica.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Costa Rica passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Costa Rica address. No Cedula Juridica or DGT registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Costa Rica address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Costa Rica passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Costa Rican Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Costa Rican resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The LLC still files an annual information return, and you must confirm your own situation with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Costa Rica Reporting" index={2}>Costa Rican residents declare income to the DGT under local rules. A foreign-held US LLC does not remove your Costa Rica obligations. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign company with a Costa Rican contador.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={3}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and deadlines to avoid penalties. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
