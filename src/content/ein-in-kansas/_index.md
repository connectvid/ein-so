## Is a Kansas EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Kansas EIN and a federal EIN are the same number. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN that works in all 50 states. Kansas does not issue a separate EIN. Your federal EIN and your Kansas state tax registration are two different identifiers with two different purposes.

The confusion is common, so here is the clear split. Your federal EIN comes from the IRS through Form SS-4. It handles federal tax filing, payroll reporting, and banking. Your Kansas state tax ID comes from the Kansas Department of Revenue. It handles Kansas sales tax and state withholding.

| Identifier | Issuer | Purpose | How to Get It |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll | Form SS-4 |
| Kansas state tax ID | Kansas Department of Revenue | Sales tax, state withholding | State registration |

You may need both numbers, depending on what your Kansas business does. A business selling taxable goods in Kansas registers for state sales tax. The EIN never replaces state registration. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction and our [EIN cost breakdown](/ein-cost/) for the federal side.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Kansas?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Kansas through 1 of 4 IRS methods: online, fax, phone, or mail. The method depends on whether you have a US Social Security Number. The process is federal and identical in every state, so a Kansas applicant follows the same steps as a Texas or Florida applicant.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 days | $0 (DIY) / $49 (ein.so) | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day (some) | $0 | Varies |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

A US resident with an SSN uses the IRS online tool at irs.gov and receives the EIN instantly at no cost. A non-resident without an SSN cannot use the online tool. The non-resident path is the fax method, where the passport number replaces the SSN. See the full [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) walkthrough and the [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) details.

### Steps for a Kansas Business

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Kansas LLC">Register your LLC with the Kansas Secretary of State and appoint a registered agent with a Kansas address. The LLC must exist before you apply, because Form SS-4 asks for your legal name and formation date. Confirm the current Kansas filing fee on the official Secretary of State site.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your LLC name, formation state, and formation date. US residents need an SSN. Non-residents need a valid passport. No ITIN is required for the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Kansas LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN or passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Faxed applications go to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Kansas LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident can get an EIN for a Kansas LLC without an SSN or an ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and fax the form to the IRS at 855-215-1627. The IRS issues the EIN to non-residents the same way it does to residents.

The blocker is the IRS online tool. It requires a US SSN, so it locks out every foreign founder. That is not a dead end. The fax method exists precisely for applicants without an SSN. The cost of the EIN is still $0 from the IRS; the work is in preparing Form SS-4 correctly and reaching the IRS fax line.

<Card title="No SSN or ITIN Needed" index={1}>Your passport number goes on Line 7b. You do not need a Social Security Number or an ITIN to get the EIN. See our [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) for the full method.</Card>
<Card title="Fax Is the Path" index={2}>Because the online tool is blocked, non-residents fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627. A correctly filed form returns an EIN in 4-7 business days.</Card>
<Card title="Address Formatting Matters" index={3}>International addresses do not map cleanly to the SS-4 fields, and the IRS rejects malformed entries silently. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style.</Card>
<Card title="ein.so Files It for You" index={4}>ein.so prepares and faxes the SS-4, then delivers your EIN by email. Standard is $49 (4-7 days); Express is $97 (2-3 days). See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).</Card>

## What Tax Filings Does a Kansas LLC With an EIN <Accent>Need?</Accent>

A Kansas LLC with an EIN files at both the federal and state level. Federal filings depend on ownership and structure. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. State filings go to the Kansas Department of Revenue.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. This is a federal filing, separate from Kansas.</Card>
