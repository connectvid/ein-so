## How Do Kenyan Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Kenyan citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Kenya passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Kenyan applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Kenyan founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You complete the LLC entirely online from Kenya.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Kenya passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Kenya address. No KRA PIN or Kenyan registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Kenya address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Kenya passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) and ein.so emails it to you.</Step>
</Steps>

| SS-4 Field | Kenyan Entry |

## What Should Kenyan Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Kenyan resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, so the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The LLC still must file an annual Form 5472 information return. Confirm your full position with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is filed free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Kenyan Reporting (KRA)" index={3}>Kenyan residents declare worldwide income to the Kenya Revenue Authority. Confirm how a foreign-held US LLC affects your KRA filings with a Kenyan tax advisor. ein.so does not advise on KRA rules.</Card>
<Card title="Double Taxation" index={4}>Check whether a US-Kenya tax arrangement reduces withholding on your income. Treaty positions are fact-specific. A US CPA experienced with East African owners should confirm your treatment before you rely on any rate.</Card>
