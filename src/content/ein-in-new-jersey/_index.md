## Is a New Jersey EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A New Jersey EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a single federal number issued by the IRS, and it is identical regardless of the state where your business operates. New Jersey does not issue its own version of an EIN.

People often confuse the federal EIN with New Jersey's state tax registration. They are two distinct numbers from two distinct authorities. You get the EIN once from the IRS, then register separately with New Jersey for state taxes if your activities require it.

| Number | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 |
| NJ state tax ID | NJ Division of Taxation | State sales tax, employer withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration, and your state tax ID does not replace the EIN. Most active New Jersey businesses need both. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for how the two fit together. The EIN comes first because banks and the state both ask for it.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in New Jersey?</Accent>

You get an EIN in New Jersey by filing IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN use the IRS online tool for instant issuance. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | $0 | No |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The IRS online tool at irs.gov requires an SSN or ITIN. Non-residents who lack both cannot use it and must use the fax method. The fax path is the standard route for international founders. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full walkthrough.

### Steps to Apply

<Steps>

Yes. A non-resident can get an EIN for a New Jersey LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b as the responsible party, and the IRS issues the EIN in 4-7 business days by fax.

Non-US residents form New Jersey businesses to reach the US market, access the Port of New York and New Jersey for logistics, or run e-commerce stores serving American buyers. The EIN requirement is the same for them as for residents. The only difference is the application method.

## What Do You Do After Getting an EIN <Accent>in New Jersey?</Accent>

After getting your EIN, you register with the New Jersey Division of Taxation, open a US business bank account, complete federal BOI reporting, and set up payments. The EIN is the key that unlocks each of these steps.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Register With NJ Division of Taxation" index={1}>New Jersey requires businesses to register for state tax obligations such as sales tax and employer withholding. This state registration is separate from your federal EIN. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for details.</Card>
<Card title="Open a US Business Bank Account" index={2}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise accept LLC owners with an EIN and a passport. You receive US account and routing numbers for ACH and domestic wires. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
