## Is an Arizona EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. An Arizona EIN is the same as a federal EIN because the IRS issues every EIN as a federal number. Arizona does not create its own EIN. The same 9-digit number (XX-XXXXXXX) works for your Arizona business nationwide.

The confusion comes from a second number Arizona uses. Your federal EIN and your Arizona state tax registration are two separate items with two separate purposes.

| Identifier | Issuer | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form SS-4 |
| Arizona state tax registration | Arizona Department of Revenue | State-level taxes such as transaction privilege tax |

You may need both. The EIN does not replace Arizona state tax registration, and state registration does not replace the EIN. Get the EIN first, because most banks and payment processors require it before you open an account. See our [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) explainer for the distinction and our [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) breakdown.

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an EIN <Accent>in Arizona?</Accent>

Non-residents apply for an Arizona EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the route for non-residents. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Arizona LLC">Register your LLC with the Arizona Corporation Commission through a registered agent. This gives you a legal entity to attach the EIN to. Confirm the current state filing fee on the Commission website. You can complete formation online from outside the United States.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required. Non-residents from over 150 countries use this same process.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Arizona LLC name on Line 1, your home address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent the errors that cause IRS rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by mail or fax.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Non-Resident Arizona Owners

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Arizona LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |
