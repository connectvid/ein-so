## Who Can Use the <Accent>EIN Online Application?</Accent>

US residents and entities with a responsible party who holds a valid SSN or ITIN can use the EIN online application. The IRS EIN Assistant verifies the responsible party against a US tax record, so anyone without an SSN or ITIN is blocked. The principal business must also be located in the US or its territories.

The online tool sets four hard requirements. Each one is a gate; failing any one stops the application.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="A US Tax ID" index={1}>The responsible party must enter a valid SSN or ITIN. The system cross-checks this against IRS records in real time. A passport number does not work online. Applicants without an SSN or ITIN use the fax route described below. See [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/).</Card>
<Card title="An Individual Responsible Party" index={2}>The responsible party must be a person, not another company. For an LLC, this is usually the managing member or owner. The IRS removed the option to name an entity as responsible party in 2018. See [responsible party on EIN](/responsible-party-ein/).</Card>
<Card title="A US Principal Location" index={3}>The entity must report a principal address in the United States or a US territory. The online tool does not accept a foreign principal address. Non-residents with a US LLC still cannot use it because the responsible party lacks an SSN.</Card>
<Card title="One EIN Per Day" index={4}>The IRS limits each responsible party to one EIN per day, online or otherwise. If you need EINs for multiple entities, space the applications across business days. This daily limit applies to every application method.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## How Do You Apply for an EIN Online With the <Accent>IRS EIN Assistant?</Accent>

You apply for an EIN online by opening the IRS EIN Assistant at irs.gov, selecting your entity type, entering the responsible party SSN or ITIN, supplying your business details, and submitting. The IRS issues your EIN immediately. The full process takes 10-15 minutes, and the tool runs Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 10:00 PM Eastern Time.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Open the IRS EIN Assistant">Go to irs.gov and find the EIN Assistant on the Small Business page. Use only the official irs.gov domain. Avoid third-party sites that copy the IRS interface and charge a fee for the free government form.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Select Your Entity Type">Choose your structure: sole proprietorship, partnership, corporation, S-corporation, LLC, trust, estate, or other. Your selection determines which IRS returns you file later. An LLC asks how many members it has next.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Enter the Responsible Party">Provide the responsible party name and SSN or ITIN. This must be an individual. The IRS validates the number against its records before letting you continue. A failed match ends the session.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Enter Business Details">Supply the legal name, trade name (DBA), US business address, county, state, reason for applying, date the business started, closing month of the accounting year, expected employee count, and principal activity.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Review and Submit">Check every field for accuracy. A wrong entity name or address requires a later correction by mail. Submit when correct. The IRS issues your EIN on the confirmation screen instantly.</Step>
<Step step="6" title="Save Your EIN Letter">Download and save the CP 575 confirmation notice as a PDF. Print a copy. You need this letter to open a [US bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/), file taxes, and verify your business with payment processors.</Step>
</Steps>

### Information Checklist Before You Start

The session expires after 15 minutes of inactivity and does not save progress. Gather everything first.

| Field | What to Enter |
| --- | --- |
| Responsible party | Full legal name plus SSN or ITIN |
| Entity type | LLC, corporation, partnership, sole prop, etc. |
| Legal name | Exact name on your formation documents |
| US address | Street, city, county, state, ZIP |
| Reason for applying | Started new business, banking, hiring, etc. |
| Date started | Month and year the business began |
| Principal activity | E.g., software, e-commerce, consulting |

For the underlying form fields, read the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).

## Who Cannot Use the <Accent>EIN Online Application?</Accent>

Non-US residents without an SSN or ITIN cannot use the EIN online application, because the IRS EIN Assistant verifies the responsible party against a US tax record. The tool also blocks foreign principal addresses, runs only on weekday business hours, and serves one EIN per responsible party per day. These applicants file Form SS-4 by fax instead.

The limitations below explain why millions of international founders skip the online tool entirely.

1. **No SSN or ITIN** — The system requires a US tax ID and rejects passport numbers. This blocks most non-residents.
2. **Weekend and evening closures** — The tool is offline outside Mon-Fri 7am-10pm ET, including most federal holidays.
3. **One EIN per day** — Each responsible party gets one EIN per day across all methods.
4. **US principal address only** — A foreign principal business address is not accepted online.
5. **No saved progress** — A 15-minute timeout ends the session, forcing a full restart.

## How Do Non-Residents Get an EIN Without the <Accent>Online Tool?</Accent>

Non-residents get an EIN by filing Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS does not require a Social Security Number, an ITIN, or a US visit for international applicants. The IRS issues most error-free faxed EINs within 4-7 business days.

This is the same federal process that issues every EIN; only the channel differs. The fax method has run for decades and remains stable.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Complete Form SS-4 With Your Passport">Enter your entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Write "Foreign" if the SSN field demands a value.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Fax the SS-4 to the IRS">Send the completed form to the IRS EIN fax line at 855-215-1627. International applicants use this dedicated number. ein.so handles this step so you avoid international fax setup.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Receive Your CP 575 EIN Letter">The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter, the CP 575. Standard ein.so processing takes 4-7 business days; Express takes 2-3 business days. Your EIN never expires.</Step>
</Steps>

A common failure point is address formatting. The IRS silently rejects malformed international addresses, and the applicant waits weeks for a letter that never arrives. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style before faxing. ## What Should You Do After You Get Your <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

After you get your EIN, open a US business bank account, connect payment processors, file your beneficial ownership report, and meet your annual IRS filing duties. The EIN is the starting point for US banking, payments, and tax compliance, not the final step.

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC carries specific federal filing duties. Missing them is expensive.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Open a US Bank Account" index={1}>Use your EIN, CP 575 letter, and passport to open an account with Mercury, Relay, or Wise. Most approve non-resident LLC owners remotely within a few business days. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="File Your BOI Report" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and complete the report to avoid penalties. The filing itself is free at fincen.gov.</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 Annually" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill. Confirm specifics with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Set Up Payments" index={4}>Connect your EIN to Stripe, PayPal, or Payoneer to bill US customers in dollars. A US entity with an EIN unlocks payout rails that favor US businesses. Pair this with your new US bank account.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Confirm your tax filings with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters. Your EIN opens the door; a CPA keeps you compliant.
