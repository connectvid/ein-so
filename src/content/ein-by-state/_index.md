## Why Is the EIN the Same in <Accent>Every State?</Accent>

The EIN is the same in every state because the Internal Revenue Service, a federal agency, issues it under one national system. States do not assign EINs. They register your LLC and grant a separate state entity number, but that state number is not your tax ID.

Many non-residents confuse two different identifiers. The first is the state entity ID, assigned when your LLC is registered with a Secretary of State. The second is the EIN, assigned by the IRS. Banks, Amazon, and Stripe ask for the EIN, not the state ID. You apply for the EIN once. It works for a Wyoming LLC and a Florida LLC the same way.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| State entity number | Secretary of State | State formation records |
| EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, payments |
| ITIN | IRS | Personal tax ID for individuals |

The practical result is simple. You do not need a "by state" EIN strategy. You need one Form SS-4 filed correctly. Read the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [complete EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/) to see every line explained.

State filing fees vary widely, and state rules change. Confirm the current fee on your state's Secretary of State website before you form. The four factors below drive most non-resident decisions.

## How Do You Apply for an EIN in <Accent>Any State?</Accent>

You apply for an EIN in any state by faxing Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register your LLC in your chosen state through a registered agent. This works the same in Wyoming, Delaware, Florida, or any other state. You can complete it online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name, your state and formation date, and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to get the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent silent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

This four-step path is identical in all 50 states. The only difference between a Texas application and an Ohio application is the state name on Line 1. See [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for full detail on the no-SSN route.

## What Do You File After Getting an EIN in <Accent>Any State?</Accent>

After getting an EIN, a foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. This federal requirement applies in all 50 states. Confirm your filings with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. This is an information return and applies regardless of which state your LLC sits in.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and deadlines so you do not miss the window. This federal report is the same in every state.</Card>
<Card title="State Annual Report" index={3}>Your state requires its own annual or biennial report with a renewal fee. This is the one filing that does vary by state. Check your state guide and the Secretary of State for the current amount and due date.</Card>
<Card title="ITIN If Needed" index={4}>Some non-residents also need an ITIN for personal US tax matters. ein.so offers an ITIN service for $197 Standard or $297 Express. The EIN is for your business; the ITIN is for you as an individual.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
