## How Do Irish Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Irish citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Irish passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Irish founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Ireland.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Irish passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Irish address. No CRO Number or Irish registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Irish address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Irish passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

### Ireland-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Irish Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Irish address (street, town, county, Eircode) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Irish passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Irish Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Irish Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Irish resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The LLC must still file an annual information return. Confirm your exact position with a US CPA.

<Card title="Irish Reporting" index={2}>Irish residents declare worldwide income to the Revenue Commissioners. Income from your US LLC is reported in Ireland under Irish rules. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with an Irish tax adviser.</Card>
