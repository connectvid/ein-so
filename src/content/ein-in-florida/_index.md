## Is a Florida EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Florida EIN is the same as a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN per business, and it works in every state. Florida does not issue its own EIN. Your Florida business uses the identical number it would use anywhere in the US.

Confusion comes from mixing two separate registrations. The federal EIN identifies your business to the IRS. A Florida tax registration identifies your business to the Florida Department of Revenue for state taxes. These are different numbers with different purposes.

| Identifier | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) |
| Florida tax registration | Department of Revenue | State sales and use tax, reemployment tax |

Most non-resident-owned Florida LLCs that sell services or digital products to US clients need only the federal EIN. Businesses that sell taxable goods inside Florida register separately with the Department of Revenue. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction. Confirm your sales tax duties with a CPA.

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Florida LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 with your Florida LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Line 7b is where non-residents replace the SSN. Errors here cause most IRS rejections.

| SS-4 Field | Florida LLC Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Florida LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your address (home country or US registered agent) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, single-member or multi-member |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

Single-member LLCs select the disregarded-entity treatment unless they elect otherwise. The IRS reads Line 7b carefully; a missing or malformed entry blocks the EIN.
