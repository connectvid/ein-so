An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a 9-digit tax ID the IRS issues to people who must file or report US taxes but cannot get a Social Security Number. The IRS issues every ITIN through Form W-7. You cannot apply online, by phone, or by fax. The number always starts with 9 and follows the format 9XX-XX-XXXX. An ITIN identifies an individual, not a business. A US business uses an EIN instead. This guide explains the full W-7 process, the 3 filing methods, document rules, costs, and how an ITIN differs from an [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).

## What Are the 3 Ways to Get <Accent>an ITIN?</Accent>

You get an ITIN through one of 3 methods: mail to the IRS, a Certifying Acceptance Agent, or an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. All 3 use Form W-7 and take 7-11 weeks to process. The methods differ in whether you keep your original passport.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Mail to the IRS" index={1}>Mail Form W-7, your federal tax return, and your original passport or a copy certified by the issuing agency to the IRS ITIN Operation, PO Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342. This method is the most common but requires you to part with your original document.</Card>
<Card title="Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)" index={2}>A CAA verifies your passport in person and certifies copies, so you keep your original document. The CAA submits Form W-7 on your behalf. Find an authorized CAA on the IRS list at irs.gov. CAA fees vary by agent.</Card>
<Card title="IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center" index={3}>Book an appointment at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center and bring your documents. Staff verify your identity in person and return your passport the same visit. These centers exist only in US cities, so this method suits applicants already in the United States.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

| Method | Processing | Keep Original Passport? | Best For |
|--------|-----------|------------------------|----------|
| Mail | 7-11 weeks | No | Applicants comfortable mailing originals |
| CAA | 7-11 weeks | Yes | Non-residents abroad |
| IRS office | 7-11 weeks | Yes | Applicants inside the US |

## What Do You Need to Apply for <Accent>an ITIN?</Accent>

You need a completed Form W-7, a federal tax return, and proof of identity and foreign status. A valid passport is the only document that satisfies both identity and foreign status on its own. Without a passport, you submit 2 documents from the IRS list.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Completed Form W-7">Form W-7 is the IRS Application for Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. You state your reason for applying, your name, your foreign address, and your foreign tax ID if you have one. See the detailed [W-7 form guide](/w7-form-guide/) for line-by-line help.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Federal Tax Return">Attach Form 1040 or 1040-NR unless you meet 1 of the 5 listed exceptions. The return is the primary reason the IRS issues an ITIN. Without a return or a valid exception, the IRS rejects the application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Identity and Foreign Status Documents">Submit a valid passport on its own, or 2 documents from the IRS list. Acceptable documents include a national ID card, foreign or US driver's license, birth certificate, foreign voter registration card, or US state ID. At least one must show a photo.</Step>
</Steps>

### What Counts as a Valid Document?

The IRS accepts 13 document types for ITIN identity proof. Every document must be current and unexpired. The passport is the only standalone document; all others must be combined to cover both identity and foreign status.

<InfoBox title="Common acceptable documents">
<ul>
<li>Passport (standalone — covers identity and foreign status)</li>
<li>National identification card (photo, name, address, date of birth)</li>
<li>Foreign driver's license</li>
<li>US driver's license</li>
<li>Birth certificate (required for dependents under 18)</li>
<li>Foreign voter registration card</li>
<li>US state identification card</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

## How Does the ITIN Application Process <Accent>Work Step by Step?</Accent>

The ITIN process runs in 5 steps: confirm you need an ITIN, complete Form W-7, prepare your tax return, gather documents, and submit by your chosen method. The IRS reviews the packet, assigns the ITIN, and mails an assignment notice in 7-11 weeks.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm You Need an ITIN">Verify that you have a US tax filing or reporting requirement and cannot get an SSN. If you only need a tax ID for a US LLC, you need an EIN instead. Read [ITIN number](/itin-number/) to confirm your situation.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form W-7">Select your reason code, enter your name exactly as it appears on your passport, list your foreign address, and add your foreign tax ID if applicable. Sign and date the form. Errors on Form W-7 are a leading cause of rejection.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Prepare Your Federal Tax Return">Complete Form 1040 or 1040-NR for the relevant tax year, or document your exception. Write "ITIN TO BE REQUESTED" where the SSN or ITIN normally goes. The return and W-7 travel together in the same envelope.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your valid passport or 2 substitute documents. If mailing, decide whether to send originals or agency-certified copies. If using a CAA, schedule your identity verification.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Submit and Wait">Mail to Austin, hand to a CAA, or visit an IRS office. The IRS processes the packet in 7-11 weeks and mails a CP 565 notice with your assigned ITIN. Keep this notice; you reuse the ITIN on every future US return.</Step>
</Steps>

The IRS does not accept Form W-7 by fax, phone, or online. Paper submission is the only channel. Plan around the 7-11 week window, especially if you file near the April tax deadline.
</Section>

## How Long Does It Take to Get <Accent>an ITIN?</Accent>

The IRS processes an ITIN in 7-11 weeks. Applications filed during peak tax season from January through April take 9-11 weeks. Off-season applications take about 7 weeks. The IRS offers no expedited ITIN service and no electronic filing.

| Season | Processing Time |
|--------|----------------|
| Peak (January-April) | 9-11 weeks |
| Off-season (May-December) | 7 weeks |

These windows cover IRS review only. Add mailing time for your documents to reach Austin and for the CP 565 notice to reach your foreign address. International mail adds 1-3 weeks on each leg. Compare this to the [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) of 4-7 business days, which is why a US LLC owner needs the EIN, not an ITIN, to open a bank account quickly.

## Do You Need an ITIN or an EIN for Your <Accent>US LLC?</Accent>

You need an EIN, not an ITIN, to run a US LLC. The EIN is the business tax ID required for banking, payments, and federal filings. An ITIN identifies an individual person, while an EIN identifies a business entity. Many non-residents only ever need the EIN.

A non-resident forming a US LLC does not need an SSN or an ITIN to get an EIN. On Form SS-4 Line 7b, the responsible party enters a passport number in place of an SSN or ITIN. The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN, so non-residents file by fax. ein.so faxes the SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 and delivers the EIN by email.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Use an EIN for the business" index={1}>Open a US bank account, accept Stripe and PayPal, and file business taxes with an EIN. Read [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) to see how the business identity works.</Card>
<Card title="Use an ITIN for the individual" index={2}>Claim a treaty benefit, report personal US income, or appear on a US return as a spouse or dependent with an ITIN. It is a personal number, separate from any business.</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 with your EIN" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This filing uses the LLC's EIN, not an ITIN.</Card>
<Card title="File your BOI report" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) so your new entity stays compliant.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

If you came here to set up a US company, the [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) path is what you want. If you have a personal US tax obligation without an SSN, the ITIN is the correct number.

## Why Do ITIN Applications Get <Accent>Rejected?</Accent>

The IRS rejects ITIN applications for missing tax returns, expired documents, name mismatches, and incomplete Form W-7 fields. A rejection restarts the 7-11 week clock. Most rejections trace to 4 avoidable errors.

<Alert type="warning" title="Top causes of ITIN rejection">
<ol>
<li>No federal tax return attached and no valid exception claimed.</li>
<li>An expired passport or an expired substitute document.</li>
<li>The name on Form W-7 does not match the name on the passport.</li>
<li>A missing reason code or unsigned Form W-7.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

Check every document for an expiration date before you submit. Match your name letter for letter to your passport. Confirm any exception with a CPA before filing without a tax return, because the IRS treats a missing return as the single most common rejection reason. ein.so does not provide tax advice; for personal tax questions, work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters.
