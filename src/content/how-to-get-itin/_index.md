<Section>
<Capsule>**Apply for an ITIN by submitting IRS Form W-7 with a federal tax return and identity documents.** Three methods: (1) Mail to IRS ITIN Operation, PO Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342. (2) Through an IRS Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA). (3) In-person at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. Processing takes 7-11 weeks.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>Methods</Label>
## 3 Ways to Get <Accent>an ITIN</Accent>

### Method 1: Mail (Most Common)
Mail Form W-7 + tax return + original ID documents to:
**IRS ITIN Operation, PO Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342**

### Method 2: Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)
CAAs verify your identity and documents locally. You keep your original passport. Find a CAA at irs.gov.

### Method 3: IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center
Visit an IRS office with your documents. Available in major US cities only.

| Method | Processing | Keep Originals? |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Mail | 7-11 weeks | No (sent to IRS) |
| CAA | 7-11 weeks | Yes |
| In-person | 7-11 weeks | Yes |

1. **Completed [Form W-7](/w7-form-guide/)**

3. **Identity documents** — passport (standalone) OR 2 from: national ID, driver's license, birth certificate, visa, state ID
