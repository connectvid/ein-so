## How Do Australian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Australian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Australian passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Australian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Australian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Australia.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Australian passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Australian address. No ABN, TFN, or Australian registration is required for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Australian address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Australian passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Australia-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Australian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Australian address (street, suburb, state, postcode) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Australian passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

A common rejection cause is address formatting. The Australian suburb, state, and 4-digit postcode do not map cleanly to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed international addresses. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Should Australian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Australian Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Australian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still carry US information-reporting duties, and you continue to report income in Australia under ATO rules.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-Australia Tax Considerations" index={2}>A US-Australia income tax treaty exists and can affect withholding and double taxation. Treaty outcomes depend on income type and your situation. Confirm with a CPA before relying on any treaty position.</Card>
<Card title="Australian Reporting" index={3}>Australian residents generally report worldwide income and foreign company interests to the ATO. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with an Australian registered tax agent or accountant.</Card>

## How Does an Australian ABN Differ From a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

An Australian ABN identifies your business to the ATO and has no standing in the United States. A US EIN identifies your US entity to the IRS and US banks. The two are separate systems; an ABN does not substitute for an EIN, and you need an EIN to run a US LLC.
