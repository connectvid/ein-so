## Is a Missouri EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Missouri EIN is the same federal EIN issued by the IRS to businesses in every state. The IRS assigns one 9-digit number (XX-XXXXXXX) that works nationwide. Missouri does not issue a separate state version of the EIN.

Your federal EIN and your Missouri state tax registration are two different things. The EIN comes from the IRS for federal tax filing, banking, and payment processing. The Missouri state tax ID comes from the Department of Revenue for state-level obligations like sales tax collection or employer withholding.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, bank account, Stripe, payroll |
| Missouri state tax ID | Missouri Department of Revenue | State sales tax, employer withholding |

You may need both, depending on your business activity in Missouri. The EIN does not replace state registration. A non-resident with no US employees and no in-state sales may need only the federal EIN. Confirm your state registration duties with a CPA. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction in detail.

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Missouri?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Missouri by filing IRS Form SS-4. US applicants with an SSN use the IRS online tool for same-day issuance. Non-residents without an SSN file by fax to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. Most correctly filed faxes return an EIN in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [IRS online](/ein-online-application/) | Same day | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day call | $0 | Varies |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The process is identical in every state because the EIN is federal. Missouri location does not change a single SS-4 field. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the complete walkthrough and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for current timelines.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Missouri LLC">Register your LLC with the Missouri Secretary of State at sos.mo.gov, or use a registered agent. This gives you a legal US entity and a business address to attach the EIN to. Non-residents complete this entirely online. Confirm the current Missouri filing fee on the official site.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and Missouri formation details, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visa is required to obtain the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Missouri LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent the errors that cause IRS rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Missouri LLC Owners Know About <Accent>Taxes and Filing?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, so income passes through to the owner. A foreign-owned single-member Missouri LLC must still file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.
