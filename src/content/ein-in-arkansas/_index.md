## Is the Arkansas EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

The Arkansas EIN is not a separate number. The EIN is a single federal identifier issued by the IRS, and it stays the same whether your business operates in Arkansas, Texas, or any other state. Arkansas does not issue its own EIN.

Many founders confuse the federal EIN with Arkansas state tax registration. They are two distinct numbers from two distinct authorities. You may need both, depending on your activities in the state.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 |
| Arkansas state tax ID | Department of Finance and Administration | State sales tax and withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. If you sell taxable goods or hire employees in Arkansas, register separately with the Department of Finance and Administration. See our [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) guide for the distinction. The federal EIN comes first, because banks and the IRS require it before any state-level setup.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN <Accent>in Arkansas?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN in Arkansas by filing IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is required. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents must use the fax route. ein.so handles this filing remotely.

Non-residents can form an Arkansas LLC and obtain an EIN without ever entering the United States. The federal EIN process is identical in all 50 states.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Arkansas LLC">Register your LLC with the Arkansas Secretary of State through a registered agent with an Arkansas address. This creates the legal entity you attach the EIN to. Confirm the current state filing fee on the Secretary of State website, since fees change.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required for the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. See our [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for every field.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The form is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## How Do Arkansas and Federal Taxes Work With <Accent>an EIN?</Accent>

Your federal EIN handles IRS reporting, while a separate Arkansas state tax ID handles state filings. Arkansas levies a state income tax administered by the Department of Finance and Administration; confirm the current rate with a CPA. The EIN itself is not a tax bill, it is an identifier.

A single-member US LLC owned by a non-resident is usually treated as a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. The owner reports income rather than the LLC, but the LLC still carries information-return duties.

<Alert type="warning" title="Foreign-owned LLC filing duties">
<ol>
<li>File Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 every year for a foreign-owned single-member LLC.</li>
<li>The penalty for failing to file Form 5472 is $25,000.</li>
<li>File a federal BOI report with FinCEN for most LLCs.</li>
<li>Confirm Arkansas state tax registration needs with the Department of Finance and Administration.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>
