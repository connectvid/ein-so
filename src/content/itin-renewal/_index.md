<Capsule>**ITINs expire if not used on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years.** ITINs with middle digits 70-88 have also expired. Renew by filing Form W-7 with the Renew box checked. No tax return is required for renewal-only submissions. Processing takes 7-11 weeks.</Capsule>

<Section surface>
<Label>When to Renew</Label>
## When Your ITIN <Accent>Expires</Accent>

### Expiration Rules
1. **3-year non-use:** If you did not use your ITIN on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years, it expires
2. **Middle digits 70-88:** All ITINs with middle digits 70-88 have expired regardless of use

### How to Check
If your ITIN is 9XX-70-XXXX through 9XX-88-XXXX, it has expired. If you have not filed a US tax return in 3+ years, it has likely expired.
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>How to Renew</Label>
## How to Renew <Accent>Your ITIN</Accent>

1. Complete [Form W-7](/w7-form-guide/)
2. Check box **h** (Renew existing ITIN)
3. Attach identity documents (passport or 2 alternatives)
4. **No tax return needed** for renewal-only
5. Mail to IRS ITIN Operation, Austin TX 78714
6. Wait 7-11 weeks

### Family Renewal
You can renew ITINs for your entire family on one submission. Include Form W-7 for each family member.
