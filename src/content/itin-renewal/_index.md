The format is 9XX-XX-XXXX, and every ITIN starts with the digit 9. An ITIN is not permanent. The IRS expires ITINs that go unused for 3 years and has expired entire batches by their middle digits. ## When Does Your <Accent>ITIN Expire?</Accent>

Your ITIN expires after 3 consecutive years without use on a federal tax return. The IRS also expired all ITINs with middle digits 70 through 88 in prior annual batches. Both rules apply independently, so check your number against each one before your next filing.

The IRS retires ITINs to keep its records current. Two separate rules trigger expiration, and either one can deactivate your number.

### The Two Expiration Rules

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Three-Year Non-Use Rule" index={1}>An ITIN expires if you do not use it on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years. If you last filed a US return in 2022 and skipped 2023, 2024, and 2025, your ITIN has expired. You must renew before filing your next return.</Card>
<Card title="Middle-Digit Rule" index={2}>The IRS expired all ITINs with middle digits 70 through 88 in earlier batches. If your number reads 9XX-70-XXXX through 9XX-88-XXXX, it has expired regardless of how recently you used it. These numbers must be renewed before use.</Card>
<Card title="How to Check Your Number" index={3}>Read the middle two digits of your ITIN. If they fall between 70 and 88, renew now. Then check your filing history. If you have not attached your ITIN to a return in 3 years, renew before the next filing season.</Card>
<Card title="What Happens If You File With an Expired ITIN" index={4}>The IRS processes the return but disallows exemptions and credits tied to the expired ITIN. Your refund is delayed, and you receive a CP48 or similar notice. Renewing first avoids the hold. File the W-7 with your return only if you missed the early window.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

<Alert type="warning" title="Renew before peak season">
<ol>
<li>The IRS warns that processing extends to 9-11 weeks during peak season, January 15 through April 30.</li>
<li>Submit your renewal in the fall or early winter so your ITIN is active before the April deadline.</li>
<li>Filing a return with an expired ITIN delays any refund until the renewal completes.</li>
</ol>

## How Do You Renew Your <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

You renew your ITIN by completing Form W-7, checking the renewal box, attaching identity documents, and mailing the package to the IRS ITIN Operation in Austin, Texas. A renewal-only Form W-7 does not require a federal tax return. Processing takes 7-11 weeks.

The renewal process mirrors a new application with one major difference: no tax return is attached.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Complete Form W-7">Download the current Form W-7 from IRS.gov. Enter your legal name, mailing address, foreign address, date of birth, and country of citizenship. Write your existing ITIN in the field provided so the IRS matches the renewal to your record.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Check the Renewal Box">On Form W-7, check the box that indicates you are renewing an existing ITIN. This is the step that distinguishes a renewal from a first-time application. Selecting the wrong box causes the IRS to treat the form as new and request a tax return.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Attach Identity Documents">Attach your valid, unexpired passport, which proves both identity and foreign status alone. Without a passport, attach 2 documents from the IRS list, such as a national ID card plus a birth certificate. Send originals or copies certified by the issuing agency.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Skip the Tax Return">Do not attach a tax return for a renewal-only submission. This is the key difference from a first-time ITIN application. Renew before filing season so your number is active when you submit your return later.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Mail to Austin">Mail the package to the IRS ITIN Operation, Austin, TX 78714. You can also use a Certified Acceptance Agent or an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center to verify documents in person and keep your originals.</Step>
<Step step="6" title="Wait 7-11 Weeks">The IRS processes the renewal in 7-11 weeks and returns your original documents within 60 days. You receive a CP565 notice confirming the same ITIN remains active. Track status by calling the IRS ITIN line after 7 weeks.</Step>
</Steps>

### Family Renewal in One Submission

You renew ITINs for your spouse and dependents in a single mailing. Include one Form W-7 and a complete set of identity documents for each family member. The IRS recommends renewing all household ITINs together when any one member's number has expired. This avoids partial processing and reduces the risk of lost documents across separate mailings.

## What Is the Difference Between an <Accent>ITIN and an EIN?</Accent>

An ITIN identifies an individual for US tax purposes, while an EIN identifies a business entity. You renew an ITIN with Form W-7, but an EIN never expires and uses Form SS-4. A non-resident who owns a US LLC needs an EIN for the company, not an ITIN.

Many non-residents confuse the two numbers. The distinction determines which form you file and whether renewal applies at all.

| Factor | ITIN | EIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies | An individual taxpayer | A business entity |
| Format | 9XX-XX-XXXX | XX-XXXXXXX |
| Application form | Form W-7 | Form SS-4 |
| Expires? | Yes, after 3 years of non-use | No, never expires |
| Renewal needed? | Yes | No |
| SSN required? | No | No |
| IRS cost | $0 | $0 |
| ein.so price | $197 / $297 | $49 / $97 |

A non-resident who owns a US LLC almost always needs an EIN, not an ITIN. The EIN opens a US business bank account, runs payroll, and files business tax returns. You get an EIN with [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) using your passport number on Line 7b; no SSN or ITIN is required. The IRS online tool demands an SSN, so non-residents file by fax to 855-215-1627. ein.so handles that fax for $49 Standard or $97 Express. ## What Documents Do You Need to <Accent>Renew an ITIN?</Accent>

You need either a valid, unexpired passport or 2 documents from the IRS approved list to renew an ITIN. A passport is the only document that proves both identity and foreign status by itself. The IRS returns originals within 60 days.

The IRS accepts 13 document types. Most renewals use a passport because it satisfies both requirements with one document.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Passport (Stand-Alone)" index={1}>A valid, unexpired passport is the single document that proves identity and foreign status together. This is the simplest option. Mail the original or a copy certified by the issuing passport agency. The IRS does not accept notarized copies for ITIN purposes.</Card>
<Card title="Two-Document Combination" index={2}>Without a passport, submit 2 documents from the IRS list. A common pair is a national identity card and a foreign birth certificate. Each document must show your name, and at least one must include a photograph and a recent date if applicable.</Card>
<Card title="Certified Acceptance Agent" index={3}>A Certified Acceptance Agent verifies your documents in person so you keep your originals. The CAA submits the Form W-7 with a certificate of accuracy. This avoids mailing your passport across borders for 7-11 weeks.</Card>
<Card title="Original Document Return" index={4}>The IRS returns mailed originals within 60 days. If you do not receive them, call the IRS to trace the documents. Using a CAA or a Taxpayer Assistance Center eliminates this risk because the agent verifies and returns documents on the spot.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## Do Non-Resident Business Owners Need an <Accent>ITIN or an EIN?</Accent>

Non-resident business owners almost always need an EIN, not an ITIN. The EIN is the business tax ID that opens bank accounts and files company returns. An ITIN identifies you as an individual and applies only when you must file a personal US return.

The ITIN and the EIN serve different roles in a US business structure. Mixing them up delays banking and compliance.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Your LLC Needs an EIN" index={1}>A US LLC cannot open a bank account, run Stripe, or file taxes without an EIN. You get it with Form SS-4 and your passport number on Line 7b. The EIN never expires, so there is no renewal. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).</Card>
<Card title="When You Personally Need an ITIN" index={2}>You need an ITIN only when you must file a personal US tax return and lack an SSN. Examples include claiming a treaty benefit or reporting US-sourced personal income. Most LLC owners with non-US income do not need an ITIN at all.</Card>
<Card title="Form 5472 for Foreign-Owned LLCs" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return tied to the EIN, not the ITIN. Confirm your filing with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Banking With the EIN" index={4}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise open US business accounts using the EIN and a passport, not an ITIN. The EIN links your LLC to the US banking system. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) for approved providers.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## How Long Does ITIN <Accent>Renewal Take?</Accent>

ITIN renewal takes 7-11 weeks, the same timeline as a new ITIN application. The IRS extends processing to 9-11 weeks during peak filing season, from January 15 to April 30. Submit your renewal early so your number is active before you file.

The timeline depends mostly on when you mail the package. Off-season submissions clear faster.

| Timeline Factor | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Standard processing | 7 weeks |
| Peak season (Jan 15-Apr 30) | 9-11 weeks |
| Original document return | Within 60 days |
| Confirmation notice | CP565, same ITIN remains |
| Status check | Call IRS ITIN line after 7 weeks |

Renewing in the fall or early winter means your ITIN is active before the April filing deadline. Filing a return with an expired ITIN holds your refund until the renewal completes, which compounds the delay. The EIN process is faster: ein.so delivers EINs in 4-7 business days Standard and 2-3 business days Express, because the SS-4 is faxed to the IRS rather than mailed.
