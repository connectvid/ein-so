## How Do Iranian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Iranian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Iran passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Iranian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most non-resident founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC online from Iran.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Iran passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Iran address. No Economic Code or local registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Iran address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Iran passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) and ein.so emails it to you.</Step>
</Steps>

### Iran-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Iranian Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Iran address (city, province, postal code) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Iran passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Iranian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Filing?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Iranian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, but it must still file an information return each year. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120, and the penalty for not filing is $25,000.

## How Does an EIN Compare to an <Accent>ITIN for Iranian Applicants?</Accent>

An EIN identifies a US business entity, while an ITIN identifies an individual for US tax purposes; Iranian founders usually need the EIN first. The EIN is required for an LLC, a bank account, and Form 5472. An ITIN matters when an individual must file a personal US return.

<InfoBox title="EIN vs ITIN at a glance">
<ul>
<li><strong>EIN</strong> identifies a business; required for every US LLC and for Form 5472 filing.</li>
<li><strong>ITIN</strong> identifies a person; used on a personal US tax return when no SSN exists.</li>
<li><strong>EIN cost:</strong> $0 from the IRS; ein.so files for $49 Standard or $97 Express.</li>
<li><strong>ITIN cost:</strong> ein.so charges $197 Standard or $297 Express for the ITIN service.</li>
<li>Most Iranian founders start with the EIN, then add an ITIN only if a personal filing requires it.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>
