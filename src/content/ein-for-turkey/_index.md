## How Do Turkish Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Turkish citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Turkey passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Turkish applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Turkish founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Turkey.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Turkey passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Turkey address. No Vergi Kimlik Numarası or Turkish registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Turkey address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Turkey passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days. Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Turkey-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Turkish Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Turkey address (mahalle, posta kodu, il) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Turkey passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

A common Turkish rejection cause is address formatting. The posta kodu and il do not map cleanly to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed international addresses. ein.so reformats Turkish addresses to an IRS-accepted style. ## How Do Turkish Applicants Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Turkish applicants get an EIN without an SSN by entering their Turkey passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is required for the federal EIN process. The IRS accepts foreign responsible parties who have no US tax number.

The block is the online tool, not the EIN itself. The IRS online EIN application requires the responsible party to hold an SSN or ITIN. Turkish applicants without either number cannot use the online tool. The fax route exists precisely for this case.

<InfoBox title="Three EIN routes for Turkish applicants">
<ul>
<li><strong>Fax to 855-215-1627</strong> — works without an SSN. Standard ein.so delivery 4-7 business days. This is the method ein.so uses.</li>
<li><strong>Phone at 267-941-1099</strong> — same-day issuance but requires international calls during US business hours and English-language hold times.</li>
<li><strong>Online tool</strong> — fastest but requires an SSN or ITIN, so it is closed to most Turkish applicants.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

You do not need an ITIN to get an EIN. The two numbers serve different purposes. An EIN identifies your business. An ITIN identifies an individual for personal US tax filing. ein.so offers a separate ITIN service ($197 Standard / $297 Express) if your situation later requires one. ## What Should Turkish Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Turkish resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must still file an annual information return with the IRS.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not automatically a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-Turkey Tax Treaty" index={2}>A US-Turkey income tax treaty exists and can affect withholding on certain passive income and help prevent double taxation. Confirm the exact treaty rates that apply to your income with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Turkish Reporting" index={3}>Turkish residents declare worldwide income to the Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı under Turkish rules. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Turkish accountant (mali müşavir).</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and deadlines to avoid penalties. Filing is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
