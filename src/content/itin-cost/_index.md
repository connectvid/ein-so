An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a 9-digit tax ID the IRS issues to individuals who file a US tax return without a Social Security Number. It is not the same as an EIN. An EIN identifies a business; an ITIN identifies a person. The application form is also different: ITIN uses Form W-7, while an EIN uses [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/). The IRS charges nothing for either one. This guide breaks down every real ITIN cost in 2026, explains why ITIN service prices exceed EIN prices, and shows you how to choose the right path.

| IRS fee | $0 | $0 |
| IRS processing time | 7-11 weeks | 4-7 business days |

## How Much Does the IRS Charge for an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

The IRS charges $0 for an ITIN. Form W-7 carries no government filing fee. The IRS issues the number for free to anyone who files a valid application with a tax return and accepted identity documents. Every dollar you pay goes to a private helper, not the government.

You pay third parties for three reasons: to keep your original passport, to prepare the attached tax return, and to avoid IRS rejections. The table below shows the full 2026 cost landscape.

| Provider | Cost | Keep your passport? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| IRS direct (mail) | $0 | No - mail original |
| Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) | $50-$275 | Yes |
| Tax preparer | $150-$500 | Sometimes |
| ein.so ITIN service | $197-$297 | Depends on documents |

A Certifying Acceptance Agent certifies your passport in person or by video, so you keep the document. The IRS authorizes CAAs to verify identity on its behalf. A tax preparer adds value by preparing the Form 1040-NR return that must accompany Form W-7. Confirm which path fits your tax situation with a CPA.

## Why Does an ITIN Cost More Than an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An ITIN costs more than an EIN because it requires Form W-7, a federal tax return, and certified identity documents, while an EIN requires only Form SS-4. ein.so charges $197-$297 for an ITIN and $49-$97 for an EIN. The IRS fee for both is $0.

Most non-US residents who form a US LLC need only an EIN, not an ITIN. An EIN lets your LLC open a bank account, accept payments, and file business taxes. You do not need an ITIN to get an EIN. The [EIN without SSN process](/ein-without-ssn/) uses your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b instead of a Social Security Number.

## Can You Get an ITIN for <Accent>Free?</Accent>

Yes. You can mail Form W-7 with your tax return and original passport directly to the IRS for $0 in government fees. The catch is that your original passport sits at the IRS for 7-11 weeks, and a formatting error restarts the wait. A paid agent removes both risks.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Complete Form W-7">Enter your name exactly as it appears on your passport, your foreign address, your country of citizenship, and the reason code that matches your situation. A wrong reason code is the most common cause of rejection.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Prepare Your Tax Return">Attach Form W-7 to the federal tax return that creates the need for an ITIN, usually Form 1040-NR. A standalone W-7 without a return is rejected unless a specific exception applies. Confirm your filing duty with a CPA.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Provide Identity Documents">Include your original passport or a copy certified by the issuing agency. The IRS accepts a passport as the one document proving both identity and foreign status. Photocopies that are not certified are rejected.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Mail to the IRS and Wait">Mail the full package to the IRS ITIN processing address. Standard review takes 7-11 weeks. The IRS returns your passport and an ITIN assignment letter. No expedited IRS option exists for ITINs.</Step>
</Steps>

The free path works, but mailing an original passport overseas for two-plus months is a real cost. A Certifying Acceptance Agent for $50-$275 lets you keep your passport. ein.so handles the full package for $197-$297. Compare this with the faster [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) if a business ID is all you need.

## Do You Need an ITIN or an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

You need an EIN to run a US business and an ITIN to file a personal US tax return without an SSN. They are not interchangeable. Most non-resident LLC owners need only an EIN. An ITIN becomes relevant when you personally owe a US filing and lack an SSN.

<InfoBox title="Choose based on your goal">
<ul>
<li>**Forming a US LLC?** You need an EIN, not an ITIN. Use the [EIN without SSN method](/ein-without-ssn/).</li>
<li>**Opening a US bank account for your LLC?** The EIN does this. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</li>
<li>**Filing a personal US tax return with no SSN?** You need an ITIN via Form W-7.</li>
<li>**Foreign-owned single-member LLC?** You file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.</li>
<li>**Most non-residents start with an [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) and add an ITIN only if a personal filing requires it.**</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>
