## Do Etsy Sellers <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

Etsy sellers need an EIN if they sell through an LLC, hire help, or sell as a non-US resident with a US company. A US sole proprietor can use an SSN instead, but an EIN keeps that number private on Etsy's 1099-K.

Non-US Etsy sellers are the clearest case. To complete Etsy's tax interview and open the US business banking that Etsy payouts often require, a non-resident seller forms a US LLC and gets an EIN. The IRS online tool blocks non-residents, so they file Form SS-4 by fax with a passport number — no SSN required. [ein.so](/apply/) files it for $49 Standard or $97 Express.

## How Do Etsy Sellers Apply for an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Etsy sellers apply for an EIN in two ways. US residents with an SSN apply free online at irs.gov and receive the EIN instantly. Non-US sellers file [Form SS-4](/tools/ss4-form-helper/) by fax to 855-215-1627, entering a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN.

The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents cannot use it. The fax method is their path. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Decide Sole Proprietor or LLC">US hobby sellers can stay sole proprietors and use an SSN or EIN. Sellers wanting liability protection form an LLC first. Non-US sellers form a Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico LLC to attach the EIN to a US entity.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your legal name and address, your LLC formation details if you have one, and your passport number if you are a non-resident. No prior US registration is required to get the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your shop's legal name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, the responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN or passport number on Line 7b. List "e-commerce" or "retail - handmade goods" as the principal activity. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">US residents submit online for an instant EIN. Non-residents fax the SS-4. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the CP 575 assignment letter with your EIN.</Step>
</Steps>

### Etsy SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Etsy Seller Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your LLC name or your full legal name |

## How Do You Add an EIN to Your <Accent>Etsy Shop?</Accent>

You add an EIN to Etsy through Shop Manager, then Finances, then Legal and tax information. Enter the EIN in the taxpayer identification number field and match the legal name to your exact IRS records to avoid B-notices.

Etsy uses this tax ID to file your 1099-K. A mismatch between the name and EIN triggers IRS backup withholding and B-notices, which freeze part of your payouts. Enter the data carefully and save before your next sales cycle.

<Card title="Open Legal and Tax Information" index={1}>Inside Shop Manager, select Finances, then Legal and tax information. This is where Etsy stores the tax ID it reports to the IRS for your 1099-K filing.</Card>
<Card title="Enter the EIN and Legal Name" index={2}>Type the 9-digit EIN in the XX-XXXXXXX format. The legal name must match the name on your CP 575 assignment letter character for character. LLCs enter the LLC name, not the owner's personal name.</Card>
<Card title="Confirm and Save" index={3}>Save the form and confirm Etsy accepts the EIN. An accepted tax ID keeps payouts flowing and ensures your year-end 1099-K reports income under the correct business identity.</Card>

## What Should Etsy Sellers Know About <Accent>Taxes?</Accent>

Etsy sellers report shop income on their tax return and may owe self-employment tax. A US sole proprietor reports on Schedule C. An LLC reports based on its tax classification. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year.

The 1099-K reports gross sales, not profit. You deduct materials, shipping, and Etsy fees against that gross figure. Confirm your specific filing duties with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="1099-K Reports Gross, Not Profit" index={1}>Etsy's 1099-K shows total sales before fees and costs. You subtract cost of goods, shipping, and Etsy fees to reach taxable profit. Keep records of every expense to lower your taxable income legally.</Card>
<Card title="US Filing for Foreign-Owned LLCs" index={2}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return reporting transactions between you and the LLC.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={3}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties on your Etsy LLC.</Card>
<Card title="Get Professional Advice" index={4}>Tax rules depend on your residency, entity type, and state. Work with a US CPA who handles e-commerce and non-resident sellers. ein.so handles the EIN application only and does not provide tax advice.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Your EIN is the starting point for Etsy tax compliance, not the end of it. See [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for related guidance, and confirm tax specifics with a CPA.

An EIN covers the business. Some non-US Etsy sellers also need a personal US tax ID — an ITIN — to claim tax-treaty benefits or file a personal return. The EIN and the ITIN are separate numbers for separate purposes. Sellers who need both can get an ITIN through our sister service at [itin.so](https://www.itin.so/).
