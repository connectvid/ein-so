## How Do Chilean Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Chilean citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Chilean passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Chilean founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Chile.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Chilean passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Chilean address. No RUT or Chilean registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Chilean address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Chilean passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Chilean Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Chilean Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Chilean resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still carry annual US information-filing duties, and you continue to report income in Chile to the SII.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Chilean Reporting to SII" index={2}>Chilean residents declare worldwide income to the SII under Chilean rules. Your foreign-held US LLC may create reporting duties in Chile. Confirm your obligations with a Chilean contador or tax advisor.</Card>
<Card title="US-Chile Tax Treaty" index={3}>A US-Chile income tax treaty exists and can affect withholding and double taxation relief. Treaty treatment depends on your facts. Confirm any treaty position with a CPA before you rely on it.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) so your Chilean-owned LLC stays compliant after formation.</Card>
