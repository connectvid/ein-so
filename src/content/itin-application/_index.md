An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is a 9-digit tax ID the IRS issues to people who must file a US tax return but cannot get a Social Security Number. The IRS issues an ITIN through Form W-7. Most non-resident business owners confuse the ITIN with the EIN. The two are different IDs that serve different purposes. This guide explains who needs an ITIN, how to file Form W-7 step by step, what documents the IRS accepts, and how the ITIN differs from the EIN your US LLC actually needs.

| Factor | ITIN | EIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purpose | Personal tax ID for individuals | Business tax ID for entities |
| Form | Form W-7 | Form SS-4 |
| Who needs it | Individuals without SSN eligibility | LLCs and corporations |
| Submission | Mail, CAA, or IRS office | Fax to 855-215-1627 |
| IRS fee | $0 | $0 |
| Processing time | 7-11 weeks | 4-7 business days |
| Online option | No | No (for non-residents) |

## Who Needs to File an <Accent>ITIN Application?</Accent>

You need an ITIN when you must file a US federal tax return or claim a treaty benefit but cannot get an SSN. The IRS issues an ITIN only to people with a US tax filing requirement. You do not get an ITIN simply to form a company.

Many non-residents reach for an ITIN when they actually need an [EIN](/ein-for-non-residents/). Read the use cases below to confirm which ID your situation requires.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Non-Resident Filing a US Return" index={1}>A non-resident who earns US-source income and must file Form 1040-NR needs an ITIN to report that income and pay tax. The ITIN goes on the personal return, not on a business filing.</Card>
<Card title="Treaty Benefit Claim" index={2}>A non-resident claiming a reduced withholding rate under a tax treaty often needs an ITIN to support the claim with a withholding agent or on a tax return. Confirm the exact requirement with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Dependent or Spouse on a US Return" index={3}>A spouse or dependent of a US taxpayer who is listed on a US return but cannot get an SSN needs an ITIN. This is one of the most common ITIN reason codes on Form W-7.</Card>
<Card title="LLC Owner Who Already Has an EIN" index={4}>A foreign LLC owner who has an [EIN](/ein-for-bank-account/) needs an ITIN only when a personal US tax return becomes due. The EIN handles the business. The ITIN handles the individual filing.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## How Do You Complete an <Accent>ITIN Application?</Accent>

You complete an ITIN application by filing Form W-7 with a federal tax return and identity documents. Mail the package to IRS ITIN Operation, PO Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342. The IRS issues the ITIN in 7-11 weeks and mails notice CP 565 to your address.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm You Need an ITIN">Verify you have a US tax filing requirement and cannot get an SSN. If you only need a business tax ID for an LLC, you need an [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) instead. Do not file Form W-7 without a qualifying reason.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form W-7">Enter your name exactly as it appears on your passport. Select the correct reason code that matches your situation. Add your foreign address and date of birth. Sign and date the form. An unsigned Form W-7 is rejected.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Prepare Your Tax Return">Attach the federal tax return that triggers the ITIN requirement, usually Form 1040 or Form 1040-NR. The IRS accepts a W-7 without a return only under specific exceptions. Most applicants must include the return.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Gather Identity Documents">Include a valid passport, the only standalone document the IRS accepts. Without a passport, submit 2 documents from the IRS-approved list, with at least one showing a photo. Originals or CAA-certified copies only.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Submit the Package">Mail the package to the Austin address, use a Certifying Acceptance Agent, or visit an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. A CAA verifies your passport so you do not mail the original document.</Step>
<Step step="6" title="Wait 7-11 Weeks">The IRS reviews the package and mails your ITIN assignment notice (CP 565). Peak season from January to April takes longer. There is no expedited ITIN option from the IRS.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Documents Does the IRS Accept for an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

The IRS accepts a valid passport as the only standalone document for an ITIN application. Without a passport, you submit 2 documents from the approved list, and at least one must show a photo. Photocopies are rejected unless certified by a Certifying Acceptance Agent.

| Document | Standalone? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Passport | Yes | Only single document the IRS accepts alone |
| National ID card | No | Must show name, photo, address, DOB |
| US driver's license | No | Pair with a second document |
| Foreign driver's license | No | Pair with a second document |
| US state ID card | No | Pair with a second document |
| Birth certificate | No | Required for dependents under 18 |
| Voter registration card | No | Pair with a second document |

Mailing your original passport to the IRS means weeks without your travel document. A Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) verifies the original passport in person and certifies a copy for the IRS, so you keep your passport. ein.so files ITIN applications and protects your documents during the process.

## Why Does the IRS Reject an <Accent>ITIN Application?</Accent>

The IRS rejects an ITIN application when the Form W-7 package is incomplete or inconsistent. The most common cause is a missing federal tax return. The IRS mails notice CP 567 or a similar letter explaining the issue. Fix the problem and resubmit with a corrected package.

| Reason | Fix |
| --- | --- |
| Missing tax return | Attach Form 1040 or Form 1040-NR |
| Wrong reason code | Select the code that matches your situation |
| Photocopies instead of originals | Send originals or use a CAA |
| Missing signature | Sign and date Form W-7 |
| Name mismatch | Use the exact name from your passport |
| Expired identity document | Submit a current, valid passport or ID |

A rejected ITIN delays your tax return and any refund tied to it. Each resubmission restarts the 7-11 week clock. Accurate document preparation on the first attempt is the fastest path. See [how to get an ITIN](/how-to-get-itin/) for the full document checklist.

## Should You Get an ITIN or an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Get an EIN for a business and an ITIN for an individual. A foreign-owned US LLC needs an EIN to open a bank account, accept payments, and file business returns. You apply for an ITIN only when a personal US tax return becomes due. Most non-resident LLC owners need the EIN first.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Forming a US LLC" index={1}>Get an EIN. You do not need an ITIN to form an LLC or to get an EIN. Non-residents enter a passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. See [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/).</Card>
<Card title="Opening a US Bank Account" index={2}>Get an EIN. Banks like Mercury and Relay require the LLC's EIN, not a personal ITIN, to open a business account. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Filing a Personal US Tax Return" index={3}>Get an ITIN. When you must file Form 1040-NR for US-source personal income, the ITIN is your filing ID. The EIN does not replace it on a personal return.</Card>
<Card title="Annual LLC Compliance" index={4}>Use the EIN. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. The EIN, not the ITIN, identifies the LLC.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The fastest path for a non-resident business owner is the EIN. ein.so files Form SS-4 by fax to the IRS at 855-215-1627 and delivers your EIN by email.
