## Is a North Dakota EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A North Dakota EIN and a federal EIN are the same thing. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN that works in all 50 states. North Dakota does not assign its own EIN. The number you get applies nationwide.

People often assume each state issues its own EIN. It does not. The EIN is purely federal. What North Dakota does issue is a separate state tax registration through the Office of State Tax Commissioner. These two identifiers serve different purposes.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payments |
| ND state tax ID | Office of State Tax Commissioner | North Dakota sales and withholding tax |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. A North Dakota business may need both, depending on whether it has employees, makes taxable sales, or collects sales tax. Confirm your specific state obligations with a CPA. The federal EIN comes first. See the [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full picture.

## Can a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>North Dakota LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident can form a North Dakota LLC and get an EIN without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627.

This is the single most important fact for international founders. The IRS does not require US residency, a visa, or a Social Security Number to assign an EIN. The barrier is technical: the online application validates an SSN, which non-residents lack. The fax route bypasses that requirement entirely.

## How Do You Apply for an EIN in <Accent>North Dakota?</Accent>

You apply for an EIN in North Dakota through the IRS using one of four methods. The process is federal and identical to every other state. Non-residents use the fax method to 855-215-1627 because the online tool requires an SSN.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | International applicants | $0 | No |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | About 4 weeks | $0 | No |

Each method submits the same Form SS-4. The difference is speed and whether an SSN is required. For a complete walkthrough, see [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) and the [EIN processing time guide](/ein-processing-time/).

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>North Dakota LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 with your North Dakota LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. The passport number replaces the SSN requirement for non-residents. Errors cause silent IRS rejection.

| SS-4 Field | Entry for a North Dakota LLC |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your North Dakota LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign or US mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, then number of members |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting that the IRS cannot parse. A malformed international address gets silently rejected, and you lose days waiting for a letter that never arrives. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style before faxing. Read the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for line-by-line detail.

## What North Dakota State Taxes Apply After You Get an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

North Dakota state taxes are separate from your federal EIN. North Dakota imposes a state income tax in the 0-2.5% range and administers it through the Office of State Tax Commissioner. Your EIN handles federal reporting, while a state tax registration handles North Dakota taxes.

The EIN does not register you for state taxes. If your North Dakota business has employees, makes taxable sales, or collects sales tax, you register separately with the Office of State Tax Commissioner. Many non-resident, single-member LLCs that only serve clients remotely have lighter state obligations, but this depends on your facts.

## What Federal Filings Does a North Dakota LLC Owe After the <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member North Dakota LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Most LLCs also file a BOI report with FinCEN. These are federal obligations, separate from North Dakota state filings.

The EIN is the start of federal compliance, not the end. Foreign-owned LLCs face information-reporting duties that carry steep penalties when missed. These filings are federal and apply regardless of which state you formed in.
