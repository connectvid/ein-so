<Capsule>**An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a 9-digit IRS tax ID in the format 9XX-XX-XXXX. The IRS issues it to individuals who must file or appear on a US tax return but cannot get a Social Security Number.** The IRS issues roughly 3 million ITINs each year. People use an ITIN to file federal returns, claim tax treaty benefits, and open some bank accounts. An ITIN does NOT grant work authorization. An ITIN does NOT change immigration status.</Capsule>

An ITIN solves one problem: it gives the IRS a way to process a tax return for a person who has no SSN. The IRS created the ITIN in 1996 for exactly this reason. The number always starts with the digit 9. The middle digits fall in IRS-assigned ranges. You apply with Form W-7, and the IRS reviews your identity documents before assigning the number.

Many people confuse the ITIN with the EIN. They are different IRS numbers for different purposes. An ITIN is for an individual. An [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) is for a business. If your goal is to run a US LLC, accept payments, or open a business bank account, you need an EIN, not an ITIN. Business owners rarely need both. This guide explains the ITIN in full, then shows where the EIN path replaces it.

## What Is an <Accent>ITIN Number?</Accent>

An ITIN is a 9-digit federal tax processing number in the format 9XX-XX-XXXX. The IRS issues it to individuals who have a US tax filing or reporting requirement but are not eligible for a Social Security Number. The number serves tax purposes only.

The IRS introduced the ITIN so it could collect tax and process returns from people the Social Security Administration cannot cover. Foreign nationals, non-resident investors, and dependents of US taxpayers all use ITINs. The number never carries any work or immigration meaning. It is a tax identifier and nothing more.

<InfoBox title="What an ITIN does and does not do">
<ul>
<li><strong>Does</strong> let you file a US federal tax return without an SSN</li>
<li><strong>Does</strong> let you claim eligible tax treaty benefits</li>
<li><strong>Does</strong> let you open accounts at some US banks</li>
<li><strong>Does not</strong> authorize you to work in the United States</li>
<li><strong>Does not</strong> change or improve your immigration status</li>
<li><strong>Does not</strong> qualify you for Social Security benefits</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

<Label>Who Needs One</Label>

## Who Needs an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

You need an ITIN if you have a US federal tax filing or reporting duty and you cannot get an SSN. The IRS will not process your return without a taxpayer ID, so the ITIN fills that gap. Business owners forming an LLC do not need an ITIN.

The most common ITIN applicants fall into clear groups. Each has a US tax touchpoint that triggers the requirement.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Non-Resident Aliens Filing a US Return" index={1}>A non-resident with US-sourced income files Form 1040-NR. The IRS requires a taxpayer ID on that return. Without an SSN, the filer attaches Form W-7 to get an ITIN.</Card>
<Card title="Treaty Benefit Claimants" index={2}>Foreign individuals claiming a reduced withholding rate under a tax treaty often need an ITIN to support the claim. Confirm your specific treaty position with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Spouses and Dependents" index={3}>A spouse or dependent of a US citizen or resident may need an ITIN to be listed on a joint or family tax return. The ITIN ties them to the filer's return.</Card>
<Card title="Foreign Investors and Property Owners" index={4}>A foreign person who earns rental income or sells US real estate reports that income to the IRS. An ITIN identifies them on the return and on withholding documents.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Notice what is missing from this list: forming a US company. A non-resident who wants a US LLC does not need an ITIN. The business uses an [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) instead. The owner provides a passport number, not an ITIN, when applying.

## How Do You Get an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

You get an ITIN by submitting Form W-7 with a federal tax return and proof of identity to the IRS. The IRS reviews your documents and assigns the number. Processing takes 7-11 weeks. There are 3 accepted submission methods.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Complete Form W-7">Fill out Form W-7 with your name, foreign address, country of citizenship, and the reason you need an ITIN. The reason code on the form must match your situation, or the IRS rejects the package.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Attach Your Federal Tax Return">In most cases you must attach the federal tax return that creates the filing requirement. The W-7 and the return travel together. A few exception categories let you file W-7 without a return.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Prove Your Identity">You submit original documents or certified copies. A valid passport is the single document that proves both identity and foreign status. The IRS does not accept plain photocopies.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Wait">Mail the package to the IRS ITIN Operation, Austin, TX 78714, use a Certifying Acceptance Agent, or visit an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. The IRS issues the ITIN in 7-11 weeks.</Step>
</Steps>

### Three Ways to Submit Form W-7

| Method | How It Works | Document Handling |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mail to IRS | Send W-7, return, and original or certified IDs to Austin, TX | Originals are mailed back after review |
| Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) | A CAA verifies your documents and submits for you | You keep your originals |
| IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center | In-person document review by appointment | You keep your originals |

## ITIN vs EIN: Which Number Do <Accent>You Actually Need?</Accent>

You need an EIN, not an ITIN, if your goal is to run a US business. An ITIN identifies a person; an EIN identifies a company. Most non-resident founders never need an ITIN because the EIN application accepts a passport number instead of an SSN or ITIN.

This is the single biggest source of confusion for non-residents. People assume they must get an ITIN before they can form a US LLC or open a US bank account. That assumption is wrong. The IRS issues an [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) when you file Form SS-4 by fax with your passport number on Line 7b.

## How Long Does an ITIN Take and What Does It <Accent>Cost?</Accent>

An ITIN takes 7-11 weeks to process after the IRS receives a complete Form W-7 package. The IRS charges $0 for the number itself. Peak tax season from January to April sits at the slow end of the range because volume rises.

The processing clock starts when the IRS opens your mail, not when you send it. International mail adds transit time on both ends. A CAA or an in-person appointment removes the risk of mailing original documents but does not shorten IRS review.

## When Does an ITIN <Accent>Expire?</Accent>

An ITIN expires if you do not use it on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years. The IRS also retires older ITINs on a rolling schedule based on the middle digits. You renew an expired ITIN by filing a fresh Form W-7.

An expired ITIN does not disappear from IRS records, but using it on a current return triggers processing delays and can hold up a refund. The IRS notifies affected taxpayers, though notices do not always reach foreign addresses on time. Check the status before filing season if you have not used the number recently.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Three-Year Non-Use Rule" index={1}>If an ITIN appears on no federal return for 3 straight years, the IRS deactivates it. You file a new Form W-7 to reactivate it before your next return.</Card>
<Card title="Rolling Retirement by Digits" index={2}>The IRS retires batches of older ITINs by middle digit. Holders of affected numbers renew with Form W-7. This schedule is separate from the non-use rule.</Card>
<Card title="Renewal Is a New W-7" index={3}>Renewal uses the same form and the same identity documents as a first application. There is no shortcut form. Plan for the same 7-11 week window.</Card>
<Card title="SSN Replaces ITIN" index={4}>If you obtain an SSN, the ITIN is rescinded. You notify the IRS so it merges your ITIN tax history into the SSN record. You then file under the SSN.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## How Does a US LLC Owner Get a Tax ID <Accent>Without an ITIN?</Accent>

A US LLC owner gets a tax ID by applying for an EIN with Form SS-4, using a passport number on Line 7b. No ITIN and no SSN are required. ein.so faxes the form to the IRS at 855-215-1627 and delivers the EIN by email in 4-7 business days.

This is the route almost every non-resident founder should take. The EIN, not the ITIN, unlocks US banking, payment processors, and platform sellers. After you have the EIN, your annual compliance shifts to the business return.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC through a registered agent. Common choices are Wyoming, Delaware, and New Mexico. You complete this entirely online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="File Form SS-4 for an EIN">Enter the LLC name on Line 1 and your passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method. ein.so prepares and faxes the form.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Open US Banking and Payments">Use the EIN to open a US business account and connect payment rails. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Step>
