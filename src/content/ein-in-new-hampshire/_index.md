## Is a New Hampshire EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A New Hampshire EIN is the same federal EIN issued by the IRS to businesses in all 50 states. New Hampshire does not issue a separate state version of an EIN. The number is federal, the format is the same, and it works nationwide.

Your federal EIN and any New Hampshire state registration serve different purposes. The EIN identifies your business to the IRS for federal tax filing, banking, and payment processors. A New Hampshire state tax registration, issued by the New Hampshire Department of Revenue Administration, applies to certain state-level activities. You may need both depending on what your business does in the state.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax, banking, Form SS-4 |
| NH state tax ID | NH Dept. of Revenue Administration | State-level business taxes |
| LLC registration | NH Secretary of State | Legal formation of the entity |

Your EIN does not replace state registration, and a state tax ID does not replace the EIN. Confirm whether your New Hampshire activities require state registration with a New Hampshire CPA, since rules change. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full federal process.

## Can a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a New Hampshire LLC <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Yes. Non-residents get an EIN for a New Hampshire LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b instead. The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method to 855-215-1627.

The fax route is the standard path for international founders forming a New Hampshire LLC. The IRS accepts a passport number as the responsible-party identification, which means you never need a US Social Security Number to receive an EIN. This is the single most common point of confusion, and it stops many non-residents who try the online tool and get blocked.

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an EIN <Accent>in New Hampshire?</Accent>

Non-residents apply for a New Hampshire EIN by forming a New Hampshire LLC, completing Form SS-4 with a passport number on Line 7b, and faxing it to the IRS at 855-215-1627. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your New Hampshire LLC">Register your LLC with the New Hampshire Secretary of State, or choose another state such as Wyoming or Delaware. A registered agent lets you form the entity entirely online from abroad. This creates the legal entity that the EIN attaches to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, your formation date, and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US residency is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Mark "Started new business" on Line 10. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax to the IRS and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Form SS-4 Field Guide for New Hampshire Non-Residents

| SS-4 Field | What to Enter |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your New Hampshire LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 8a (LLC) | Yes, with number of members |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting. International addresses do not map cleanly to the SS-4 fields, and the IRS rejects malformed entries. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [complete EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/).

| Method | Speed | Cost | Works for Non-Residents |

## What Are the Tax Rules for a New Hampshire LLC <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member New Hampshire LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. New Hampshire has no broad-based personal income tax on wages and no general state sales tax. Confirm specifics with a CPA.
