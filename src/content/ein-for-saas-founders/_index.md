## Do SaaS Founders <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

SaaS founders need an EIN when they run a US LLC or corporation, hire employees, or connect a payment processor. A sole proprietor with no employees can use an SSN, but an EIN keeps that SSN off contracts and W-9 forms. The EIN is free from the IRS.

A SaaS business touches money fast. The moment you sign up for Stripe, sign a vendor contract, or open a bank account, a tax ID is required. Using your EIN instead of your SSN protects your personal identity on every form a customer, processor, or vendor sees.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| SaaS LLC | Yes (required) |
| SaaS corporation | Yes (required) |
| SaaS with employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US founder with US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Solo founder, no entity, no employees | Recommended |
| Pre-launch, no entity formed | Not yet |

## How Does a SaaS Founder Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

A non-US SaaS founder applies for a US EIN by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register a US LLC (often Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico) or a Delaware C-corporation through a registered agent. This creates the legal entity the EIN attaches to. You complete formation online from any country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your entity name and formation state, and your mailing address. Non-residents need no SSN, no ITIN, and no US address for the responsible party.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. List "software" or "SaaS" as the principal activity on Line 16.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">Fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery runs 4-7 business days; Express runs 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, called the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

### SaaS Founder SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | SaaS Founder Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC or corporation name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home or registered agent address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as shown on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC or Corporation |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Software" or "SaaS" |

## What Taxes Does a Foreign-Owned <Accent>SaaS LLC Owe?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 with the IRS each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. The LLC is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, but tax treatment of SaaS revenue varies by structure.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + Pro-Forma 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for non-filing is $25,000. This is an information return reporting transactions between the LLC and its foreign owner.</Card>
<Card title="C-Corporation Tax" index={2}>A Delaware C-corporation files its own corporate return and pays US federal corporate tax on US income. Founders raising venture capital use this structure despite the extra filing. Confirm corporate tax exposure with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Sales Tax on SaaS" index={3}>Some US states tax SaaS subscriptions as taxable services. Nexus rules and rates vary by state. SaaS sales tax is complex and state-specific, so confirm your obligations with a CPA before you scale.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) so you meet the deadline and avoid penalties.</Card>

## What Does a SaaS Founder <Accent>Need to Get an EIN?</Accent>

A non-US SaaS founder needs three things to get an EIN: a US entity, a passport, and a mailing address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address for the responsible party is required. A US founder needs the same plus an SSN for the faster online route.

<InfoBox title="Checklist before you apply">
<ul>
<li>A formed US LLC or corporation with a name and formation state</li>
<li>A valid, unexpired passport (non-residents use this on Line 7b)</li>
<li>A mailing address for Line 4 (home or registered agent address)</li>
<li>Your principal business activity ("software" or "SaaS") for Line 16</li>
<li>Your full legal name as it appears on your passport for Line 7a</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

You do not need revenue, customers, or a US bank account before applying for the EIN. In fact, the EIN comes first: you need it to open the bank account and activate Stripe.
