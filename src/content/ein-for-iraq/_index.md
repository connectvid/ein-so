## How Do Iraqi Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Iraqi citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Iraq passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Iraqi applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Iraqi founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Iraq.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Iraq passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Iraq address. No Iraqi Tax Identification Number or local registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Iraq address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Iraq passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Iraqi Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Iraqi resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The foreign-owned LLC must still file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.
