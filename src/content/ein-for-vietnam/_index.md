## How Do Vietnamese Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Vietnamese citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Vietnam passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Vietnamese applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Vietnamese founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Vietnam.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Vietnam passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Vietnam address. No Vietnam Tax Code or business registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Vietnam address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Vietnam passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by fax, and ein.so emails it to you.</Step>
</Steps>

### Vietnam-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Vietnamese Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |

| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Vietnam passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |

## What Should Vietnamese Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Vietnamese resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. The LLC owner files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. Filing the report directly at fincen.gov/boi costs $0.</Card>
<Card title="Vietnam Reporting" index={3}>Vietnamese residents may have reporting duties for foreign company interests and foreign accounts. Confirm your obligations for a foreign-held US LLC with a Vietnamese tax professional.</Card>
<Card title="Treaty and Withholding" index={4}>The US and Vietnam have an income tax treaty framework. Treaty effects on dividends, interest, and royalties depend on facts. Confirm withholding and double-taxation relief with a CPA.</Card>
