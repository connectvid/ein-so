## Do eBay Sellers <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

eBay sellers need an EIN if they operate as an LLC, corporation, partnership, or hire employees. Sole proprietors can legally use an SSN, but an EIN protects that SSN on W-9 forms and enables business banking. Non-US sellers with a US LLC always need an EIN.

The table below maps common eBay seller situations to EIN requirements. Confirm edge cases with a CPA.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| eBay seller with an LLC | Yes (required) |
| eBay seller with a corporation | Yes (required) |
| eBay sole proprietor | Recommended, not always required |
| eBay seller with employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US seller with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Occasional hobby seller, no business | No |

## How Does eBay 1099-K Reporting Affect <Accent>Sellers With an EIN?</Accent>

eBay issues a Form 1099-K to sellers who cross the IRS reporting threshold for the calendar year. The 1099-K reports gross payment volume, not profit. eBay sends a copy to the seller and to the IRS, so the numbers must match your tax return.

The IRS 1099-K threshold has shifted across recent tax years. Confirm the current dollar amount with a CPA before you file, because eBay follows the active IRS rule each year. The form reports total gross sales, so you subtract eBay fees, shipping costs, refunds, and cost of goods on your return to arrive at taxable profit.

<InfoBox title="What the eBay 1099-K reports">
<ul>
<li>Gross payment volume processed through eBay for the year</li>
<li>Your business name and tax ID (EIN or SSN)</li>
<li>A month-by-month breakdown of transactions</li>
<li>The same totals sent to the IRS for matching</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

An EIN on file with eBay means the 1099-K lists your business tax ID rather than your SSN. This keeps your personal identifier off marketplace records. Keep detailed records of fees and costs, because the 1099-K never reflects your actual net income.

## How Do eBay Sellers <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

eBay sellers get an EIN through one of three methods, depending on whether they have a US SSN. US residents apply free online at irs.gov for an instant EIN. Non-US sellers fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627, which takes 4-7 business days.

The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN or ITIN. Non-US sellers without either use the fax method. ein.so prepares and faxes Form SS-4 so the application is not rejected for formatting errors.

### EIN Application Steps for Non-US Sellers

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business entity to attach the EIN to. You complete this online from your home country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name and formation state, and your home address. No SSN or ITIN is required. See [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/).</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. List "e-commerce" or "online retail" as the activity. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days. Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## Which Business Structure Should <Accent>eBay Sellers Choose?</Accent>

eBay sellers choose between a sole proprietorship and an LLC. A sole proprietorship needs no formation and uses your SSN, but offers no liability protection. An LLC separates business and personal assets and is required for non-US sellers who want a US entity.

A sole proprietor can request an EIN to protect an SSN, but the business and the owner remain legally the same. An LLC creates a separate legal entity, which protects personal assets if the business faces a lawsuit or debt.

| Feature | Sole Proprietor | LLC |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Formation cost | $0 | State filing fee (varies) |
| Liability protection | None | Yes |
| Business bank account | Optional | Required by banks |
| Tax ID on W-9 | SSN or EIN | EIN |
| Best for | US hobby-to-small sellers | Growing or non-US sellers |

Most non-US sellers form an LLC because eBay payouts, US banking, and supplier accounts all expect a US entity. See [EIN for an LLC](/ein-for-llc/) and [EIN for a sole proprietor](/ein-for-sole-proprietor/) for structure-specific guidance.

## What Should eBay Sellers Know About <Accent>Taxes and Compliance?</Accent>

eBay sellers report gross sales from the 1099-K, then deduct fees, shipping, refunds, and cost of goods to reach taxable profit. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.

eBay reports gross payment volume, not net income. Keep records of every fee and expense so your taxable profit is accurate. Tax rules vary by structure and country, so confirm your specific obligations with a CPA.
