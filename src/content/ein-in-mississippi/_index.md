## Is a Mississippi EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Mississippi EIN is not a separate thing — the EIN is one federal number issued by the IRS and used in all 50 states. Mississippi does not issue its own EIN. The same 9-digit number works for your federal tax filing, banking, and payroll nationwide.

Many new business owners confuse the federal EIN with Mississippi state tax registration. They are two different numbers from two different agencies. You may need both, depending on what your business does in the state.

| Number | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form 5472 |
| Mississippi state tax ID | Department of Revenue | State sales tax, state withholding |

Your federal EIN does not replace Mississippi state tax registration. If you sell taxable goods or hire employees in Mississippi, register with the [Mississippi Department of Revenue](/state-tax-id/) separately. The EIN comes from the IRS through [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/), and the process is identical in every state.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Mississippi LLC?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN for a Mississippi LLC by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is required. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

The IRS online EIN tool requires the responsible party to have an SSN or ITIN. Non-residents without an SSN cannot use it. The fax method is the path for international applicants, and it is the method [ein.so](/apply/) handles end to end.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Mississippi LLC">Register your LLC with the Mississippi Secretary of State through a registered agent with a Mississippi address. You complete this online from any country. The state filing creates the legal entity that your EIN attaches to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to file Form SS-4.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent IRS rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by fax.</Step>
</Steps>

### Mississippi SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Mississippi LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home-country address |

## What Are the Ways to Get an EIN in <Accent>Mississippi?</Accent>

There are four ways to get an EIN in Mississippi, and all four go through the IRS. The method you use depends on whether you have an SSN. Non-residents without an SSN use fax.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Online (irs.gov) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| Fax (855-215-1627) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | No |
| Phone (international) | Same call | $0 | No |
| Mail | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The fax method works for everyone and does not require an SSN, which is why it is the standard route for non-residents. The phone option (800-829-4933) is restricted to international applicants and means long-distance calls during US business hours. Mail is the slowest path at 4-6 weeks.

## What Federal Tax Filings Does a Foreign-Owned <Accent>Mississippi LLC Have?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Mississippi LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 with the IRS each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not automatically a tax bill. State filings are separate and handled with the Mississippi Department of Revenue.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. Your EIN identifies the LLC on these returns.</Card>
<Card title="Disregarded Entity Treatment" index={2}>A single-member US LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. The LLC itself often pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. Confirm your exact position with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Mississippi State Reporting" index={3}>A Mississippi LLC files an annual report with the Secretary of State and may owe state income tax (0-5%) and sales tax. State rules differ from federal rules. Confirm current brackets and deadlines with a Mississippi CPA.</Card>
