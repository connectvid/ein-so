## How Do Dominican Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Dominican citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Dominican passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Dominican applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Dominican founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC online from Dominican Republic.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Dominican passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Dominican address. No RNC or DGII registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Dominican address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Dominican passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) confirming your number.</Step>
</Steps>

## Is an EIN the Same as the Dominican <Accent>RNC?</Accent>

No. The EIN and the Dominican RNC are different tax IDs issued by different authorities for different countries. Your RNC identifies your business to the DGII in Dominican Republic. Your EIN identifies your US entity to the IRS. Neither replaces the other.

A Dominican entrepreneur running a US LLC holds both numbers at once. The RNC governs your local obligations. The EIN governs your US obligations, including banking, payment processing, and Form 5472.

| Attribute | RNC (Dominican Republic) | EIN (United States) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Issuer | DGII | IRS |
| Format | 9 digits | XX-XXXXXXX |
| Country of standing | Dominican Republic | United States |
| Used for | Local tax and invoicing | US banking, payments, US tax |
| Cost | DGII rules | $0 from the IRS |
| SSN required | No | No (fax method) |

If you only have an RNC, no US bank or US marketplace will accept it. You must apply for a separate EIN. Start your application on the [apply page](/apply/).
