## Does a Cleaning Business <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A cleaning business needs an EIN if it forms an LLC, forms a corporation, or hires any employees. A single-owner sole proprietor with no staff can use an SSN. The EIN separates business and personal tax identities and protects your SSN.

The table below shows when an EIN is required versus recommended for common cleaning business setups.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Cleaning business LLC | Yes (required) |
| Cleaning corporation | Yes (required) |
| Cleaning business with employees | Yes (required) |
| Sole proprietor, no employees | Recommended |
| Non-US resident with US cleaning LLC | Yes (required) |
| Hobby or one-time side income | No |

### Why Cleaning Businesses Get an EIN

1. **Commercial contracts** — Office buildings and property managers list your EIN on vendor agreements and W-9 forms.
2. **Payroll** — Hiring cleaners as W-2 employees requires an EIN for [employee payroll](/ein-for-hiring-employees/).
3. **Business banking** — Banks require an EIN to open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).
4. **Privacy** — The EIN keeps your SSN off invoices, W-9s, and contracts.
5. **Insurance** — General liability and bonding carriers reference your EIN on policies.

## How Does a Cleaning Business <Accent>Apply for an EIN?</Accent>

A US owner with an SSN applies online at irs.gov and receives an instant EIN. A non-US resident faxes Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627, entering a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS charges $0 and issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Choose Your Business Structure">Most cleaning companies form an LLC for liability protection. Sole proprietors can skip formation but get no liability shield. Confirm the best structure with a CPA. See [EIN for an LLC](/ein-for-llc/) and [EIN for a sole proprietor](/ein-for-sole-proprietor/).</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your LLC name, formation state, and formation date. US owners use an SSN. Non-residents use a valid passport number; no SSN or ITIN is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the cleaning LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN or passport number on Line 7b. List "cleaning services" as the principal activity on Line 16.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">US owners submit online for an instant EIN. Non-residents fax the SS-4. ein.so Standard delivers in 4-7 business days; Express delivers in 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Cleaning Business SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your cleaning LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full legal name |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | SSN (US owner) or passport number (non-resident) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |

## What Tax Filings Does a Cleaning Business <Accent>Have After the EIN?</Accent>

A US cleaning LLC reports profit on the owner's personal return by default, and a corporation files its own return. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, with a $25,000 penalty for not filing.

The EIN is the starting point for compliance, not the end. Payroll, sales tax in some states, and annual federal filings all reference it. The cards below summarize the main obligations; confirm specifics with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Income Tax" index={1}>A single-member cleaning LLC is a disregarded entity by default, so profit flows to the owner's return. A corporation files its own return. Confirm your treatment with a CPA before the first tax year ends.</Card>
<Card title="Payroll Taxes" index={2}>Cleaning companies with W-2 staff file Form 941 quarterly and Form 940 annually, all under the EIN. Each employee receives a W-2 listing the same EIN. See [EIN for hiring employees](/ein-for-hiring-employees/).</Card>
<Card title="Foreign-Owned LLC Filings" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US cleaning LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. This is an information return reporting owner transactions.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and [deadlines](/boi-filing-deadline/) to avoid penalties on your cleaning entity.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
