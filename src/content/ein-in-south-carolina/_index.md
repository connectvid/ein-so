## Is a South Carolina EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A South Carolina EIN is not a separate number. The EIN is a federal number issued by the IRS, and it is identical in every state. South Carolina does not issue its own EIN. Your business uses one 9-digit federal EIN nationwide.

South Carolina does issue its own state tax registration through the South Carolina Department of Revenue. This is separate from your federal EIN. The two serve different purposes and do not replace each other.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, payments |
| SC state tax ID | SC Department of Revenue | State sales tax, state withholding |

You may need both depending on your activities. A non-resident running an online business often needs only the federal EIN. A business hiring South Carolina employees or collecting SC sales tax also registers with the state. ## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>South Carolina?</Accent>

You get an EIN in South Carolina through the IRS, not the state. US persons with an SSN apply online at irs.gov for an instant EIN. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN required |

The process is identical regardless of state. The IRS charges $0 for the EIN itself. ein.so charges a service fee to prepare, file, and deliver the EIN for non-residents who cannot use the online tool. See [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for details.

3. [File your BOI report](/boi-filing/) with FinCEN (federal requirement for most LLCs)

## Can Non-Residents Get an EIN for a <Accent>South Carolina LLC?</Accent>

Yes. Non-US residents can get an EIN for a South Carolina LLC without an SSN, ITIN, or US visit. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and fax the form to 855-215-1627. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

Non-residents form a South Carolina LLC through the Secretary of State using a registered agent with a physical South Carolina address. The LLC gives you a legal US entity. The EIN attaches to that entity. You complete both steps entirely online from outside the United States.

## How Do You Complete Form SS-4 for a <Accent>South Carolina EIN?</Accent>

You complete Form SS-4 by entering your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. You then fax the form to 855-215-1627. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register your LLC with the South Carolina Secretary of State, or in another state, through a registered agent. This gives you a US business entity and address to attach the EIN to. You complete this online from any country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent IRS rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">Fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS issues your EIN assignment letter once the form is processed.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Non-Resident Applicants

| SS-4 Field | What to enter |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address (foreign address allowed) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (no SSN needed) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |

## What Tax Forms Does a South Carolina LLC <Accent>File?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill. Most LLCs also file a BOI report with FinCEN.
