## What Is the Difference Between a Georgia EIN and a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

There is no separate Georgia EIN. The EIN is a single federal number from the IRS, identical in Georgia and every other state. Georgia issues a separate state tax account through the Department of Revenue for state income and sales tax.

Many new owners assume Georgia hands out its own EIN. It does not. You receive one federal EIN that follows your business nationwide. Georgia layers its own state tax registration on top of that federal number when your activity triggers it.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, payroll, payment processors |
| Georgia state tax account | Georgia Department of Revenue | State income tax, sales tax, employer withholding |
| BOI report | FinCEN | Beneficial ownership disclosure (federal) |

You register for a Georgia state tax account when you hire employees, collect Georgia sales tax, or owe state income tax. The federal EIN does not replace that step. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) and the federal [EIN cost breakdown](/ein-cost/) for more detail.

## How Do You Get an EIN for a <Accent>Georgia Business?</Accent>

You get a Georgia EIN by filing IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN can apply online at irs.gov for an instant EIN. Non-residents file Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 using a passport number, since the online tool requires an SSN.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | $0 | Varies |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The process is identical regardless of which state your business sits in. Non-residents without an SSN use the fax method, which is the path ein.so handles end to end. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full walkthrough and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for timing details.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Georgia LLC or Corporation">Register your entity with the Georgia Secretary of State and appoint a Georgia registered agent. Confirm the current state filing fee with the Secretary of State. You can complete this online from anywhere, including outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your passport (for non-residents) or SSN (for US residents), your entity name, the formation state, and your business address. Non-residents need no SSN or ITIN for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Georgia entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN or passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent IRS rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Faxed to 855-215-1627, the IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575). Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days.</Step>
</Steps>

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Georgia LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident can form a Georgia LLC and get a federal EIN without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and fax the form to 855-215-1627. The IRS online tool is blocked without an SSN.

Georgia welcomes non-resident owners. You do not need to live in the US, hold a visa, or visit the country to form a Georgia LLC or get its EIN. You appoint a Georgia registered agent with a physical in-state address, file with the Secretary of State, and then complete the federal SS-4.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Georgia LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country or US business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting that does not match IRS fields. ein.so reformats international addresses to an IRS-accepted style so the application is not silently rejected.
