## Is an EIN in Idaho the Same as a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

An EIN in Idaho is identical to a federal EIN because the IRS issues every EIN. Idaho does not issue its own EIN. Your Idaho LLC uses the same 9-digit number that any US business uses. The number works nationwide.

The EIN is federal. Idaho state tax registration is separate. Many Idaho businesses need both. Your EIN identifies your business to the IRS for federal taxes. An Idaho permit from the Idaho State Tax Commission covers state sales tax or employee withholding when you have Idaho activity.

| Identifier | Issued by | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll |
| Idaho state tax permit | Idaho State Tax Commission | Idaho sales tax, withholding |
| Idaho LLC registration | Idaho Secretary of State | Legal formation of the entity |

Your EIN does not replace Idaho state registration. It does not replace your LLC filing with the Idaho Secretary of State. ## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for an <Accent>Idaho LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 for an Idaho LLC by entering the LLC name on Line 1, the address on Line 4, the responsible party on Line 7a, and the passport number on Line 7b. Non-residents leave the SSN field and use a passport instead. Errors cause silent IRS rejection.

| SS-4 Field | Entry for an Idaho LLC |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Idaho LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, then number of members |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for international applicants is address formatting. Foreign address formats do not map cleanly to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed entries without explanation. ein.so reformats addresses to an IRS-accepted style. ein.so does not give tax advice; confirm entity-type details with a CPA.
