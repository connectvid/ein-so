## Is a Washington EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Washington EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the IRS issues one 9-digit EIN that works in every state. Washington does not issue its own EIN. A Washington business uses the identical Form SS-4 process as a business in Texas, Florida, or any other state.

The confusion comes from two separate numbers. The federal EIN handles IRS filings. Washington state registration handles Washington taxes. You may need both.

| Number | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 |
| Washington state registration | Department of Revenue | State B&O tax, state filings |

Your EIN does not replace Washington state registration. The federal EIN is the starting point for the US financial system. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how the two interact, and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full federal process.

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an EIN <Accent>in Washington?</Accent>

Non-residents submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for non-residents. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Washington LLC">Register your LLC with the Washington Secretary of State through a registered agent. This gives you a US legal entity and a business address to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC online from outside the US. Confirm the current state filing fee on the official Washington website.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US documents are required for the EIN application itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent rejection. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by mail or via ein.so by email.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should You Know About <Accent>Washington and Federal Taxes?</Accent>

Washington has no state personal income tax, but it applies a Business and Occupation (B&O) tax on gross business receipts. This is separate from your federal EIN obligations. The B&O rate depends on your business classification, so confirm your classification and rate with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not automatically a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Washington B&O Tax" index={2}>Washington taxes gross receipts through the B&O tax rather than corporate income. Register with the Washington Department of Revenue and confirm your B&O classification with a CPA before your first filing.</Card>
<Card title="No State Income Tax" index={3}>Washington levies no state personal income tax. This does not remove your federal IRS obligations or your home-country reporting duties. Your EIN handles the federal side only.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and confirm current deadlines, since requirements have changed over time.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## Do You Need an EIN or an ITIN for a <Accent>Washington Business?</Accent>

Most non-resident business owners need an EIN, not an ITIN, to operate a Washington LLC. The EIN identifies the business to the IRS. An ITIN identifies an individual who has a US tax filing duty but no SSN. Some founders eventually need both.

| Number | Identifies | Issued by | ein.so price |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| EIN | The business entity | IRS (Form SS-4) | $49 / $97 |
| ITIN | An individual filer | IRS (Form W-7) | $197 / $297 |

You apply for the EIN first, because the bank account, payment processors, and Form 5472 all reference it. An ITIN becomes relevant only when you personally have a US filing obligation. Confirm with a CPA whether your situation requires an ITIN. ein.so offers an ITIN service at $197 (Standard) or $297 (Express) if you need one.
