## Which Business Types Actually Need an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Most business types need an EIN, and any foreign-owned US LLC needs one regardless of industry. A US business needs an EIN to hire employees, file federal taxes, open a bank account, or run payment processing. A foreign-owned single-member LLC needs an EIN to file Form 5472, even with no employees.

The list below groups the industries on this hub by their primary EIN driver. Every group still applies for the EIN the same way.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Online Sellers and Marketplaces" index={1}>Dropshipping, eBay, Fiverr, Upwork, and import-export businesses need an EIN to complete platform tax interviews and connect a US bank account. See [EIN for dropshipping](/ein-for-dropshipping/) and [EIN for eBay sellers](/ein-for-ebay-sellers/).</Card>
<Card title="Tech and Digital" index={2}>SaaS founders, web developers, graphic designers, podcasters, and crypto businesses use an EIN to bill in USD, integrate Stripe, and verify on platforms. See [EIN for SaaS founders](/ein-for-saas-founders/) and [EIN for crypto](/ein-for-crypto/).</Card>
<Card title="Trades and Local Services" index={3}>Construction, HVAC, plumbing, electrician, landscaping, and cleaning businesses need an EIN to hire crews, get licensed, and bill clients. See [EIN for construction](/ein-for-construction/) and [EIN for HVAC](/ein-for-hvac/).</Card>
<Card title="Licensed and Regulated" index={4}>Restaurants, law firms, medical and dental practices, daycares, and churches need an EIN for licensing, payroll, and regulatory filings. See [EIN for restaurants](/ein-for-restaurants/) and [EIN for medical practice](/ein-for-medical-practice/).</Card>
</NumberedCards>

<InfoBox title="When a sole proprietor can skip the EIN">
<ul>
<li>A US sole proprietor with no employees can sometimes use a Social Security Number instead of an EIN.</li>
<li>A foreign owner cannot rely on this; non-residents have no SSN and the [foreign-owned LLC](/ein-for-non-residents/) must file Form 5472.</li>
<li>Banks and processors increasingly require an EIN even for sole proprietors, so most owners get one anyway.</li>
<li>The EIN is free from the IRS, so there is no cost reason to avoid it.</li>
</ul>

## How Do You Apply for an EIN in Any <Accent>Industry?</Accent>

You apply by submitting IRS Form SS-4, and non-residents fax it to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days. The steps do not change by industry.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register your LLC or corporation first, because the EIN attaches to a named entity. Most non-residents form a Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico LLC online. The IRS needs your entity name and state for Form SS-4 Line 1.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your entity formation details, and your address. No SSN or ITIN is required. Decide your principal activity for Line 16 based on your main industry.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Enter your industry on Line 16. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

The single most common rejection cause for non-residents is malformed international address formatting, which the IRS rejects silently. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. Read the full [EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/) and the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) for the complete non-resident path.

## What Do Industries Do With the EIN <Accent>After Approval?</Accent>

After approval, every industry uses the EIN to open a US business bank account, connect payment processors, and meet federal filing duties. The EIN is the federal tax ID that links your entity to the US financial system. Foreign-owned LLCs add an annual Form 5472 filing on top of these steps.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Open a US Bank Account" index={1}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise accept non-resident LLC owners with an EIN and a passport. You receive US account and routing numbers for ACH and wires. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Connect Payment Processors" index={2}>Stripe, PayPal, and Payoneer link to your EIN so you can bill US customers and settle in USD. Online sellers and SaaS founders rely on this step to start collecting revenue.</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 Annually" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="File Your BOI Report" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. Confirm current deadlines, since FinCEN rules have changed.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Confirm your federal and state tax obligations with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters. Your EIN is the starting point for US compliance in every industry. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the full non-resident playbook.
