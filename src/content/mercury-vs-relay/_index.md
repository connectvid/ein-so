## Why Do Mercury and Relay Require an <Accent>EIN First?</Accent>

Mercury and Relay require an EIN because federal banking rules force every US business bank to verify a company's tax identity before opening an account. The EIN is your LLC's federal tax ID. Without it, neither bank can complete identity verification, so the application stops.

The EIN is a 9-digit number the IRS issues on Form SS-4. The IRS charges $0 for it. Non-residents do not need an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Line 7b instead. The catch is that the IRS online tool requires an SSN, which blocks most non-residents. The fax method solves this.

## What Tax Duties Come With a <Accent>US LLC Bank Account?</Accent>

Opening a Mercury or Relay account does not create a tax bill, but owning a foreign-held US LLC creates annual filing duties. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000.
