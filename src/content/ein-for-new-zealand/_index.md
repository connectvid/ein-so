## How Do New Zealand Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

New Zealand citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a New Zealand passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for Kiwi applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most New Zealand founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from New Zealand.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your New Zealand passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your New Zealand address. No IRD Number or NZBN is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your New Zealand address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your New Zealand passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### New Zealand-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | New Zealand Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your New Zealand address (street, suburb, city, postcode) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your New Zealand passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should New Zealand Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and New Zealand Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a New Zealand resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still file an annual US information return, and you still declare income to Inland Revenue in New Zealand.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="New Zealand Reporting" index={2}>New Zealand residents declare worldwide income to Inland Revenue. A foreign-held US LLC may create reporting duties at home. Confirm your exact obligations with a New Zealand accountant.</Card>
<Card title="Double Taxation Relief" index={3}>A US-New Zealand income tax treaty exists to allocate taxing rights and reduce double taxation. Treaty outcomes depend on income type and residency. Confirm any treaty position with a CPA before you rely on it.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is filed separately from the EIN.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## What Mistakes Do New Zealand Applicants Make on <Accent>Form SS-4?</Accent>

New Zealand applicants most often fail by leaving Line 7b blank, by entering an IRD Number on Line 7b, or by formatting the New Zealand address in a way the IRS rejects. Each error restarts the 4-7 day wait. ein.so reviews every field before faxing the SS-4.

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these SS-4 errors">
<ol>
<li>Leaving Line 7b blank. Enter your New Zealand passport number, not an SSN.</li>
<li>Entering your IRD Number or NZBN. These have no US standing and trigger rejection.</li>
<li>Using a New Zealand date format. The IRS expects month-day-year on the SS-4.</li>
<li>Naming the wrong responsible party. Line 7a must be the individual who controls the LLC.</li>
<li>Submitting an unsigned form. The SS-4 must be signed before faxing to 855-215-1627.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>
