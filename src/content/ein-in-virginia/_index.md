## Is a Virginia EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Virginia EIN and a federal EIN are the same number. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) that works in all 50 states. Virginia does not issue its own EIN. A Richmond business uses the identical federal EIN format as a business in any other state.

The confusion comes from a separate requirement. Virginia operates its own state tax system through the Virginia Department of Taxation. That registration produces a Virginia state tax account, which is not an EIN.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, hiring |
| Virginia state tax account | Virginia Department of Taxation | State sales tax, employer withholding |
| BOI report | FinCEN | Beneficial ownership disclosure |

Your EIN does not replace Virginia state tax registration. Many businesses need both. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for how the two interact. The EIN is the federal foundation; the state account layers on top for Virginia-specific taxes.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Virginia?</Accent>

You get a Virginia EIN by filing IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN apply online at irs.gov and receive the EIN instantly. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days. The process is identical in every state.

| Method | Speed | SSN Needed | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | No | $0 or $49 |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | Varies | $0 |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | No | $0 |

Non-residents cannot use the online tool because it requires an SSN. The fax method is the working path. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for full timelines.

## What Tax and Compliance Duties Follow a <Accent>Virginia EIN?</Accent>

A Virginia EIN triggers federal and state obligations. Foreign-owned single-member US LLCs file Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 with the IRS each year, with a $25,000 penalty for non-filing. Most LLCs also file a BOI report with FinCEN. Virginia adds its own state tax registration.

<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties.</Card>
<Card title="Virginia State Taxes" index={3}>Virginia levies a 2% to 5.75% state income tax and operates a separate Department of Taxation registration for sales and withholding taxes. Confirm which Virginia registrations apply with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Federal Income Tax" index={4}>A single-member LLC is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. Your specific liability depends on residency and income source. Confirm your filing position with a US CPA.</Card>
