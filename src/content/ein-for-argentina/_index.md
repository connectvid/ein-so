## How Do Argentine Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Argentine citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Argentina passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Argentine applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Argentine founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Argentina.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Argentina passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Argentina address. No CUIT or AFIP registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Argentina address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Argentina passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Should Argentine Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Argentine Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Argentine resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The LLC still has annual US information-reporting duties. Confirm your specific position with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Argentine Reporting" index={2}>Argentine residents declare worldwide income to AFIP. A foreign-held US LLC and its profits may create Argentine reporting and tax duties. Confirm your obligations with an Argentine contador.</Card>
<Card title="No US Presence Needed" index={3}>You file the EIN application and all US compliance forms remotely. The IRS does not require an Argentine owner to enter the United States to form the entity or maintain it.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand current deadlines and avoid penalties.</Card>
