## Is a Louisiana EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Louisiana EIN is the same federal EIN issued by the IRS to businesses in every state. The IRS assigns one 9-digit number per entity. Louisiana does not change, reissue, or duplicate it. Your federal EIN works nationwide.

Louisiana businesses often confuse the federal EIN with Louisiana state tax registration. They are two separate numbers from two separate authorities. The table below shows the difference.

| Number | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, banking, hiring, Form 5472 |
| Louisiana state tax ID | Louisiana Department of Revenue | State sales tax and state withholding |
| Entity registration | Louisiana Secretary of State | Forming and maintaining the LLC |

Your EIN does not replace Louisiana state tax registration. A Louisiana LLC that sells taxable goods registers with the Department of Revenue separately. See our [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) overview, and confirm your specific Louisiana registration duties with a Louisiana CPA. ein.so files federal EINs and does not handle state registration.

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Louisiana?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Louisiana through the IRS using one of four methods. US residents with an SSN use the online tool for instant issuance. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

The method depends on whether you hold a US Social Security Number. The process is identical in all 50 states because the EIN is federal.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) at irs.gov | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) to 855-215-1627 | 4-7 days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | $0 | International applicants only |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

Non-residents cannot use the online tool because it requires an SSN. The fax method to 855-215-1627 is the practical path. See our full [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) guide and our [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) breakdown for each option.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Louisiana LLC?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN for a Louisiana LLC by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is needed. The IRS issues a correctly filed EIN in 4-7 business days.

Many non-residents form a Louisiana LLC to run an online business, sell on US marketplaces, or invoice US clients in dollars. The EIN connects that LLC to the US financial system.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Louisiana LLC">Register your LLC with the Louisiana Secretary of State, usually through a registered agent who provides a Louisiana address. You can do this from outside the US. Confirm the current state filing fee on the official Secretary of State website.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to obtain the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Louisiana LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. See our [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">Fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Goes on Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Louisiana LLC?</Accent>

Form SS-4 for a Louisiana LLC lists the LLC name, the responsible party, and the reason for applying. Non-residents enter a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. Errors on Line 7b and on the address lines cause most IRS rejections.

The IRS reads each field literally. A malformed international address is silently rejected, which costs weeks. The table below shows the entries that matter most for a non-resident Louisiana applicant.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Louisiana LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (no SSN needed) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | Limited liability company |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Does a Foreign-Owned Louisiana LLC File <Accent>Each Year?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Louisiana LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 every year with the IRS. The penalty for failing to file Form 5472 is $25,000. This federal rule applies to a Louisiana LLC exactly as it applies in any other state.

The EIN is the starting point for this compliance, not the end. The numbered points below list the main federal obligations after you receive the EIN.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + Pro-Forma 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. It is an information return reporting transactions between the LLC and its foreign owner.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand who must file and the current deadlines. This is a federal filing, separate from the EIN.</Card>
<Card title="Louisiana State Registration" index={3}>If your LLC has Louisiana tax obligations, register with the Louisiana Department of Revenue. The federal EIN does not satisfy this state step. Confirm your duties with a Louisiana CPA.</Card>
<Card title="US Bank Account" index={4}>Use your EIN to open a US business account with Mercury or Relay so the LLC can receive USD and pay vendors. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
</NumberedCards>
