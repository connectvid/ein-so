<Capsule>**The IRS imposes a $25,000 penalty for each Form 5472 not filed, filed late, or filed incorrectly.** This penalty applies per form, per year and can accumulate rapidly. A foreign-owned LLC that fails to file for 3 years faces $75,000+ in penalties. Penalty abatement is possible for reasonable cause but requires a formal request.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Label>Penalties</Label>

## Form 5472 <Accent>Penalty Structure</Accent>

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to file | $25,000 per form per year |
| Filing late | $25,000 (same as non-filing) |
| Filing incorrectly | $25,000 |
| Failure to maintain records | $25,000 |
| Continued non-compliance | Additional $25,000 per 30-day period |

### How Penalties Accumulate
| Years Not Filed | Penalty |
|----------------|----------|
| 1 year | $25,000 |
| 2 years | $50,000 |
| 3 years | $75,000 |
| 5 years | $125,000 |
</Section>

<Label>Abatement</Label>

## Penalty <Accent>Abatement</Accent>

The IRS may abate Form 5472 penalties for 'reasonable cause.' Common grounds:

1. **Ignorance of filing requirement** — Didn't know about Form 5472 (first-time filers)
2. **Reliance on professional** — CPA or attorney failed to advise
3. **Medical emergency** — Serious illness preventing filing
4. **Natural disaster** — Events beyond your control

To request abatement: file a letter with the IRS explaining reasonable cause, include supporting documentation, and reference IRC Section 6038A.

**Tip:** File the overdue Form 5472 before requesting abatement. Showing compliance strengthens your case.

## How to Avoid <Accent>Penalties</Accent>

1. Get your [EIN](/apply/) as soon as your LLC is formed
2. File Form 5472 by April 15 each year (or request extension)
3. Keep records of ALL transactions between you and your LLC
4. Hire a US CPA experienced with foreign-owned LLCs
5. File even if your LLC had $0 in transactions
