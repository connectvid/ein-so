## Which Service Is Built for <Accent>Non-US Residents?</Accent>

ein.so is built specifically for non-US residents who have no SSN or ITIN, because it files Form SS-4 by fax using your passport number on Line 7b. Incfile and most formation companies orient their EIN handling toward applicants who can use the IRS online tool, which requires an SSN.

The IRS online EIN tool blocks anyone without an SSN. That single rule decides the path for most international founders. Non-residents must fax Form SS-4 to the IRS, and the responsible party enters a passport number rather than an SSN or ITIN. ein.so does this every day.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="You provide your details">Send your US LLC name, formation state, formation date, your full legal name as on your passport, and your passport number. No SSN and no ITIN are needed.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="ein.so prepares Form SS-4">ein.so enters your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="ein.so faxes the IRS">The completed SS-4 goes to the IRS EIN fax line at 855-215-1627. ein.so monitors the application until the IRS assigns the number.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="You receive your EIN by email">Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. You receive your EIN and the IRS assignment letter (CP 575) by email.</Step>
</Steps>

A non-resident who only needs an EIN should not pay for an LLC formation bundle to get one. ein.so files the EIN as a standalone product no matter where you formed your LLC. See [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the full method.

## What Do You Do After Getting Your EIN From <Accent>Either Service?</Accent>

After the IRS issues your EIN, you use it to open a US business bank account, connect payment processors, and meet annual federal filing duties. The EIN is the federal tax ID that links your foreign-owned US LLC to the US financial system, and the next steps are the same no matter who filed your SS-4.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Open a US bank account" index={1}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise accept non-resident LLC owners with an EIN and a passport. You receive US account and routing numbers. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="File your BOI report" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your status and avoid penalties.</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 annually" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. This is an information return, not a guaranteed tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Need an ITIN later?" index={4}>If you need a US individual tax ID for personal filing, ein.so files ITINs at $197 Standard and $297 Express. The EIN identifies the business; the ITIN identifies the individual.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters to confirm Form 5472 and other federal filings. Your EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance, not the whole picture.
