## How Do Nepalese Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Nepalese citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Nepal passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Nepalese founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Nepal without travel.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Nepal passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Nepal address. No PAN or Nepal registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Nepal address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Nepal passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by fax or mail.</Step>
</Steps>

## Can Nepalese Applicants Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Yes. Nepalese applicants get an EIN without an SSN or ITIN by writing "Foreign" in the SSN field and entering a Nepal passport number on Line 7b. The IRS assigns EINs to foreign responsible parties through the fax channel, not the online application.

The IRS online EIN assistant blocks anyone without an SSN or ITIN. This is the single reason Nepalese applicants cannot use the web tool. The fax method to 855-215-1627 has no such restriction. A foreign responsible party with a passport qualifies.

## What Should Nepalese Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Nepalese resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. The LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The owner still files a US information return each year, and Nepal reporting duties continue separately.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-Nepal Tax Treaty" index={2}>The US and Nepal do not have a comprehensive income tax treaty as of 2026. This affects withholding on US-sourced income. Confirm your exact position with a CPA experienced in non-resident matters.</Card>
<Card title="Nepal Reporting" index={3}>Nepalese residents report foreign income to the Inland Revenue Department under Nepal law. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Nepali tax advisor and a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
