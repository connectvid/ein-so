<Section>
<Capsule>**A VAT (Value Added Tax) Identification Number is a tax registration for businesses in over 170 countries that use consumption tax systems.** The EU, UK, India (GST), Australia (GST), and Canada (GST/HST) all use VAT-style taxes. The United States does not have a federal VAT — it uses state-level sales taxes and the EIN system instead.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>What Is VAT</Label>
## What Is a <Accent>VAT Number?</Accent>

| Detail | Information |
|--------|------------|
| Full Name | Value Added Tax Identification Number |
| Used In | 170+ countries (EU, UK, India, Australia, Canada) |
| Purpose | Consumption tax registration and collection |
| Format | Varies by country (EU: country code + 8-12 digits) |
| US Equivalent | None (US uses sales tax + EIN) |

### VAT Number Formats by Country
| Country | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| UK | GB + 9 digits | GB123456789 |
| Germany | DE + 9 digits | DE123456789 |
| France | FR + 11 chars | FR12345678901 |
| India (GST) | 15 digits | 22AAAAA0000A1Z5 |
| Australia (ABN) | 11 digits | 51 824 753 556 |

<Section>
<Label>US vs VAT</Label>
## US Tax System vs <Accent>VAT Countries</Accent>

The US is one of the few developed countries without a federal consumption tax (VAT). Instead:
- **Federal level:** Income tax, payroll tax (identified by EIN)
- **State level:** Sales tax (rates vary 0-10.25%)
- **No VAT:** US businesses do not register for VAT

If you are from a VAT country and form a US business, you need an [EIN](/what-is-ein/), not a VAT number. See [EIN vs VAT](/ein-vs-vat/).
</Section>

<Label>For Intl</Label>
## For International <Accent>Entrepreneurs</Accent>

If you operate in both a VAT country and the US, you need:
- A VAT number in your home country (for local sales)
- An EIN in the US (for US business operations)

These are separate registrations with separate authorities.
