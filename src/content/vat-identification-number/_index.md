## What Is a <Accent>VAT Identification Number?</Accent>

A VAT identification number is a unique code that a national tax authority assigns to a business so it can charge, collect, and reclaim Value Added Tax. The number proves the business is registered for VAT. It appears on invoices, customs forms, and tax returns across 170+ countries.

VAT is a consumption tax added at each stage of production and distribution. A registered business charges VAT on its sales (output VAT) and reclaims VAT on its purchases (input VAT). It remits the difference to the tax authority. The end consumer bears the final cost. This multi-stage design separates VAT from the single-stage US sales tax.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|------------|
| Full name | Value Added Tax Identification Number |
| Used in | 170+ countries (EU, UK, India, Australia, Canada) |
| Purpose | Charge, collect, and reclaim consumption tax |
| Format | Country code + 8-12 characters (varies) |
| US equivalent | None — US uses EIN + state sales tax |
| Cost | Usually free to register |

### VAT Number Formats by Country

| Country | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| United Kingdom | GB + 9 digits | GB123456789 |
| Germany | DE + 9 digits | DE123456789 |
| France | FR + 11 chars | FR12345678901 |
| India (GST) | 15 characters | 22AAAAA0000A1Z5 |
| Australia (ABN) | 11 digits | 51 824 753 556 |
| Canada (GST/HST) | 9 digits + RT | 123456789RT0001 |

A foreign VAT number has zero standing in the United States. US banks, payment processors, and the IRS do not recognize it. For US operations you need a US EIN. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) and [what an EIN is](/what-is-ein/).

## Why Does the US Use an EIN Instead of a <Accent>VAT Number?</Accent>

The US uses an EIN instead of a VAT number because the United States never adopted a federal Value Added Tax. The IRS identifies businesses with a 9-digit EIN for income tax, payroll, banking, and reporting. Consumption is taxed by states through sales tax, not by a national VAT.

The US tax structure splits across two levels. At the federal level, the IRS taxes business income and payroll, and it identifies every business by its EIN. At the state level, 45 states plus Washington, D.C. charge sales tax on final retail sales, with rates set independently by each state. There is no single national consumption tax and therefore no national VAT number.

## How Do Non-Residents Get a US EIN Instead of a <Accent>VAT Number?</Accent>

Non-residents get a US EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for non-residents. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in a state like Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US legal entity and address to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from abroad.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name, formation state, and formation date, plus your home address. No VAT number, SIREN, or local registration is needed for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your home address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax to the IRS and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Tax Filings Replace VAT Returns for a <Accent>US LLC?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, not VAT returns. The penalty for late or missing Form 5472 is $25,000. These are US information filings tied to your EIN and have no connection to your home-country VAT obligations.

A US LLC owned by a foreign person does not file VAT returns because the US has no VAT. Instead, it has US federal filing duties driven by the EIN. Your home-country VAT returns stay entirely separate and continue under local rules. Confirm both sets of obligations with a US CPA and a local tax adviser, since ein.so files the EIN and does not provide tax advice.
