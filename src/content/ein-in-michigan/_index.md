## Is a Michigan EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Michigan EIN is identical to a federal EIN because the IRS issues every EIN at the federal level. The same 9-digit number works in all 50 states. Michigan does not issue its own EIN. Your state tax registration is separate.

The EIN and the Michigan state tax registration serve two different governments. Confusing them is the most common mistake new owners make. The table below shows the split.

| Item | Federal EIN | Michigan State Tax Registration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Issued by | IRS | Michigan Department of Treasury |
| Purpose | Federal tax, banking, hiring | Sales tax, withholding, state filings |
| Form used | Form SS-4 | Michigan Treasury registration |
| Cost | $0 (IRS) | Confirm on michigan.gov |
| Required for non-residents | Yes | Depends on Michigan activity |

Your federal EIN does not replace Michigan state registration. A retail or product business selling to Michigan customers usually registers for state sales tax separately. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how the two numbers work together.

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Michigan?</Accent>

You get a Michigan EIN by filing IRS Form SS-4. US residents with an SSN can apply online at irs.gov for instant assignment. Non-residents file by fax to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS issues most correct EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Michigan LLC">Register your LLC with the Michigan Secretary of State (LARA) before applying. You need the exact legal name and formation details for Form SS-4. Confirm the current filing fee on michigan.gov.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your Michigan LLC name, the formation date, and your foreign or US address. No SSN or ITIN is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the Michigan LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>

## How Do Non-Residents Get a Michigan EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Non-residents get a Michigan EIN by faxing Form SS-4 with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS does not require an SSN or ITIN for foreign responsible parties. The online tool blocks applicants without an SSN, so fax is the path. ein.so handles the full filing.

The fax method works for every non-resident regardless of country. The table below maps the key SS-4 fields for a foreign-owned Michigan LLC.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Michigan LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

## What Tax and Compliance Steps Follow a <Accent>Michigan EIN?</Accent>

After getting a Michigan EIN, a foreign-owned single-member LLC must file Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. You also register with the Michigan Department of Treasury and file a BOI report with FinCEN.

These obligations split across federal and Michigan state authorities. Track each one to avoid penalties.

<Card title="Federal: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for non-filing is $25,000. This is an information return tied to your EIN, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Federal: BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Filing is free at fincen.gov/boi. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties.</Card>
