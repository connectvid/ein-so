## Is a Texas EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Texas EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the EIN is a federal number issued only by the IRS. Texas does not issue its own EIN. Your 9-digit EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) works in all 50 states and never changes when you operate across state lines.

Many founders confuse the federal EIN with Texas state tax registration. They are two separate numbers from two separate authorities.

| Number | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form 5472 |
| Texas state tax registration | Comptroller of Public Accounts | Sales tax, franchise tax |

Your EIN does not replace state registration. A Texas business may need both depending on its activities. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how state registration differs from the federal EIN. The federal EIN application is identical whether your LLC is in Texas, Florida, or Wyoming.

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Texas LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 for a Texas LLC by entering the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Non-residents leave the SSN field unused and enter the passport number instead.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Texas LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country or US mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, with member count |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting. International addresses that do not map to the SS-4 layout get silently rejected by the IRS. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style so your application is not bounced.
