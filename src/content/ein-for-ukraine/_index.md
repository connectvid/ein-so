## How Do Ukrainian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Ukrainian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Ukraine passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Ukrainian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Ukraine.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Ukraine passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Ukraine address. No EDRPOU or Ukrainian registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Ukraine address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Ukraine passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

## How Do Ukrainians Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Ukrainians get an EIN without an SSN by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS does not require an SSN or ITIN to assign an EIN to a foreign responsible party. Only the online tool is blocked.

The IRS offers three filing channels, but two of them are closed or impractical for Ukrainian applicants. The online application demands an SSN or ITIN before it proceeds. The phone line at 267-941-1099 reaches a US-hours desk with long international hold times and language barriers. The fax method works for every non-resident with a valid passport.

## What Should Ukrainian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes and Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Ukrainian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. Foreign-owned LLCs still carry mandatory annual information filings with a $25,000 penalty for non-compliance.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties. The BOI report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Ukrainian Reporting" index={3}>Ukrainian residents may need to report foreign company interests and foreign accounts to the State Tax Service. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Ukrainian accountant.</Card>
<Card title="EIN Is the Starting Point" index={4}>Your EIN is the federal tax ID that makes US filing possible. Without it, you cannot file Form 5472 or open the bank account that records your US transactions. See [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for timelines.</Card>
