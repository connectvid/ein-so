## What Is the IRS Fax Number for an <Accent>EIN (Form SS-4)?</Accent>

The IRS fax number for an EIN application is 855-215-1627. This single line accepts [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) from every applicant worldwide. The IRS EIN Operation center in Cincinnati, Ohio processes faxed applications within 4-7 business days and faxes the EIN back to your return number.

This number is dedicated to EIN applications. Do not send other tax forms to it. The IRS routes each form type to a separate fax line, listed in the directory below.

### Complete IRS Fax Directory

| Form | Purpose | Fax Number |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Form SS-4** | **EIN Application** | **855-215-1627** |
| Form 2553 | S-Corp Election | 855-214-7520 |
| Form 2848 | Power of Attorney | 855-214-7519 |
| Form 8822-B | Change of Address | 855-214-7520 |
| Form 8832 | Entity Classification | 855-214-7520 |

For EIN-specific details, see [IRS fax number for EIN](/irs-fax-number-for-ein/). For the full fax workflow, see [EIN by fax](/ein-by-fax/). Confirm any form-specific fax number against the current IRS instructions before sending, since the IRS updates fax routing periodically.

### Tips for Faxing the IRS

1. Fax during US business hours for fastest pickup
2. Include a cover page with your return fax number
3. Sign Form SS-4 on Line 7; an unsigned form gets rejected
4. Keep your transmission confirmation as proof of sending
5. Fax the form once; sending twice creates duplicate EINs
6. Use a reliable fax service or [ein.so](/apply/) for $49

## How Do You Fax Form SS-4 to the <Accent>IRS Fax Number?</Accent>

Fax your completed and signed Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with a cover page that lists your return fax number. The IRS reads the form, assigns an EIN, and faxes the assignment back within 4-7 business days. Non-residents enter a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC or entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Sign and date Line 7. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for line-by-line help.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Add a Cover Page">Create a one-page cover sheet with your name, the date, and the return fax number where you want the EIN delivered. The IRS faxes your EIN to this number, so confirm it is correct and active.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Fax to 855-215-1627">Send the cover page and the signed SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Send the form once. Keep the transmission confirmation as proof of the date and time you sent it.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Receive Your EIN">The IRS faxes back your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) within 4-7 business days. Save this letter; banks and payment processors request it. ein.so emails you a clean copy when filing for you.</Step>
</Steps>

## What If the IRS Does Not Fax Back Your <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

If 7 business days pass with no reply, the IRS likely rejected the fax for a missing signature, a bad address, or an unreadable transmission. Do not refax the same form, which risks duplicate EINs. Confirm the errors first, then resend a corrected SS-4.

<Alert type="warning" title="Common Reasons the IRS Rejects a Faxed SS-4">
<ol>
<li>Missing or unsigned Line 7 (the IRS requires a signature)</li>
<li>Malformed international address the IRS cannot parse</li>
<li>Wrong fax number, so the form never reached EIN Operation</li>
<li>Illegible scan or low-resolution PDF the operator cannot read</li>
<li>No return fax number on the cover page, so the IRS cannot reply</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

Most rejections are silent. The IRS files the form away and faxes back nothing, so a long wait with no answer usually means an error rather than a slow queue. Keep your transmission confirmation; it proves the send date if you need to follow up.

<InfoBox title="Before You Refax, Check These">
<ul>
<li>Was Line 7 signed and dated? An unsigned SS-4 is voided.</li>
<li>Did your cover page list a correct, active return fax number?</li>
<li>Was the passport number on Line 7b entered cleanly?</li>
<li>Did you wait the full 4-7 business days before assuming failure?</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>
