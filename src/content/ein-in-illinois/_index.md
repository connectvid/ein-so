## Is the Illinois EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. The Illinois EIN and the federal EIN are the same number. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN per business entity. Illinois does not issue its own EIN. Your Illinois LLC uses the identical federal EIN that any other US LLC uses.

Many founders confuse the federal EIN with Illinois state tax registration. These are two separate items. The EIN comes from the IRS for federal purposes. Illinois state registration comes from the Illinois Department of Revenue for state purposes.

| Item | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax ID, banking, hiring |
| Illinois state registration | Department of Revenue | State income tax, sales tax, payroll |

You get the federal EIN first. You may then register with the Illinois Department of Revenue depending on your activities. The EIN does not replace state registration. See our [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) page for the distinction.

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an Illinois <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Non-residents apply for an Illinois EIN by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is required. The IRS online tool needs an SSN, so most non-residents use the fax route. The IRS issues correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Illinois LLC">Register your LLC with the Illinois Secretary of State through a registered agent. This gives you a legal entity and a US business presence. You can complete the formation online from outside the United States. Confirm the current state filing fee on the official site.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation date, and your address. No SSN, ITIN, or US residency is required. Non-US persons use a passport number as identification for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Illinois LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent the errors that cause IRS rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, and the penalty for not filing is $25,000. The EIN is the starting point for this federal compliance. Illinois adds its own state registration and reporting on top of federal duties.
