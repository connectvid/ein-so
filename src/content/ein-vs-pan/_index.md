<Capsule>**An EIN is a 9-digit US federal tax ID issued by the IRS. A PAN is a 10-character alphanumeric ID issued by the Income Tax Department of India.** They serve different countries. US businesses need an EIN. India taxpayers need a PAN. Cross-border founders may need both.</Capsule>

India entrepreneurs expanding into the US market encounter both EINs and PANs. Understanding the difference between these two numbers is essential for operating legally in both jurisdictions. This guide compares both identifiers, explains when you need each, and shows how India business owners obtain a US EIN.

The core distinction is jurisdiction. The US Internal Revenue Service (IRS) issues the EIN for US federal tax purposes. The Income Tax Department of India issues the PAN for India tax purposes. Neither number is valid in the other country.

## What Is an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is a 9-digit tax ID issued by the IRS to businesses in the United States. The format is XX-XXXXXXX, for example 12-3456789. The IRS assigns it permanently once issued.

Every US business entity needs an EIN to file federal taxes, open a bank account, hire employees, and accept payments. The IRS charges $0 for an EIN. Non-residents pay [ein.so $49](/apply/) to handle the filing. For a full explanation, read [what is an EIN](/what-is-ein/).

<InfoBox title="Key EIN Facts">
<ul>
<li>**Issued by:** Internal Revenue Service (IRS)</li>
<li>**Format:** XX-XXXXXXX (9 digits)</li>
<li>**Cost:** $0 from the IRS / $49 through [ein.so](/apply/)</li>
<li>**Purpose:** US federal tax identification</li>
<li>**Application:** [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) by online, fax, phone, or mail</li>
<li>**Processing:** 4-7 business days by [fax](/ein-processing-time/)</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN. India residents do not have an SSN, so they file [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) by fax instead. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full process.

## What Is a <Accent>PAN?</Accent>

A PAN (Permanent Account Number) is a 10-character alphanumeric ID issued by the Income Tax Department of India. It uses the format AAAAA9999A: 5 letters, 4 digits, and 1 letter. The PAN is the primary income tax identifier for individuals and entities in India.

The Income Tax Department of India issues the PAN under Section 139A of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The fourth character signals the holder type. P marks an individual. C marks a company. H marks a Hindu Undivided Family. F marks a firm. India requires a PAN for many financial transactions, including bank accounts, property purchases, and large payments.

<InfoBox title="Key PAN Facts">
<ul>
<li>**Issued by:** Income Tax Department of India</li>
<li>**Format:** AAAAA9999A (5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter)</li>
<li>**Country:** India</li>
<li>**Purpose:** Income tax identification for individuals and entities</li>
<li>**Holder type:** Encoded in the fourth character (P, C, H, F, and others)</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

A PAN does not authorize US tax filing. It has no standing with the IRS. Confirm any India-specific PAN rules with a chartered accountant in India.

## How Does an EIN Compare to a <Accent>PAN Side by Side?</Accent>

An EIN identifies a business to the US government. A PAN identifies a taxpayer to the India government. They never overlap in jurisdiction. Cross-border founders hold both numbers at once without conflict.

| --- | --- | --- |

| Format | XX-XXXXXXX (9 digits) | AAAAA9999A (10 characters) |
| Who needs it | US business entities | India individuals and entities |
| Cost to obtain | $0 from the IRS | Set by India authorities |

| Application | [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) to the IRS | Form 49A or 49AA in India |
| SSN needed | No (passport on Line 7b) | No (India identity documents) |

### The Core Difference

An EIN links your company to the US financial system. A PAN links you to India's tax system. You apply to each authority separately. Holding a PAN does not speed up or replace your EIN application.

## How Do India Entrepreneurs <Accent>Get a US EIN?</Accent>

India entrepreneurs get a US EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627. No SSN is required. The India passport number goes on Line 7b. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register an LLC or corporation with a US state. Wyoming, Delaware, and New Mexico are common choices for non-residents. You complete this online from India.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your India address on Line 4, your full name on Line 7a, and your India passport number on Line 7b.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Submit by Fax">Fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. ein.so handles the filing for $49 Standard or $97 Express so you avoid international IRS phone calls.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Receive Your EIN">The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575). Standard delivery runs 4-7 business days. Express runs 2-3 business days. ein.so emails the letter to you.</Step>
</Steps>

### India-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | India Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your India address (street, city, PIN code) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your India passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

Your existing PAN has no bearing on the EIN application. The IRS does not ask for or verify foreign tax IDs. Your passport is sufficient identification. Read more in [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) and [how to get an EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/).

## Do India Founders Need <Accent>Both Numbers?</Accent>

India founders need both numbers only when they operate in both the US and India. A PAN covers India tax duties. An EIN covers US tax duties. A US LLC owned from India creates a US obligation that a PAN cannot satisfy.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="You Need an EIN When" index={1}>You formed a US LLC or corporation, you sell on Amazon.com or a US Shopify store, you need a US [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/), or you file US tax returns. The EIN is required first.</Card>
<Card title="You Need a PAN When" index={2}>You operate a business in India, you have India tax obligations, you employ workers in India, or you complete large financial transactions in India. The PAN is your India identifier.</Card>
<Card title="You Need Both When" index={3}>You run businesses in both countries, you are an India resident with a US LLC, or you earn income in both jurisdictions. Most India founders already hold a PAN and add an EIN when they form a US entity.</Card>
<Card title="They Coexist" index={4}>The two numbers never conflict. The IRS uses your EIN. The Income Tax Department of India uses your PAN. You report to each authority under its own rules. Confirm cross-border filing with a CPA.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## What US Filings Does an <Accent>India-Owned US LLC Face?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill. Your EIN is the starting point for these filings.

<Alert type="warning" title="Annual US Compliance for an India-Owned LLC">
<ol>
<li>**File [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year.** The non-filing penalty is $25,000.</li>
<li>**File your [BOI report](/boi-filing/)** with FinCEN as required for most LLCs.</li>
<li>**Track US and India deadlines separately.** The IRS and the Income Tax Department of India do not share filing calendars.</li>
<li>**Confirm specifics with a CPA.** ein.so files your EIN and does not provide tax advice.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

A single-member US LLC owned by an India resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. This means the LLC itself often pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. India residents still declare income under India rules. Work with a US CPA and an India chartered accountant for your exact position.
