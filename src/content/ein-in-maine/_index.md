## Is a Maine EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Maine EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a single federal number issued by the IRS. The same 9-digit EIN works in Maine, in every other state, and nationwide. Maine does not issue its own version of an EIN.

The confusion comes from two different identifiers. Your federal EIN comes from the IRS and covers federal tax filings. Your Maine state tax registration comes from Maine Revenue Services and covers state-level taxes such as sales tax and employer withholding. These are separate numbers from separate authorities. Your EIN does not replace state registration, and state registration does not replace your EIN.

| Identifier | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, Form SS-4 |
| Maine state tax ID | Maine Revenue Services | State sales tax, withholding, state filings |

Most businesses operating in Maine need both. You get the EIN first, then use it to register with Maine Revenue Services. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction and [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for a full price breakdown.

## Can Non-US Residents Get an EIN for a <Accent>Maine Business?</Accent>

Yes. Non-US residents get an EIN for a Maine LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and fax the form to the IRS at 855-215-1627. The IRS online tool is blocked without an SSN, so the fax method is your path.

Maine welcomes non-resident business owners the same way every state does, because EIN issuance is a federal function. You form a Maine LLC with the Maine Secretary of State through a registered agent, then apply for the federal EIN that attaches to that entity. You do not need to visit the United States.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Maine LLC">Register your LLC with the Maine Secretary of State through a registered agent with a Maine address. This creates the legal entity that the EIN attaches to. You complete this entirely online from abroad.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name, the formation state (Maine), and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US residency is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN confirmation letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

Foreign address layouts do not map cleanly to the SS-4 fields, and the IRS rejects malformed entries. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. ## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Maine LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 with your Maine LLC name, your address, your name as responsible party, and your passport number if you have no SSN. The form is one page. A single error in the address or name fields can delay the EIN by weeks.

| SS-4 Field | What to Enter |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Maine LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home or business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC details |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The responsible party on Line 7a must be a person, not a company. Non-residents leave the SSN blank and write the passport number instead. US residents with an SSN can skip the fax entirely and use the instant online tool. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [complete EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/).

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these SS-4 mistakes">
<ol>
<li>Malformed addresses that the IRS silently rejects.</li>
<li>Entering a company name on Line 7a instead of a person.</li>
<li>Leaving Line 7b fully blank when no SSN exists; use the passport number.</li>
<li>Selecting the wrong entity type on Line 9a.</li>
</ol>
