What ties an EIN to Alabama is your entity: you form an LLC with the Alabama Secretary of State, then attach a federal EIN to that company. ## Is an Alabama EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. An Alabama EIN is a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit number that works in all 50 states. Alabama does not assign its own EIN. Your federal EIN and your Alabama state tax registration are two separate things.

The confusion comes from the word "state." Alabama does register businesses for state-level taxes, but that registration is not an EIN. Keep the two systems distinct:

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form 5472 |
| Alabama state tax ID | Alabama Department of Revenue | Alabama sales tax, state withholding |

You apply for the EIN once through the IRS. You register separately with the Alabama Department of Revenue if your activities require it. Your EIN does not replace state registration, and state registration does not replace the EIN. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) and the broader [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) walkthrough. Confirm your exact Alabama registration duties with a CPA before you start trading.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Alabama?</Accent>

Apply through the IRS by online, fax, phone, or mail. Online is instant but requires an SSN. Fax to 855-215-1627 takes 4-7 business days. Non-residents without an SSN use the fax method, which ein.so files for $49.

The application method is identical in every state, because the EIN is federal. The right method depends on whether you have an SSN.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | $0 | Varies |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

US persons with an SSN use the [online application](/ein-online-application/) for an instant EIN. Non-US residents cannot use the online tool, because it validates an SSN or ITIN. They submit [Form SS-4 by fax](/ein-by-fax/) to 855-215-1627. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575) once it processes the form. See our [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) pages for the full breakdown.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Alabama LLC">Register your LLC with the Alabama Secretary of State and appoint a registered agent with an Alabama address. You receive formation documents and your official legal business name. The EIN attaches to this entity, so form the company first. Confirm the current Alabama filing fee on the state website.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name and formation date, and your address. Non-residents do not need an SSN or ITIN. US applicants use their SSN. The legal name on Line 1 must match your Alabama registration exactly.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b if you have no SSN. Pick "Started new business" as the reason on Line 10. ein.so completes the SS-4 to prevent silent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Fax the SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS issues your EIN and assignment letter, which you then use for banking and tax filing.</Step>
</Steps>

## Can a Non-Resident Get an EIN for an <Accent>Alabama LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for an Alabama LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS online tool blocks non-residents, so you submit the SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627.

The federal rules do not change by state. A founder in Lagos, Manila, or Berlin forming an Alabama LLC follows the exact same SS-4 process as one forming a Wyoming LLC. The only blocker is the online tool, which demands an SSN or ITIN it can validate. The fax method bypasses that requirement entirely.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Alabama LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign or US business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (no SSN) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The most common rejection cause is address formatting. International addresses do not map cleanly to the SS-4 fields, and the IRS rejects malformed entries without explanation. ein.so reformats addresses to an IRS-accepted style and files the fax for you. See our [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/), the [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) overview, and the detailed [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/). If you also need an ITIN for personal US tax filing, ein.so offers ITIN service at $197 Standard or $297 Express.

## What Do You Do After Getting an EIN <Accent>in Alabama?</Accent>

Open a US business bank account, register for Alabama state taxes if required, file your federal BOI report, and meet your annual federal filing duties. The EIN unlocks each of these steps. Most banks and processors accept a non-resident LLC with an EIN and passport.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Open a US Bank Account" index={1}>Use your EIN, formation documents, and passport to open a US business account. Mercury and Relay approve many non-resident LLC owners remotely. You receive US account and routing numbers for ACH and wires. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Register for Alabama State Taxes" index={2}>If you sell taxable goods or hire staff in Alabama, register with the Alabama Department of Revenue for sales tax or withholding. This is separate from your federal EIN. Confirm your exact obligations with a CPA before you trade.</Card>
<Card title="File Your BOI Report" index={3}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. The filing is free at fincen.gov/boi. Review the rules on our [BOI filing guide](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties and to confirm your deadline.</Card>
<Card title="Meet Annual Federal Filings" index={4}>A foreign-owned single-member LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. See our [Form 5472 guide](/form-5472-guide/) and confirm specifics with a US CPA.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
