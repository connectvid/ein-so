## Is a Wisconsin EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Wisconsin EIN is not a separate thing. The EIN is a single federal number issued only by the IRS. The same 9-digit EIN applies in Wisconsin, Texas, California, and every other state. Wisconsin never issues its own EIN.

Many business owners confuse the federal EIN with the Wisconsin state tax registration. These are two different identifiers from two different authorities. You use each one for a different purpose.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, Form 5472, Form 1120 |
| Wisconsin state tax ID | Wisconsin Department of Revenue | Wisconsin sales tax, state withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. A Wisconsin business that sells taxable goods or hires employees often needs both numbers. Get the federal EIN first, then register with the state if your activity requires it. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction, and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the federal process.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Wisconsin LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for a Wisconsin LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method instead.

Non-residents form Wisconsin LLCs to invoice US clients in dollars, sell on US marketplaces, and present a US business identity. The EIN connects that LLC to the US financial system. You never need to visit the United States to get it.

<Card title="Online Tool Is Blocked" index={2}>The IRS online EIN application requires an SSN or ITIN at the final step. Non-residents cannot complete it, which is why the fax route is the working path. ein.so faxes the form for you.</Card>

<Card title="One EIN Covers Wisconsin" index={4}>Because the EIN is federal, your single number serves the Wisconsin LLC nationwide. You do not file a second EIN application for any other state you expand into later.</Card>

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Wisconsin?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Wisconsin through the IRS using one of four methods. US residents with an SSN use the instant online tool. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 (DIY) / $49 (ein.so) | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same call | $0 | International only |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The process is identical regardless of state. The form, the IRS fax number, and the rules do not change for Wisconsin.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Wisconsin LLC">Register the LLC with the Wisconsin Department of Financial Institutions through a registered agent. Non-residents often choose Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico instead, but a Wisconsin LLC works the same way for the EIN. You complete formation online.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name and formation date, and your home address. No SSN, ITIN, or state ID is required to apply for the federal EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent rejections. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">Fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter in 4-7 business days. ein.so delivers it by email: $49 Standard or $97 Express (2-3 business days).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Do You File After Getting a Wisconsin <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

After getting a Wisconsin EIN, you complete federal compliance, register for state taxes if required, and file a BOI report. A foreign-owned single-member LLC also files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for missing Form 5472 is $25,000.

<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and deadlines to avoid penalties. Filing is federal and applies to Wisconsin LLCs.</Card>
