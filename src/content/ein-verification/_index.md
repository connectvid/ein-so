## How Do You Verify <Accent>an EIN?</Accent>

You verify an EIN by requesting IRS Letter 147C at 800-829-4933, checking your original CP 575 notice, or reading the EIN off a filed tax return. Each method confirms the same three facts: the EIN, the legal entity name, and the registered address.

The right method depends on whether you are verifying your own EIN or another company's. For your own EIN, the documents you already hold give instant proof. When you lost those documents, Letter 147C is your replacement path.

### Verify Your Own EIN

| Method | Timeline | Cost |
|--------|----------|------|

| [Letter 147C](/irs-letter-147c/) | 24-48 hrs fax / 10 days mail | $0 |
| Filed tax return | Instant | $0 |

The IRS does not operate a public EIN lookup for private companies. Privacy rules limit EIN disclosure to the business and its authorized representatives.

## What Is the Difference Between <Accent>Letter 147C and CP 575?</Accent>

Letter 147C is a replacement EIN verification letter you request any time, while CP 575 is the original assignment notice the IRS mails once when your EIN is first issued. Banks accept both. The IRS cannot re-issue a lost CP 575, so you request 147C instead.

Both documents confirm the same EIN, legal entity name, and address. The difference matters only when you lost the original. Keep your CP 575 in a safe place. If you cannot find it, Letter 147C replaces it for free and without limit.

## How Do You Request Letter 147C From the <Accent>IRS?</Accent>

You request Letter 147C by calling the IRS Business and Specialty Tax Line at 800-829-4933, verifying your identity as the responsible party, and choosing fax or mail delivery. Fax arrives in 24-48 hours; mail takes 10 business days. The IRS charges $0.

## Why Do Banks Verify Your <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

Banks verify your EIN to confirm your business exists on IRS records before they open a business account, as part of federal Know Your Customer and anti-money-laundering rules. Banks accept Letter 147C, CP 575, or a recent tax return as proof. Mismatched names cause rejection.

The most common rejection cause is a name mismatch between your EIN letter and your LLC formation documents. Your EIN legal name on the CP 575 must match your Articles of Organization exactly. A single different word triggers a hold.

## How Do You Verify Another Company's <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

You verify another company's EIN by requesting a Form W-9 from the business, searching SEC EDGAR for public companies, or using the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search for nonprofits. The IRS does not run a public EIN lookup for private companies because of taxpayer privacy rules.

Each method fits a different entity type. Choose the one that matches who you are verifying. A W-9 is the standard request when you must report payments to a vendor or contractor.

| Source | Best For | What It Confirms |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Form W-9 | Vendors, contractors | Legal name and EIN for 1099 reporting |
| SEC EDGAR | Public companies | EIN, filings, entity details |
| IRS TEOS | 501(c) nonprofits | EIN, exempt status, name |
| State SOS records | Registered entities | Legal name, status, registered agent |
| Commercial data services | Large enterprises | Cross-referenced business data |
