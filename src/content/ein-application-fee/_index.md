<Label>No Fee</Label>

## The IRS EIN Application Fee Is <Accent>$0</Accent>

The Internal Revenue Service does not charge any fee for EIN applications. This is stated clearly on the IRS website: "Applying for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) is a free service offered by the Internal Revenue Service."

This applies to all application methods:

| Method | Government Fee | Third-Party Fee | Total Cost (DIY) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Online | $0 | N/A | $0 |
| Fax | $0 | N/A | $0 |
| Phone | $0 | N/A | $0 |
| Mail | $0 | N/A | $0 |

### What About Filing Fees?

There is no "filing fee" for an EIN. Some websites use confusing language like "IRS filing fee" or "government processing fee" to justify their charges. These fees do not exist. The IRS processes EIN applications at no cost to the applicant.

For the complete breakdown, see [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) and [free EIN](/free-ein/).

## What Third-Party <Accent>Services Charge</Accent>

Third-party EIN services charge a service fee for labor — not for the EIN. This is an important distinction.

**What the service fee covers:**
- Form SS-4 completion (21 fields with IRS-specific requirements)
- Fax submission to IRS at 855-215-1627
- Follow-up with IRS if issues arise
- EIN delivery by email
- Customer support

**What the service fee does NOT cover:**
- The EIN itself (always free from IRS)
- Any government fee (none exists)
- Any IRS processing fee (none exists)
