## Is a New Mexico EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A New Mexico EIN is the same as a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit number that works in all 50 states. New Mexico does not assign its own EIN. The state issues a separate tax registration through the Taxation and Revenue Department for state-level obligations.

Your EIN and your New Mexico state tax registration serve different purposes. Keep them separate in your records.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payment processors |
| NM state tax ID | NM Taxation and Revenue Department | State gross receipts and withholding |

The EIN does not replace state registration. If your New Mexico LLC has state tax obligations, you may need both. See our [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) and the [EIN cost breakdown](/ein-cost/) for the full picture.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in New Mexico?</Accent>

You get an EIN in New Mexico by submitting IRS Form SS-4 through one of four methods. US residents with an SSN use the instant online tool. Non-residents without an SSN fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The process is identical to every other state.

| Method | Speed | Cost | SSN Required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | Yes |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 days | $0 / $49 | No |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same day | $0 | US callers |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | No |

The online tool at irs.gov is fastest but requires an SSN or ITIN. Non-residents must use fax. See the full [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) walkthrough and the [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) details.

### Steps to Apply as a Non-Resident

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your New Mexico LLC">Register your LLC with the New Mexico Secretary of State through a registered agent. New Mexico does not publish member names, which protects owner privacy. You complete this entirely online from anywhere.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC name and formation date, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required.</Step>

## Can You Get a New Mexico EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Yes. Non-residents get an EIN for a New Mexico LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b instead. The IRS online tool blocks applicants without an SSN, so you fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The IRS still issues the EIN.

This is the single biggest source of confusion for foreign founders. The online tool rejects you not because you are ineligible, but because it cannot verify a non-US identity. The fax path solves this.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your New Mexico LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your foreign passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is malformed international address formatting. The IRS silently rejects addresses that do not fit the SS-4 layout. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. New Mexico competes with Wyoming and Delaware as a non-resident formation state. Each pairs with the same federal EIN.

## What Tax and Compliance Steps Follow a <Accent>New Mexico EIN?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member New Mexico LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Most LLCs also file a federal BOI report. New Mexico has its own state tax registration and gross receipts rules, which are separate from federal filings.

<Alert type="warning" title="Foreign-Owned LLC Filing Duties">
<ol>
<li>File <strong>Form 5472</strong> with a pro-forma <strong>Form 1120</strong> every year — the penalty for not filing is <strong>$25,000</strong>.</li>
<li>File your <strong>BOI report</strong> with FinCEN — required for most LLCs, free at fincen.gov/boi.</li>
<li>Register with the <strong>New Mexico Taxation and Revenue Department</strong> if you have state tax obligations.</li>
<li>Confirm all tax specifics with a <strong>CPA</strong> — ein.so files your EIN and does not give tax advice.</li>
</ol>

<InfoBox title="New Mexico State-Level Notes">
<ul>
<li>New Mexico levies a <strong>gross receipts tax</strong> rather than a traditional sales tax — confirm current rates with the state.</li>
<li>State personal income tax in New Mexico is <strong>graduated</strong>, with rates that have ranged from roughly 1.7% to 5.9% — verify the current schedule with a CPA.</li>
<li>Your <strong>federal EIN</strong> handles federal reporting; a separate <strong>state registration</strong> handles New Mexico taxes.</li>
