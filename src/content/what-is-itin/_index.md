<Capsule>
**An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a 9-digit tax processing number the IRS issues to people who must have a US taxpayer ID but cannot get a Social Security Number.** ITINs begin with the digit 9 and use the format 9XX-XX-XXXX. The IRS issues them under IRC Section 6109 for federal tax reporting only — never for work authorization, Social Security benefits, or immigration status.
</Capsule>

The ITIN exists for one reason: the IRS needs a way to process tax returns and payments from individuals who have a US tax obligation but no SSN. Non-resident aliens, their dependents, and foreign investors with US income all fall into this group. The ITIN does not change anyone's immigration status. It does not grant the right to work. It is a tax administration tool and nothing more.

Many non-residents confuse the ITIN with the EIN. The two are not interchangeable. An ITIN identifies a person. An [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) identifies a business. If you are forming a US LLC, you need an EIN, not an ITIN. This guide explains what an ITIN is, who needs one, how it differs from an EIN, and how to apply.

## What an ITIN <Accent>Is and Isn't</Accent>

An ITIN is a federal tax processing number for individuals not eligible for an SSN. It lets the IRS track tax returns, withholding, and payments tied to a specific person. It serves no purpose outside the US tax system.

People often assume an ITIN does more than it does. It does not. The list below sets the boundaries clearly.

### An ITIN IS:
- A 9-digit tax processing number in the format 9XX-XX-XXXX
- Issued by the IRS under IRC Section 6109
- Valid for filing US federal tax returns such as Form 1040-NR
- Accepted by some banks to open a personal account
- Required to claim certain US tax treaty benefits

### An ITIN is NOT:
- A work permit or any form of work authorization
- A Social Security Number replacement
- Immigration documentation or proof of legal status
- Valid for Social Security or Medicare benefits
- A business tax ID — that role belongs to the [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/)

For direct comparisons, see [EIN vs ITIN](/ein-vs-itin/) and [ITIN vs SSN](/itin-vs-ssn/).

## What Is the Difference Between an <Accent>ITIN and an EIN?</Accent>

An ITIN identifies an individual person for tax purposes, while an EIN identifies a business entity. A non-resident forming a US LLC needs an EIN, not an ITIN. The two numbers solve different problems and follow different application paths.

This is the single most common point of confusion for non-resident founders. You do not need an ITIN to start a US business. You need an EIN. The EIN is the federal tax ID that lets your LLC open a bank account, accept payments, and file US returns.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="The ITIN Is for People" index={1}>An ITIN belongs to a human being. Non-resident aliens use it to file a personal Form 1040-NR or to be listed as a dependent. You apply with Form W-7 attached to a tax return. Processing runs 7-11 weeks.</Card>
<Card title="The EIN Is for Businesses" index={2}>An EIN belongs to an entity such as an LLC or corporation. You apply with Form SS-4. The IRS charges $0. Non-residents fax the form to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/).</Card>
<Card title="You Can Get an EIN Without an ITIN" index={3}>The fax method for Form SS-4 does not require an ITIN or an SSN. Your passport number is enough. See [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/). This is why most non-resident founders skip the ITIN entirely.</Card>
<Card title="When You Need Both" index={4}>You may need an EIN for your LLC and an ITIN for yourself if you must file a personal US return or claim treaty benefits as an individual. Confirm your exact filing duties with a CPA.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The bottom line: if your goal is a US LLC and a US bank account, start with an [EIN](/ein-for-non-residents/). The ITIN is a separate, slower, personal-level number that most LLC owners never need.

## Who Needs <Accent>an ITIN?</Accent>

A person needs an ITIN when they have a US federal tax obligation but cannot get a Social Security Number. This includes non-resident aliens filing US returns, certain dependents and spouses, and foreign investors with US-source income.

The IRS does not issue ITINs on request. You must show a tax reason for needing one, usually by attaching Form W-7 to a federal tax return. The categories below cover the common cases.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Non-Resident Aliens Filing US Returns" index={1}>A non-resident with US-source income files Form 1040-NR and needs an ITIN to do so. Examples include foreign landlords renting US property and investors earning US partnership income.</Card>
<Card title="Dependents and Spouses" index={2}>Dependents or spouses of US citizens, resident aliens, or non-resident aliens with US tax obligations need an ITIN to be claimed on a return when they lack an SSN.</Card>
<Card title="Treaty Benefit Claimants" index={3}>A non-resident claiming reduced withholding under a US tax treaty often needs an ITIN to file the supporting return. Confirm treaty eligibility with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Foreign Students and Scholars" index={4}>Foreign students with US scholarship or fellowship income that is taxable need an ITIN to report it correctly to the IRS.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

If you are a non-resident whose only goal is to run a US LLC, you usually do not fall into any of these categories. Your LLC needs an [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/), and you can apply for it directly. See [how to get an ITIN](/how-to-get-itin/) only if a personal US tax filing requires it.

## How Do You Apply for an <Accent>ITIN?</Accent>

You apply for an ITIN by filing IRS Form W-7 with a federal tax return and original identity documents or certified copies. Processing takes 7-11 weeks. Most non-residents use a Certifying Acceptance Agent so they do not mail their passport to the IRS.

The ITIN process is slower and more document-heavy than the EIN process. You cannot apply online, and there is no fax shortcut. The steps below outline the standard path.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm You Have a Tax Reason">The IRS requires a valid tax purpose for an ITIN. In most cases that means a federal tax return you must file. Without a tax reason, the IRS rejects the W-7.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form W-7">Enter your name, foreign address, country of citizenship, passport details, and the reason code for your application. Errors here cause the most rejections.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Gather Identity Documents">A valid passport is the single document the IRS accepts on its own. Otherwise you submit two documents proving identity and foreign status. Originals or certified copies are required.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit With Your Tax Return">Mail Form W-7, your tax return, and your documents to the IRS ITIN unit, or work through a Certifying Acceptance Agent who verifies your documents so you keep your passport.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Wait 7-11 Weeks">The IRS reviews the application and mails an assignment letter with your ITIN. Peak season extends the timeline.</Step>
</Steps>

## How Long Is an <Accent>ITIN Valid?</Accent>

An ITIN expires if it is not used on a federal tax return for 3 consecutive years. ITINs are not permanent. Once an ITIN lapses, the IRS requires a new Form W-7 to renew it before you can file again.

This is a key difference from an SSN, which never expires. The IRS uses the 3-year inactivity rule to retire ITINs that are no longer in use. If you file at least one return inside any 3-year window, your ITIN stays active.

<Alert type="warning" title="Keep your ITIN active">
<ol>
<li>File a federal tax return at least once every 3 years to prevent expiration.</li>
<li>Renew a lapsed ITIN with a fresh Form W-7 before your next filing.</li>
<li>An expired ITIN can delay your refund and reject treaty benefit claims.</li>
<li>Confirm your specific renewal timing with a CPA, since the IRS updates expiration schedules.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

An EIN, by contrast, never expires. Once the IRS assigns your LLC an EIN, it stays with that entity for life. This is one more reason non-resident founders prefer the EIN path. See [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for the EIN timeline.

## Do Non-Resident LLC Owners Need an <Accent>ITIN or an EIN?</Accent>

Non-resident LLC owners need an EIN, not an ITIN, to run a US business. The EIN is the business tax ID that opens bank accounts, connects payment processors, and files the annual returns a foreign-owned LLC must submit.

A US LLC is an entity. Entities use EINs. The ITIN is a personal number, and a typical non-resident founder has no personal US tax filing that requires one. Skipping the unnecessary ITIN saves you 7-11 weeks and the cost of a separate filing.

<InfoBox title="What a non-resident LLC owner actually needs">

<li>An <strong>EIN</strong> for the LLC — file [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) by fax to 855-215-1627. The IRS charges $0. ein.so files it for $49.</li>
<li>A <strong>US business bank account</strong> — opened with the EIN. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</li>
<li>A <strong>Form 5472 filing</strong> each year for a foreign-owned single-member LLC, with a pro-forma Form 1120. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. See [Form 5472 guide](/form-5472-guide/).</li>
<li>A <strong>BOI report</strong> to FinCEN for most LLCs. See [BOI filing](/boi-filing/).</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

## What Mistakes Do People Make With <Accent>ITINs and EINs?</Accent>

The most common mistake is applying for an ITIN when an EIN is what the situation requires. Non-resident founders waste 7-11 weeks and pay for a number their LLC cannot use, because business banking and tax filing require an EIN.

The confusion is understandable. Both numbers come from the IRS, both have 9 digits, and both are "tax IDs." But they are not substitutes. The table below maps the most frequent errors to the correct action.

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Correct Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Getting an ITIN to open a business bank account | Business accounts require an EIN | File [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) for an EIN |
| Using an ITIN as the LLC's tax ID | An LLC needs an entity ID, not a personal ID | Apply for an [EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) |
| Trying the IRS online EIN tool without an SSN | The online tool requires an SSN | Fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 |
| Assuming an ITIN allows US work | An ITIN carries no work authorization | Obtain an SSN or work permit separately |
| Letting an ITIN lapse for 3 years | The IRS expires unused ITINs | File a return or renew with Form W-7 |

If your goal is a US company, start with the [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) path. ein.so files your SS-4 and delivers the EIN by email. ein.so does not provide tax advice — confirm any personal ITIN filing with a CPA.
