## How Do Dutch Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Dutch citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Netherlands passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Dutch founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from the Netherlands.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Netherlands passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Netherlands address. No KVK number or Dutch registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Netherlands address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Netherlands passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Netherlands-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Dutch Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Netherlands address (straat, postcode, plaats) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Netherlands passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

## What Should Dutch Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Dutch Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Dutch resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. You still carry annual US information-reporting duties, and you continue to declare income in the Netherlands. Confirm every tax detail with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Dutch Reporting" index={2}>Dutch residents generally must declare foreign company interests and foreign income to the Belastingdienst. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Dutch belastingadviseur.</Card>
<Card title="Avoiding Double Tax" index={3}>The US and the Netherlands have a long-standing income tax relationship designed to prevent double taxation. Treaty positions are fact-specific. Do not assume a rate; confirm with a CPA who handles non-resident US filings.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to understand your reporting obligation after formation.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
