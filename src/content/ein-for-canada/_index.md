## What Tax and Currency Considerations Apply to <Accent>Canadian EIN Holders?</Accent>

Canada's federal corporate tax rate is 15% (plus 8-16% provincial), compared to the US federal rate of 21%. The Canada-US tax treaty eliminates double taxation for most cross-border business structures. Here are the key considerations for Canadian entrepreneurs with a US EIN:

### Treaty Benefits and CRA Reporting

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Canada-US Tax Treaty" index={1}>The Canada-US tax treaty prevents double taxation. Business profits earned through a US LLC are taxable in Canada (where you reside) unless you have a permanent establishment in the US. The treaty allows foreign tax credits to offset taxes paid in either country.</Card>
<Card title="CAD/USD Currency Management" index={2}>Operating in both CAD and USD means you need a currency strategy. A US bank account lets you hold USD without forced conversion. Consult a Canadian tax professional familiar with cross-border e-commerce.</Card>
<Card title="CRA Reporting Obligations" index={4}>Canadian residents must report worldwide income to the CRA, including income earned through US LLCs.
