## How Does the EIN Process Work for <Accent>Every Country?</Accent>

The EIN process works identically for every country: you fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with your passport number on Line 7b. The IRS does not require US residence, a visa, an SSN, or an ITIN. It issues correctly filed EINs in 4-7 business days.

The IRS treats a non-resident from India the same as a non-resident from Germany or Nigeria. One form. One fax line. One review process. The only differences are local, and they are small.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You complete this online from any country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, and your home address. No local company registration number is required for the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days. Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the CP 575 assignment letter.</Step>
</Steps>

The two country-level variables are address formatting and tax treaties. International addresses with postal codes, provinces, or non-Latin scripts do not always map cleanly to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed addresses without explanation. Read the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [full EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/) before you file.
