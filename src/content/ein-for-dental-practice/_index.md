## Does a Dental Practice <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A dental practice needs an EIN whenever it operates as an LLC, professional corporation, or partnership, or whenever it hires employees. The IRS requires the EIN for payroll tax deposits and business returns. A solo dentist with no staff and a sole proprietorship may use an SSN instead.

The trigger is your structure and your staff. The moment a dental practice runs payroll, it must report wages to the IRS under an EIN. The moment it forms an LLC or PC, the entity needs its own tax identity.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Dental practice LLC or PLLC | Yes (required) |
| Dental professional corporation (PC) | Yes (required) |
| Dental partnership or group practice | Yes (required) |
| Solo dentist with W-2 employees | Yes (required) |
| Solo dentist, sole proprietor, no employees | Recommended |
| Non-US resident with a US dental-supply LLC | Yes (required) |

### Why Dental Practices Get an EIN

1. **Payroll** — Required to report hygienist and assistant wages and run [employee payroll](/ein-for-hiring-employees/).
2. **Insurance credentialing** — Dental payers enroll the practice as a billing entity by EIN.
3. **Business banking** — Open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) separated from personal funds.
4. **SSN privacy** — Keep your Social Security Number off W-9 forms and vendor paperwork.
5. **Tax compliance** — File the practice tax return under the entity EIN.

## How Does a Dental Practice <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

Most dental practices choose one of these entities before applying for the EIN:

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>US Dental-Supply LLC?</Accent>

A non-US resident submits IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 and enters a passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path. The IRS issues most correct EINs in 4-7 business days.

Non-resident demand here comes from dental-supply importers, dental tourism operators, and overseas dentists forming a US LLC to sell instruments, aligners, or whitening products to the American market. They form a US LLC and need an EIN for banking and platform payouts.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This creates the US entity that holds the EIN. You complete this online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, the formation date, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for a Non-Resident Dental LLC

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "wholesale dental supplies," "retail" |

## How Does an EIN for a Dental Practice <Accent>Compare to an SSN?</Accent>

An EIN identifies the dental practice as a business, while an SSN identifies the dentist as an individual. The IRS requires an EIN for payroll and entity returns. Using an EIN keeps the dentist's SSN off insurance and vendor paperwork.

| Factor | Practice EIN | Personal SSN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies | The business entity | The individual dentist |
| Required for payroll | Yes | No |
| Used on dental claims | Yes (with NPI Type 2) | No |
| Required for LLC or PC | Yes | No |
| Privacy on W-9 forms | Protects SSN | Exposes SSN |
| Cost from IRS | $0 | Already issued |

A solo dentist who stays a sole proprietor with no employees can technically file under an SSN. The downside is exposure: every vendor W-9 and insurance enrollment form then carries the personal SSN. An EIN solves this for $0 from the IRS. Compare more options on [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/).
