<Capsule>**An EIN is a 9-digit federal tax identification number issued by the IRS. A DUNS number is a 9-digit business credit identification number issued by Dun & Bradstreet.** Both are 9-digit numbers, but they serve entirely different purposes. An EIN identifies your business for tax purposes. A DUNS number tracks your business credit profile. Every business needs an EIN. A DUNS number is optional.</Capsule>

Business owners frequently confuse EINs and DUNS numbers because both are 9-digit identifiers assigned to businesses. The similarity ends there. An EIN is a government-issued tax ID required for federal tax reporting. A DUNS number is a private-sector identifier used for business credit monitoring. This guide explains exactly what each number does, when you need them, and how to get both.

Understanding the difference matters because using the wrong number in the wrong context causes delays. Banks ask for your EIN, not your DUNS number. Government contract applications ask for your DUNS number, not your EIN. Mixing them up wastes time. Learn the difference once and never confuse them again.

## What Is an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is a 9-digit number formatted as XX-XXXXXXX, issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to businesses and other entities operating in the United States. The IRS uses EINs to identify taxpayers who are required to file business tax returns.

Every business that hires employees, operates as a corporation or partnership, or files certain tax returns must have an EIN. The IRS issues approximately 5 million new EINs each year. An EIN is free to obtain and permanent once assigned. Read our complete guide on [what is an EIN](/what-is-ein/) for a detailed explanation.

### Key Facts About EINs

- **Issued by:** Internal Revenue Service (IRS)
- **Format:** XX-XXXXXXX (9 digits)
- **Cost:** $0 (free from IRS)
- **Purpose:** Federal tax identification and reporting
- **Required for:** Tax returns, payroll, bank accounts, business licenses
- **Application:** [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) (online, fax, phone, or mail)
- **Processing time:** Instant (online) or 4-7 business days (fax)

## What Is a <Accent>DUNS Number?</Accent>

A DUNS (Data Universal Numbering System) number is a unique 9-digit identifier assigned to businesses by Dun & Bradstreet (D&B), a private commercial data analytics company. Dun & Bradstreet maintains a database of over 500 million business records worldwide. The DUNS number is the key identifier in this database.

Dun & Bradstreet created the DUNS system in 1963. The US federal government adopted it in 1994 as the standard identifier for entities doing business with the government. The DUNS number is linked to a company's D&B credit file, which tracks payment history, financial stress scores, and credit ratings.

### Key Facts About DUNS Numbers

- **Issued by:** Dun & Bradstreet (D&B), a private company
- **Format:** 9 digits (no hyphen, e.g., 123456789)
- **Cost:** Free (standard 30-day processing) or $229 (expedited 1-5 days)
- **Purpose:** Business credit identification and government contracting
- **Required for:** Federal contracts, grants, some business credit applications
- **Application:** Online at dnb.com or by phone at 866-705-5711
- **Processing time:** 30 business days (free) or 1-5 business days ($229)

### The Core Difference

An EIN is a **legal requirement** for operating a business in the United States. Without an EIN, you cannot file taxes, open a business bank account, or hire employees. The IRS requires it.

A DUNS number is a **business decision**. No law requires you to have one unless you bid on federal contracts or apply for federal grants. Dun & Bradstreet is a private company, not a government agency. Your DUNS number helps you build a business credit profile separate from your personal credit.

## When Do You Need an <Accent>EIN vs DUNS Number?</Accent>

### You Need an EIN When You:

1. **Form an LLC, corporation, or partnership** — The IRS requires an EIN for all multi-member entities and recommends one for single-member LLCs.
2. **Hire employees** — Payroll tax reporting requires an EIN on every W-2 and quarterly 941 filing.
3. **Open a business bank account** — Every US bank requires an EIN to open a business checking or savings account. See our guide on [EIN for bank accounts](/ein-for-bank-account/).
4. **File federal tax returns** — Your EIN appears on Form 1120, 1065, Schedule C, and all other business tax forms.
5. **Accept payments through PayPal, Stripe, or Amazon** — Payment processors require a tax ID for 1099-K reporting.
6. **Apply for business licenses** — Most state and local business licenses require an EIN.

### You Need a DUNS Number When You:

1. **Bid on US government contracts** — Registration in SAM.gov (System for Award Management) requires a DUNS number.
2. **Apply for federal grants** — All federal grant applications require a DUNS number.
3. **Build business credit** — D&B's Paydex score is one of the primary business credit scores in the US.
4. **Establish vendor credit** — Some suppliers check your D&B credit file before extending net-30 or net-60 terms.
5. **Verify business legitimacy** — Some large companies require DUNS numbers from their vendors for due diligence.

## How to Get an EIN <Accent>and DUNS Number</Accent>

### Getting an EIN

US residents with an SSN or ITIN can apply online through the IRS EIN Assistant at irs.gov. The EIN is issued immediately. Non-US residents must submit [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) by fax to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Processing takes 4-7 business days.

Non-US residents who want a faster process use ein.so. We prepare your Form SS-4, fax it to the IRS, and deliver your EIN by email. Standard processing costs $49. Express processing costs $97. Learn more about [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/).

### Getting a DUNS Number

Visit dnb.com and click "Get a DUNS Number." The free option takes 30 business days. The expedited option costs $229 and takes 1-5 business days. You will need your business name, address, phone number, and your EIN. Note: you should get your EIN first, because Dun & Bradstreet will ask for it during the application.

### Recommended Order

1. Get your EIN first (required for everything else)
2. Open your business bank account with your EIN
3. Then apply for your DUNS number if you need one
4. Start building business credit through your D&B profile

## EIN and DUNS: <Accent>Building Business Credit</Accent>

Your EIN is the foundation of your business identity. Your DUNS number is the foundation of your business credit profile. Together, they allow you to separate your personal finances from your business finances completely.

The D&B Paydex score ranges from 0 to 100. A score of 80+ indicates that your business pays on time. This score is based on payment data reported to Dun & Bradstreet by your vendors and creditors. The Paydex score is linked to your DUNS number, not your EIN.

However, you cannot get a DUNS number without first having an EIN. The EIN establishes your business as a legal entity. The DUNS number then tracks that entity's creditworthiness. For non-residents building US business credit, the first step is always getting an EIN. See our guide on [EIN for business credit](/ein-for-credit/).

### Business Credit Building Steps

1. **Get an EIN** — [Apply through ein.so](/apply/) for $49
2. **Form your LLC** — Register with a US state (Wyoming, Delaware, or your operating state)
3. **Open a business bank account** — Use your EIN and LLC documents
4. **Get a DUNS number** — Apply at dnb.com (free)
5. **Establish vendor credit** — Open net-30 accounts with suppliers who report to D&B
6. **Pay on time** — Build your Paydex score to 80+
7. **Apply for business credit cards** — Use your EIN, not your SSN

## Common Mistakes: <Accent>EIN vs DUNS</Accent>

### Mistake 1: Using a DUNS Number on Tax Forms
Your DUNS number has no function on IRS forms. Always use your EIN on tax returns, W-2s, 1099s, and any IRS correspondence. The IRS does not recognize DUNS numbers.

### Mistake 2: Thinking a DUNS Number Is Required
Unless you are bidding on government contracts or applying for federal grants, a DUNS number is optional. Many successful businesses operate without one. An EIN is the only identifier that is legally required.

### Mistake 3: Paying for an EIN
The IRS issues EINs for free. If a service charges $200+ for an EIN alone, you are overpaying. ein.so charges $49 for the complete SS-4 filing service because non-residents cannot use the free online tool. The service fee covers form preparation and fax submission.

### Mistake 4: Getting a DUNS Number Before an EIN
Always get your EIN first. Dun & Bradstreet requires your EIN during the DUNS application process. Without an EIN, you cannot complete the DUNS application.

### Mistake 5: Confusing the Two Because Both Are 9 Digits
An EIN uses the format XX-XXXXXXX (with a hyphen after the second digit). A DUNS number is 9 straight digits with no hyphen. If someone asks for your "9-digit business number," ask whether they mean your EIN (tax ID) or DUNS number (credit ID).
