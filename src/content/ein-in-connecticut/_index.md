## Is a Connecticut EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Connecticut EIN is not different from a federal EIN, because the EIN is a federal number issued only by the IRS. There is no state-specific version. A business in Connecticut uses the same 9-digit EIN it would use in any US state.

The confusion comes from two separate identifiers. The EIN is federal. The Connecticut state tax registration is separate and comes from the Department of Revenue Services (DRS). Many businesses need both numbers.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, Form 5472 |
| CT state tax registration | Department of Revenue Services | Connecticut sales tax, state withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration in Connecticut. You apply for the EIN first through the IRS, then register with DRS if your activities require it. See the [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) guide for how the two numbers work together. The EIN is the federal foundation; the DRS number sits on top for state-level obligations.

## How Do You Get an EIN for a <Accent>Connecticut Business?</Accent>

You get an EIN for a Connecticut business by applying directly to the IRS, and the method depends on whether you have an SSN. US residents with an SSN use the IRS online tool for an instant EIN. Non-residents file Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627.

| Method | Speed | SSN required | Cost |

The process is identical in every state. Connecticut adds no extra federal step. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the complete walkthrough.

### Steps to Get an EIN in Connecticut

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Connecticut Entity">Register your LLC or corporation with the Connecticut Secretary of the State, or confirm your existing entity details. You need a legal entity name and formation date to attach the EIN to. Non-residents can complete formation online through a registered agent.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (for non-residents) or SSN (for residents), your entity name, the formation state (Connecticut), and your mailing address. Non-residents do not need an SSN or ITIN for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your legal entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b if you have no SSN. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Residents submit online for an instant EIN. Non-residents fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The IRS returns the CP 575 EIN assignment letter in 4-7 business days for an error-free filing.</Step>
</Steps>

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Connecticut LLC?</Accent>

Yes, a non-US resident can get an EIN for a Connecticut LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You form the LLC through the Connecticut Secretary of the State, then file Form SS-4 by fax with your passport number on Line 7b. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

The IRS online EIN tool is blocked for applicants who have no SSN. That single rule forces non-residents onto the fax method. The fax method works for any passport holder from any country.

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Connecticut LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your foreign or US mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting that the IRS does not accept. ein.so reformats international addresses to an IRS-accepted style. See [EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the full non-resident path.

## Should You Form Your LLC in Connecticut or <Accent>Another State?</Accent>

The choice of formation state changes your fees and reporting, but it does not change the EIN, which is federal and identical everywhere. Many non-residents form in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico, while businesses that operate physically in Connecticut form there.

<InfoBox title="Key points for non-residents choosing a state">
<ul>
<li>The EIN is federal. It is the same number whether you form in Connecticut, Wyoming, or Delaware.</li>
<li>Form in Connecticut if you have a physical presence, employees, or customers there.</li>
<li>Form in a no-income-tax state if you operate fully online with no Connecticut nexus. Confirm nexus rules with a CPA.</li>
<li>Every US LLC, regardless of state, can be foreign-owned and gets its EIN through the same Form SS-4 process.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

Wherever you form, the federal SS-4 process described above stays the same. ein.so files the SS-4 for non-residents in any state for $49 Standard or $97 Express. Start your application at [apply](/apply/) once your entity is formed.
