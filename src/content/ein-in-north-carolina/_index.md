## Is a North Carolina EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A North Carolina EIN is not different from a federal EIN. The EIN is a single federal number issued by the IRS. It is the same 9-digit number (XX-XXXXXXX) whether your business operates in North Carolina, Texas, or any other state.

North Carolina does not issue its own EIN. The IRS owns the EIN program nationwide. What North Carolina does issue is a separate state tax registration through the Department of Revenue. These two numbers serve different purposes and you may need both.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, Form SS-4 |
| NC state tax ID | NC Department of Revenue | North Carolina sales tax, withholding |

Your federal EIN never replaces a state tax registration, and a state registration never replaces an EIN. The EIN comes first because banks and the IRS require it. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how the two interact, and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full federal process.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in North Carolina?</Accent>

You get an EIN in North Carolina through the IRS using one of 4 methods. US persons with an SSN use the online tool for an instant result. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

| Method | Speed | SSN Needed | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | Yes | Free |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | No | Free / $49 |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same call | International only | Free |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | No | Free |

The process is identical regardless of state because the EIN is federal. North Carolina adds no extra step to the EIN application itself.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>North Carolina LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for a North Carolina LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and fax the form to the IRS at 855-215-1627. No US visit and no Social Security Number are required.

The IRS online EIN tool blocks anyone without an SSN. That is the single reason non-residents must use the fax method instead. ein.so prepares the SS-4 correctly and faxes it for you, removing the most common rejection causes.

## What Federal Tax Filings Does a North Carolina <Accent>LLC Have?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member North Carolina LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for failing to file is $25,000. These are federal requirements, identical in North Carolina and every other state.

North Carolina also applies a 4.5% flat state income tax, separate from federal tax. Tax rates and rules change, so confirm your current obligations with a CPA. ein.so files Form SS-4 and does not provide tax advice.
