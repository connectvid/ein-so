## Do Restaurants & Food Trucks <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A restaurant or food truck needs an EIN if it is an LLC, a corporation, or hires employees. A sole proprietor with zero staff can legally use an SSN, but an EIN is recommended to protect that SSN and separate business finances. Banks, payroll, and vendors all require the EIN.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Restaurant or food truck LLC | Yes (required) |
| Restaurant or food truck corporation | Yes (required) |
| Food business with any employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US resident with a US food LLC | Yes (required) |
| Sole proprietor, no employees | Recommended |
| Hobby cooking, no business income | No |

### Why Restaurants & Food Trucks Get an EIN

1. **Payroll** — Required to hire staff and file [employment taxes](/ein-for-hiring-employees/)
2. **Business banking** — Open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) and accept card payments
3. **Tax filing** — Required on the business tax return for an LLC or corporation
4. **Vendor accounts** — Food distributors and wholesalers require an EIN to open trade accounts
5. **Privacy** — Keep your SSN off W-9s, invoices, and supplier paperwork
6. **Permits** — Health and food permits and resale certificates reference your business tax ID

## What Do Restaurants & Food Trucks Use an <Accent>EIN For?</Accent>

Restaurants and food trucks use an EIN to run payroll, open bank and merchant accounts, set up vendor trade lines, and file business taxes. The EIN is the single federal ID that connects a food business to the IRS, banks, suppliers, and payroll providers.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Hiring and Payroll" index={1}>A restaurant with cooks, servers, and dishwashers needs an EIN to run payroll and file IRS Form 941 quarterly and Form 940 annually. Payroll providers like Gusto and ADP require the EIN before processing the first paycheck. See [EIN for hiring employees](/ein-for-hiring-employees/).</Card>
<Card title="Business Banking and Card Processing" index={2}>Banks require an EIN to open a restaurant business checking account. Merchant processors like Square, Toast, and Stripe use the EIN to underwrite card payments. A separate account keeps daily sales clean for bookkeeping. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Vendor and Wholesale Accounts" index={3}>Food distributors such as Sysco and US Foods require an EIN to open a trade account and extend net terms. Many suppliers also request a state resale or sales tax certificate, which references your EIN. The EIN keeps your SSN off vendor files.</Card>
<Card title="Permits and Compliance" index={4}>Health department permits, food handler registrations, and sales tax accounts reference your business tax ID. A food truck operating across city or county lines uses one EIN across all jurisdictions. The EIN ties every permit to one legal entity.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

A single EIN covers one legal entity. If one LLC owns a restaurant and two food trucks, all three operate under that one EIN.

### Best Business Structure for a Food Business

Most restaurant and food truck owners choose one of two structures:

- **[LLC](/ein-for-llc/)** — Liability protection separates personal assets from restaurant debts and claims. Required for most bank and vendor accounts.
- **[Sole proprietorship](/ein-for-sole-proprietor/)** — Simplest to start with no formation step, but offers no liability protection from kitchen accidents or supplier disputes.

## How Does a Non-US Resident Open a <Accent>US Food Business and Get an EIN?</Accent>

A non-US resident forms a US LLC, then submits Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives your food business a US address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required for the EIN application.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. On Line 16, enter "Food service" or "Restaurant" as the principal activity. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Restaurant SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Restaurant Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Food service" or "Restaurant" |
| Line 17 (Products/services) | E.g., "Prepared food," "Mobile food sales" |

## What Should Food Business Owners Know About <Accent>EIN and Taxes?</Accent>

A restaurant or food truck uses its EIN on every federal tax filing, including employment taxes if it has staff. A US-formed LLC owned by a non-US resident has extra filing duties. ein.so files the EIN and does not give tax advice; confirm specifics with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Employment Taxes" index={1}>A restaurant with employees files IRS Form 941 quarterly for payroll taxes and Form 940 annually for federal unemployment tax. Both forms reference the EIN. State payroll registration also uses the EIN. A US CPA can set up the filing schedule.</Card>
<Card title="Business Income Tax" index={2}>An LLC or corporation reports food business income on the federal return tied to its EIN. A single-member LLC owned by a US person reports on Schedule C. Structure and residency change the rules, so confirm with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Form 5472 for Foreign Owners" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and deadlines to avoid penalties. This is separate from the EIN application.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with food service and, for foreign owners, non-resident matters. Your EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance.

## How Does an EIN Compare to an <Accent>ITIN for Restaurant Owners?</Accent>

An EIN identifies a restaurant business to the IRS, while an ITIN identifies an individual non-US person who must file a personal US return. A food business needs an EIN to operate. An owner needs an ITIN only when personal US filing or tax treaty claims apply.

| Factor | EIN | ITIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies | The business entity | An individual person |
| Form filed | Form SS-4 | Form W-7 |
| IRS fee | $0 | $0 |
| Used for | Payroll, banking, vendors, business tax | Personal US tax return |
| ein.so Standard | $49 | $197 |
| ein.so Express | $97 | $297 |

Most restaurant and food truck owners need only an EIN to launch and operate. An ITIN becomes relevant for an individual's personal US filing obligations. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the full picture, and confirm your personal filing duties with a CPA.
