## How Do Jamaican Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Jamaican citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Jamaica passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Jamaican applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Jamaican founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC entirely online from Jamaica.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Jamaica passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Jamaica address. No TRN or Jamaican registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Jamaica address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Jamaica passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### Jamaica-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Jamaican Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Jamaica address (street, parish, postal code) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Jamaica passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |
