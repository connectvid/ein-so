## Can a Non-Resident Open a Relay Account <Accent>With an EIN?</Accent>

Yes. A non-resident can open a Relay account with an EIN, a registered US LLC or corporation, and a passport. Relay does not require an SSN, a US address for the owner, or a US visit. The US company is the account holder.

Relay onboards founders from most countries. As of 2026, Relay does not open accounts for sole proprietors, trusts, or businesses based in sanctioned and high-risk jurisdictions. You need a formal US entity with its own EIN. If you have not formed a company yet, register the LLC first, then get the EIN, then apply to Relay.

<InfoBox title="Who Relay accepts">
<ul>
<li>Owners of US LLCs and corporations registered in any state</li>
<li>Non-US residents from most countries (limited exceptions)</li>
<li>Single-member and multi-member LLCs</li>
<li>E-commerce sellers, agencies, SaaS founders, and service businesses</li>
</ul>

## What Do You Need to Open <Accent>a Relay Account?</Accent>

You need 4 core items to open a Relay account as a non-resident. Relay verifies each item online, so prepare them before starting.

| # | Requirement | Where it comes from |
|---|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **[EIN](/apply/)** and EIN confirmation letter | IRS — via [ein.so](/apply/) for $49 |
| 2 | Articles of Organization (LLC) or incorporation | Your state's Secretary of State |
| 3 | Operating Agreement | Your LLC formation documents |
| 4 | Passport for each 25%+ owner | Your home country |

Your EIN is the document Relay checks first. Relay matches your 9-digit EIN and legal business name against IRS records. If the name on your [EIN confirmation letter](/ein-confirmation-letter/) does not match your formation documents, Relay pauses the application. Request a [Letter 147C](/irs-letter-147c/) if you no longer have your original CP 575.

## How Do You Open a Relay Account <Accent>With Your EIN?</Accent>

Opening a Relay account takes 5 steps and 1 to 2 business days after your documents are ready.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Get Your EIN First">Non-residents cannot use the IRS online tool and must file Form SS-4 by fax. [ein.so files it for you](/apply/) and delivers the EIN in 7 to 14 business days. Start here because it is the slowest step.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Prepare Your Documents">Collect your EIN confirmation letter, Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, and a passport for each owner with 25% or more ownership. Save them as clear PDFs.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Start the Relay Application">Go to relayfi.com and click 'Open Account'. Enter your legal business name exactly as it appears on your EIN letter, your EIN, state of formation, and industry.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Add Owners and Business Details">Upload a passport for each beneficial owner and describe your business model, expected volume, and main customer markets. Specific descriptions clear review faster.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Get Approved, Then Build Your Sub-Accounts">After approval in 1 to 2 business days, open your sub-accounts and issue virtual cards. Set up separate accounts for taxes, profit, payroll, and operating expenses, then fund the account by wire or ACH.</Step>
</Steps>

## Why Relay Rejects Applications <Accent>(and How to Avoid It)</Accent>

Relay rejects applications mostly for EIN mismatches and incomplete owner information. These problems are easy to prevent.

<Alert type="warning" title="Top 5 reasons Relay holds or rejects an application">
<ol>
<li><strong>EIN name mismatch.</strong> Your EIN letter name must match your Articles of Organization exactly.</li>
<li><strong>Recently issued EIN.</strong> A new EIN can take up to 2 weeks to appear in IRS records.</li>
<li><strong>Incomplete ownership.</strong> List every owner with 25% or more and upload a passport for each.</li>
<li><strong>Unclear business activity.</strong> Describe your products, sales channels, and customers in detail.</li>
<li><strong>Restricted industry or country.</strong> High-risk activities and certain jurisdictions face denial.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

If Relay declines, fix the issue and reapply, or open a [Mercury account](/open-mercury-account-with-ein/) instead — both accept non-resident LLC owners with an EIN.
