## Do DoorDash and Delivery Drivers <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A delivery driver needs an EIN if they form an LLC, form a corporation, or hire employees. A solo driver operating as a sole proprietor can use an SSN instead. Most drivers still get an EIN to keep their SSN off W-9 forms.

The decision comes down to structure and risk. The table below maps common driver situations to the EIN requirement.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Driver with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Driver as sole proprietor | Recommended |
| Driver who hires employees | Yes (required) |
| Non-US resident with a US LLC | Yes (required) |
| Hobby or one-off side income | No |

### Why Delivery Drivers Get an EIN

1. **SSN privacy** — An EIN replaces the SSN on every platform W-9 and 1099.
2. **Business banking** — Most banks require an EIN to open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).
3. **Tax separation** — An EIN keeps delivery income separate from personal finances.
4. **Credibility** — An EIN signals a real business to lenders and platforms.
5. **Hiring** — An EIN is required before you run payroll for any helper or driver.

A driver who runs multiple apps benefits most. One EIN covers DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart, and Grubhub under a single entity. See [who needs an EIN](/who-needs-ein/) for the full eligibility list.

## How Do Delivery Drivers <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

US-resident drivers apply online at irs.gov for an instant EIN using an SSN. Drivers without an SSN file Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, which takes 4-7 business days. The IRS charges $0 for the EIN.

The right method depends on whether you hold a Social Security Number.

### Choose Your Business Structure First

Most delivery drivers pick one of two structures before applying:

- **[Sole proprietorship](/ein-for-sole-proprietor/)** — No formation step, no state fee, but no liability shield. You get an EIN to keep your SSN private.
- **[LLC](/ein-for-llc/)** — A state filing fee applies, but you gain liability protection and easier business banking.

A driver can start as a sole proprietor with an EIN, then form an LLC as income grows. The EIN stays valid under a sole proprietorship; a new entity may require a new EIN. Confirm entity-change EIN rules on the [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) page. For drivers without an SSN, the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) covers the fax path in detail.

## How Do Drivers File <Accent>Form SS-4 for an EIN?</Accent>

Drivers complete IRS Form SS-4 with the business name, address, responsible party, and reason for applying, then submit it. Online filing returns an instant EIN with an SSN. Fax filing to 855-215-1627 returns the EIN in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Pick Your Structure">Decide between sole proprietor and LLC. A sole proprietor needs no formation step. An LLC requires a state filing through a registered agent and gives you a separate legal entity to attach the EIN to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Details">You need your legal name, your business address, and your reason for applying. US residents add their SSN. Non-US residents use a passport number instead. No prior business ID is required.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the business or your legal name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, the responsible party on Line 7a, and the SSN or passport number on Line 7b. Mark "started new business" on Line 10.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Apply online for an instant EIN, or fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. ein.so files the fax and delivers the EIN by email in 4-7 business days (Standard) or 2-3 business days (Express).</Step>
</Steps>

### Driver SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Driver Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your LLC name, or your full legal name as sole proprietor |

## Why Does an EIN Protect a Driver's <Accent>SSN on a W-9?</Accent>

An EIN protects a driver's SSN because the W-9 form accepts an EIN as a valid tax ID in place of a Social Security Number. A driver who enters an EIN never exposes a personal SSN to a platform or subcontractor.

Every delivery platform that pays $600 or more in a year issues a 1099 and collects a W-9 first. The W-9 asks for a taxpayer identification number. A sole proprietor without an EIN must write the SSN in that field. That SSN then sits in the platform's records and any partner's records.

<InfoBox title="EIN vs SSN on platform forms">
<ul>
<li><strong>1099-NEC</strong> — DoorDash and Uber Eats report driver pay; an EIN keeps your SSN off the form.</li>
<li><strong>W-9</strong> — You enter an EIN instead of an SSN to confirm your tax ID.</li>
<li><strong>Subcontractors</strong> — If you pay a helper, you collect their W-9 and may issue a 1099 under your EIN.</li>
<li><strong>Banking</strong> — Banks pull the EIN, not your SSN, to open a business account.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

An EIN does not change how much tax you owe. A sole-proprietor driver still files Schedule C and pays self-employment tax. The EIN only changes which number appears on business paperwork. Confirm self-employment tax details with a CPA, because ein.so does not provide tax advice.
</Section>

A dedicated business account turns receipt-shoebox season into a clean ledger. Every deposit is delivery income. Every withdrawal is a business expense or a transfer to personal accounts.

## Can Non-US Residents Drive Through a <Accent>US LLC and EIN?</Accent>

A non-US resident with a US LLC gets an EIN by faxing Form SS-4 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is required. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only path for non-residents.

Some non-residents own US LLCs that engage in delivery, courier dispatch, or logistics operations staffed by US-based contractors. The owner abroad still needs an EIN to bank, file, and pay in the US.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form a US LLC" index={1}>Register an LLC in a state like Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. Formation happens online from abroad.</Card>
<Card title="File SS-4 by Fax" index={2}>Fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 with your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so files it for you in 4-7 business days (Standard, $49) or 2-3 business days (Express, $97). See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 Each Year" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return tied to the entity.</Card>
<Card title="File Your BOI Report" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The filing is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Non-residents who do not need an LLC but want a US tax ID for other reasons should compare an EIN against an ITIN. ein.so offers an ITIN service at $197 Standard and $297 Express. Confirm which ID fits your situation with a US CPA. The [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) covers the passport-based fax path in full.
