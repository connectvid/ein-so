An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is the 9-digit federal tax ID the IRS assigns to a business. Minnesota does not issue its own EIN. Whether you register your company in Minnesota (MN) or any other state, the EIN comes from the IRS through Form SS-4. The only state-specific step is forming the entity with the Minnesota Secretary of State and, if required, registering with the Minnesota Department of Revenue for state tax. This guide covers both the federal EIN process and the Minnesota-specific context for residents and non-US residents.

## Is a Minnesota EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Minnesota EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the IRS issues every EIN. The number is identical in format (XX-XXXXXXX) and function in all 50 states. Minnesota does not assign its own EIN. Your federal EIN works nationwide.

Two separate IDs apply to a Minnesota business, and people often confuse them. The federal EIN comes from the IRS and covers federal tax reporting. The Minnesota state tax ID comes from the Minnesota Department of Revenue and covers state-level obligations like sales tax collection or payroll withholding.

| ID | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 |
| Minnesota state tax ID | Minnesota Department of Revenue | State sales tax, MN payroll withholding |

You may need both. The EIN does not replace state tax registration, and the state ID does not replace the EIN. A non-resident with a US LLC and no Minnesota physical presence may not need state registration at all. Confirm your Minnesota obligations with a CPA. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the federal process.

## Can a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a Minnesota LLC <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for a Minnesota LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b instead. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents use the fax method to 855-215-1627. ein.so files it for you.

The IRS online EIN application blocks anyone without an SSN or ITIN. That is the single most common roadblock for foreign founders. The fax method removes that block. You submit a completed Form SS-4 by fax, and the IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Minnesota?</Accent>

You first form your entity with the Minnesota Secretary of State, then apply to the IRS for the EIN. Residents use the instant online tool at irs.gov. Non-residents fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627. The IRS returns an EIN in 4-7 business days for an error-free fax.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Minnesota Entity">Register your LLC or corporation with the Minnesota Secretary of State. This creates the legal entity the EIN attaches to. Confirm the current Minnesota filing fee on the Secretary of State site, since fees change. Non-residents may instead form in a low-fee state.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your entity name, formation state, and formation date. Non-residents also need a valid, unexpired passport. No SSN, ITIN, or Minnesota state registration is needed to obtain the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your entity name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your SSN, ITIN, or foreign passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit and Receive Your EIN">Residents submit online for an instant EIN. Non-residents fax the SS-4 to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS issues your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## Why Does a Minnesota Business Need an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

A Minnesota business needs an EIN to open a US bank account, file federal taxes, hire employees, and accept payments through processors like Stripe and PayPal. Banks and platforms require the 9-digit EIN before they will work with a US entity, resident-owned or foreign-owned.

The EIN is the federal identifier that links your Minnesota company to the US financial and tax system. Without it, a US LLC cannot function operationally.

<Card title="Open a US Bank Account" index={1}>Mercury, Relay, and traditional banks require an EIN to open a business account. Non-residents add a passport and formation documents. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="File Federal Taxes" index={2}>The EIN is your business tax ID for IRS filings. Foreign-owned single-member LLCs use it on Form 5472 and the pro-forma Form 1120 each year.</Card>
<Card title="Accept Payments" index={3}>Payment processors require an EIN to verify a US business. The EIN lets a Minnesota LLC settle in dollars and present a US business identity to platforms.</Card>
<Card title="Hire and Pay Employees" index={4}>If you hire in the US, the EIN is mandatory for payroll and IRS reporting. Minnesota payroll also triggers state Department of Revenue registration.</Card>

## What Should a Minnesota Business Do <Accent>After Getting the EIN?</Accent>

After getting the EIN, a Minnesota business registers for state tax if required, files its BOI report with FinCEN, opens a US bank account, and sets up annual federal filings. The EIN is the starting point, not the finish line, for US compliance.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Register With the Minnesota Department of Revenue">If you collect Minnesota sales tax or run payroll in the state, register with the Minnesota Department of Revenue for a state tax ID. A non-resident with no Minnesota presence may not need this. Confirm with a CPA.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="File Your BOI Report">Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. The filing is free at fincen.gov/boi. See [BOI filing](/boi-filing/) for the rules and deadlines.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Open a US Business Bank Account">Use your EIN, formation documents, and passport to open an account with Mercury, Relay, or Wise. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Set Up Annual Federal Filings">A foreign-owned single-member LLC files Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for non-filing is $25,000. See [Form 5472 guide](/form-5472-guide/).</Step>
</Steps>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident filings to confirm your Form 5472, BOI, and Minnesota state obligations. State income tax in Minnesota runs 5.35% to 9.85%, separate from federal tax, so your exact liability depends on your structure and income.
