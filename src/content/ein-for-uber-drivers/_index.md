## Do Uber & Lyft Drivers <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A rideshare driver needs an EIN when operating through an LLC, a corporation, or when hiring employees. A sole proprietor driver with no employees can use an SSN, but an EIN is recommended to protect that SSN on W-9 and 1099 forms.

The answer depends on how you structure your driving income. Use this table to find your situation.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Driver operating as an LLC | Yes (required) |
| Driver operating as a corporation | Yes (required) |
| Sole proprietor driver, no employees | Recommended |
| Driver who hires a second driver | Yes (required) |
| Non-resident owning a US delivery/fleet LLC | Yes (required) |
| Occasional driver, hobby-level income | No |

### Why Rideshare Drivers Get an EIN

1. **SSN privacy** — Enter an EIN, not your SSN, on the W-9 you give Uber and Lyft
2. **Business banking** — Open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) for rideshare income
3. **Expense tracking** — Separate fuel, mileage, and maintenance from personal spending
4. **Liability protection** — An LLC with an EIN shields personal assets
5. **Hiring** — Required to run payroll if you bring on a second driver

A driver who reports rideshare income on a Schedule C is a sole proprietor by default. That driver can file using an SSN. An EIN replaces the SSN on platform forms and bank applications, which reduces identity-theft exposure. See [who needs an EIN](/who-needs-ein/) for full eligibility rules.

## How Do Uber & Lyft <Accent>1099 Forms and the EIN Work?</Accent>

Uber and Lyft report driver earnings to the IRS on 1099 forms, and the recipient ID on those forms is your EIN or SSN. Using an EIN keeps your SSN off the platform's tax records and the copies sent to the IRS.

Each platform asks you to complete a W-9 form. The W-9 collects the taxpayer identification number the platform will print on your year-end 1099. A sole proprietor can list an EIN on the W-9 instead of an SSN.

## How Does a Driver <Accent>File Form SS-4 Without an SSN?</Accent>

A driver or LLC owner without an SSN files [Form SS-4](/tools/ss4-form-helper/) by fax to the IRS at 855-215-1627, entering a passport number on Line 7b in place of the SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for non-residents. Most error-free forms return an EIN in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Confirm Your Structure">Decide whether you operate as a sole proprietor or through a US LLC. A non-resident owning a US delivery or fleet entity registers the LLC first through a registered agent, then attaches the EIN to it.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your business name and, for an LLC, the state and formation date. No SSN and no ITIN are required for the SS-4.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your business name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Enter "started new business" on Line 10. ein.so completes the form to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Drivers

| SS-4 Field | Driver Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your business or LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-resident) |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Transportation" or "delivery services" |

## What Tax and Compliance Rules <Accent>Apply to Driver EINs?</Accent>

A sole proprietor driver reports income on Schedule C and pays self-employment tax. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC adds an annual Form 5472 and pro-forma Form 1120 filing, with a $25,000 penalty for missing it. Confirm every tax detail with a CPA.

<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120" index={2}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="Deductions" index={3}>Drivers deduct mileage or actual vehicle costs, phone use, platform fees, and supplies. Clean records from a dedicated EIN account support every deduction. Confirm deduction methods with a CPA.</Card>
