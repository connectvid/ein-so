<Section>
<Capsule>**The CP 575 is the original EIN confirmation letter the IRS mails when your EIN is first assigned.** If you lose it, request Letter 147C by calling the IRS at 800-829-4933. Banks and financial institutions often require CP 575 or 147C to verify your EIN before opening business accounts.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>CP 575</Label>
## What Is <Accent>CP 575?</Accent>

| Detail | Information |
|--------|------------|
| Full Name | Notice of EIN Assignment |
| When Issued | When EIN is first assigned |
| Delivery | Mailed to address on SS-4 |
| Replacement | Cannot be re-issued |
| Alternative | [Letter 147C](/irs-letter-147c/) |
| Banks Accept? | Yes |

CP 575 contains: your EIN, entity name, entity type, and filing requirements.
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>147C</Label>
## Letter 147C: <Accent>Replacement</Accent>

If you lose CP 575, request Letter 147C:
1. Call IRS at 800-829-4933
2. Verify identity as responsible party
3. Request fax (24-48 hours) or mail (10 business days)

Letter 147C confirms: EIN, entity name, address on file. Banks accept it as EIN verification.

See [IRS Letter 147C](/irs-letter-147c/) for details.
</Section>
