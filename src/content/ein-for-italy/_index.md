## How Do Italian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Italian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Italian passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Italian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and an entity to attach the EIN to. You can form the LLC online from Italy.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Italian passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Italian address. No Codice Fiscale or Partita IVA is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Italian address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Italian passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

| SS-4 Field | Italian Entry |

| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Italian passport number |

## What Should Italian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Italian Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Italian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The US-Italy income tax treaty helps prevent double taxation and can reduce US withholding on certain passive income.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return.</Card>
<Card title="US-Italy Tax Treaty" index={2}>The treaty allocates taxing rights and provides double-taxation relief. It can lower US withholding on dividends, interest, and royalties. It does not remove your obligation to declare income in Italy.</Card>
<Card title="Italian Reporting (Quadro RW)" index={3}>Italian residents generally must report foreign assets and holdings (for example via the Quadro RW). Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with an Italian commercialista.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and [deadlines](/boi-filing-deadline/) to avoid penalties.</Card>
