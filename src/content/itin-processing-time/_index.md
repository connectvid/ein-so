An ITIN is an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number, a 9-digit ID the IRS issues to people who must file or report under US tax rules but cannot get a Social Security Number. You apply with Form W-7. The ITIN is for individuals. It is not the same as an EIN, which identifies a business. Many non-residents who form a US LLC need only an [EIN, not an ITIN](/ein-for-non-residents/). This guide explains the full ITIN timeline, why it takes weeks rather than days, how to check status, and how ITIN timing differs from EIN timing.

| Factor | ITIN | EIN |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Issued to | An individual person | A business entity |
| IRS form | Form W-7 | Form SS-4 |
| Processing time | 7-11 weeks | 4-7 business days (fax) |
| Expedited option | None | Express via ein.so |
| Online option | None | Yes, but requires an SSN |
| ein.so price | $197 / $297 | $49 / $97 |

## How Long Does <Accent>ITIN Processing Take?</Accent>

ITIN processing takes 7-11 weeks from IRS receipt of Form W-7. Peak season (January-April) runs 9-11 weeks. Off-season (May-December) runs about 7 weeks. The clock starts when the IRS receives your form, not when you mail it.

| Season | Processing Time | Reason |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| January-April (peak) | 9-11 weeks | Tax filing season volume |
| May-December (off-season) | 7 weeks | Lower application volume |
| Renewals | 7-11 weeks | Same review as new ITINs |

### Step-by-Step Timeline Breakdown

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Mail delivery to IRS (international) | 1-3 weeks |
| IRS intake and queuing | 1-2 weeks |
| Document and identity review | 2-4 weeks |
| ITIN assignment and notice (CP565) | 1-2 weeks |
| Return mail of documents and letter | 1-3 weeks |
| **Total from receipt** | **7-11 weeks** |

International mail adds time on both ends. A W-7 mailed from outside the US can spend 1-3 weeks in transit before the IRS even logs it. Build that into your planning. The 7-11 week figure measures only the IRS internal clock.

<Label>Why Weeks</Label>

## Why Does an ITIN Take 7-11 Weeks When an EIN Takes Days?

An ITIN takes 7-11 weeks because the IRS manually verifies identity documents for a real person, while an EIN takes 4-7 business days because it identifies a business and skips identity verification. The two IDs follow completely different IRS workflows.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Identity Document Review" index={1}>The IRS verifies a passport or two alternate identity documents for every ITIN. This manual review is the main reason an ITIN takes weeks. An EIN application on Form SS-4 carries no identity-document review step.</Card>
<Card title="Original Documents in Transit" index={2}>Direct ITIN filers mail original or certified-copy documents to the IRS. Those documents sit at the IRS for the full review window. A Certifying Acceptance Agent verifies documents locally so you keep your passport.</Card>
<Card title="Tax Return Attachment" index={3}>Most W-7 forms attach a federal tax return. The IRS processes the ITIN and the return together, which adds queue time, especially during the January-April filing rush.</Card>
<Card title="No Online Channel" index={4}>The IRS has no online ITIN tool. Every W-7 is processed on paper. The EIN, by contrast, has an instant online tool, though that tool requires an SSN and is blocked for most non-residents.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

If you only need a business tax ID, you do not wait weeks. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for the 4-7 business day path.
</Section>

<Label>Speed Up</Label>

## Can You Speed Up ITIN Processing?

You cannot pay the IRS to expedite an ITIN; no expedited channel exists at any price. You reduce total time only by filing off-season, submitting an error-free Form W-7, and using a Certifying Acceptance Agent to avoid mailing original documents.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="File Off-Season">Submit your Form W-7 between May and December. Off-season processing runs about 7 weeks versus 9-11 weeks during the January-April tax rush. This single choice saves 2-4 weeks.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Submit an Error-Free W-7">Confirm every line is complete, the form is signed, and the reason-for-applying box matches your tax situation. A single error triggers a CP566 notice and restarts the review, adding weeks.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Use a Certifying Acceptance Agent">A CAA verifies your passport in person so you do not mail originals. Processing time stays at 7-11 weeks, but you keep your passport and lower your rejection risk.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Attach a Valid Tax Return or Exception">Most W-7 filings require an attached federal tax return. If you qualify for an exception (such as certain treaty or third-party reporting cases), document it precisely. Confirm your basis with a CPA.</Step>
</Steps>

<Alert type="warning" title="Common Causes of ITIN Delays Past 11 Weeks">
<ol>
<li>Missing signature on Form W-7.</li>
<li>Identity documents not certified or not accepted by the IRS.</li>
<li>Wrong reason-for-applying box checked, causing a CP566 correction notice.</li>
<li>Peak-season backlog from filing during January-April.</li>
<li>International return mail adding 1-3 weeks after assignment.</li>
</ol>

## How Do You Check ITIN Application Status?

You check ITIN status by calling the IRS at 800-829-1040 after 7 weeks have passed from your mailing date. From outside the US, call 267-941-1000, which is not toll-free. The IRS provides no online ITIN tracker, so the phone is the only status channel.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Wait at Least 7 Weeks">Do not call before 7 weeks from your mailing date. The IRS will not have a status earlier, and early calls waste a long international hold.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Details">Have your full name as written on Form W-7, your mailing address, and the date you submitted the W-7. The IRS agent uses these to locate your file.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Call the Correct Number">Call 800-829-1040 inside the US or 267-941-1000 from abroad. Call during US business hours in the Eastern time zone to reach an agent.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Watch for IRS Notices">The IRS mails a CP565 notice when it assigns your ITIN, or a CP566 or CP567 notice if it needs corrections or rejects the application. Respond to correction notices fast to avoid restarting the clock.</Step>
</Steps>

| IRS Notice | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| CP565 | ITIN assigned successfully |
| CP566 | More information or documents needed |
| CP567 | Application rejected; reapplication required |
</Section>

## How Does ITIN Processing Time Compare to Other IRS IDs?

An ITIN is the slowest common IRS identifier at 7-11 weeks, while an EIN by fax takes 4-7 business days and an SSN takes 2-4 weeks. The difference comes from the manual identity review and paper-only processing that an ITIN requires.

| Tax ID | Issued To | Processing Time | Expedited Option |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| EIN (online) | Business | Instant | N/A (needs SSN) |
| EIN (fax via ein.so) | Business | 4-7 business days | Yes, 2-3 days |
| SSN | Individual | 2-4 weeks | None |
| **ITIN** | **Individual** | **7-11 weeks** | **None** |

Non-residents forming a US LLC almost always need an EIN, not an ITIN. The EIN uses your passport number on [Form SS-4 Line 7b](/ss4-form-guide/), so you skip the SSN requirement entirely. See [getting an EIN without an SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) for the full method. An ITIN becomes relevant only when you have a personal US tax-filing obligation. Confirm your specific need with a CPA, because ein.so does not provide tax advice.

## Do You Actually Need an ITIN or an EIN?

You need an EIN, not an ITIN, when your goal is to run a US business, open a US bank account, or file business taxes. You need an ITIN only when you personally must file or be reported on a US tax return without an SSN. Most non-resident founders need only an EIN.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="You Need an EIN" index={1}>You formed or plan to form a US LLC or corporation. The EIN opens a [US bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/), connects payment processors, and files the LLC's annual returns. ein.so files Form SS-4 for $49 in 4-7 business days.</Card>
<Card title="You Need an ITIN" index={2}>You personally owe a US tax filing, claim a tax treaty benefit, or are a partner in a US partnership that reports your income. The ITIN goes on your individual return via Form W-7. Confirm the requirement with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="You May Need Both" index={3}>A foreign owner of a multi-member US LLC taxed as a partnership may need an EIN for the company and an ITIN to file an individual US return. These are separate applications on separate forms. Sequence the EIN first.</Card>
<Card title="Annual LLC Filing" index={4}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for missing it is $25,000. This uses the company EIN, not your personal ITIN.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Both IDs are free from the IRS itself; the IRS charges $0 for an EIN and $0 for an ITIN. ein.so charges for preparation and filing work: EIN at $49 or $97, and ITIN at $197 or $297. See [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for the full breakdown and what is included.

## How Should You Plan Around the ITIN Timeline?

You plan around the 7-11 week ITIN timeline by starting early, filing off-season, and sequencing your business setup so a slow ITIN never blocks faster tasks. Get your EIN and bank account moving first, since they do not depend on an ITIN.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Start the EIN First" index={1}>Your [EIN](/ein-for-non-residents/) takes 4-7 business days, not weeks. File it first so your LLC can open banking and accept payments while any ITIN application is still in IRS review.</Card>
<Card title="File the ITIN Off-Season" index={2}>If you do need an ITIN, file the Form W-7 between May and December for the 7-week window. Avoid the January-April rush, which adds 2-4 weeks to the IRS clock.</Card>
<Card title="Handle Compliance in Parallel" index={3}>File your [BOI report](/boi-filing/) and prepare [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) using the company EIN. These deadlines do not wait for a personal ITIN to be assigned.</Card>
<Card title="Keep Copies of Everything" index={4}>Keep a copy of your signed Form W-7, your mailing date, and any IRS notices. You need the mailing date to check status after 7 weeks and to prove timely filing if a delay occurs.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles EIN filing and ITIN preparation and does not provide tax advice. For personal tax questions, treaty positions, and whether you truly need an ITIN, work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters.
