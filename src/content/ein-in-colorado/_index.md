## Is a Colorado EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Colorado EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the IRS issues every EIN at the federal level. Colorado does not assign its own EIN. The 9-digit number works in all 50 states and never changes.

Many Colorado business owners confuse the federal EIN with a Colorado state tax account. They are separate registrations with separate authorities.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filings, banking, payroll |
| Colorado state tax account | Colorado Department of Revenue | State sales tax, wage withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. A Colorado retailer collecting sales tax registers separately with the Department of Revenue. A Colorado employer paying wages registers for state withholding. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for how the two interact, and [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for a full fee breakdown.

You get an EIN in Colorado through the IRS using one of four methods. SSN holders apply online for an instant number. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Colorado LLC?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN for a Colorado LLC by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. No SSN or ITIN is needed. The IRS online tool blocks applicants without an SSN, so fax is the path.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Colorado LLC">Register your LLC with the Colorado Secretary of State through a registered agent. Confirm the current state filing fee on the official Secretary of State website. This gives you a US business entity to attach the EIN to. You can complete this from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your Colorado LLC name and formation date, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to obtain the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Colorado LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is malformed international address formatting on Form SS-4. ein.so reformats foreign addresses to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Does a Foreign-Owned Colorado LLC <Accent>File Each Year?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Colorado LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 every year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not automatically a tax bill. Most LLCs also file a BOI report with FinCEN.
