<Label>What It Is</Label>

## What Does the Third Party Designee on SS-4 <Accent>Authorize?</Accent>

The third party designee on Line 18 authorizes one party to receive your EIN and answer IRS questions about the application. The authorization is narrow. It covers the single EIN application and nothing else. It ends when the IRS assigns your EIN.

The designee gains three specific abilities during the application:

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Receive the EIN" index={1}>The IRS sends the EIN assignment letter (CP 575) to the designee instead of the responsible party. The designee then forwards the EIN to you. This is how ein.so receives your EIN by fax and emails it to you the same day.</Card>
<Card title="Answer IRS Questions" index={2}>The designee can answer questions the IRS asks while processing the SS-4. This avoids delays when an examiner needs to confirm a detail. The responsible party does not have to be reachable during US business hours.</Card>
<Card title="Limited Time Window" index={3}>The authorization expires the moment the IRS assigns and mails the EIN. The designee cannot file taxes, change your address, or handle later IRS matters. Ongoing representation requires a separate Form 2848 or Form 8821.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The designee never owns or controls your EIN. You stay the responsible party on Line 7a. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for every field on the form.
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>Designee vs Responsible Party</Label>

## How Is the Third Party Designee Different From the <Accent>Responsible Party?</Accent>

The third party designee on Line 18 only receives the EIN, while the responsible party on Line 7a controls the entity. These two roles are separate and never interchangeable. The responsible party stays on file permanently. The designee authorization ends in days.

People confuse these two fields constantly. The table below makes the difference exact.

| Factor | Responsible Party (Line 7a) | Third Party Designee (Line 18) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Role | Controls the entity and its funds | Receives the EIN on your behalf |
| Required? | Yes, always | No, optional |
| Who it is | A natural person (owner/manager) | Often a service, CPA, or attorney |
| Duration on file | Permanent | Ends when EIN is assigned |
| Signs the form? | Yes | No |
| Identification used | Passport number on Line 7b | Name, phone, fax only |
| Can handle later IRS matters? | Yes | No |

The responsible party must be an individual, not a company. For a non-US resident, you enter your full passport name on Line 7a and your passport number on Line 7b. Read the dedicated [responsible party guide](/responsible-party-ein/) for the rules the IRS applies to Line 7a. For applicants without a Social Security Number, the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) explains why the passport number replaces the SSN.
</Section>

## When Should You Use a Third Party Designee on <Accent>Form SS-4?</Accent>

You use a third party designee when someone other than you files Form SS-4 and needs to receive the EIN. You leave Line 18 blank when you file the form yourself. The choice depends entirely on who submits the application.

The four common scenarios are listed below.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="You Use ein.so" index={1}>List ein.so as your designee. We prepare your SS-4, fax it to the IRS at 855-215-1627, receive your EIN, and email it to you. Non-US residents use this path because the IRS online tool requires an SSN they do not have. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/).</Card>
<Card title="You Use a CPA" index={2}>List your accountant as the designee. The CPA files the form and receives the EIN to begin your bookkeeping and tax setup. The CPA forwards the EIN to you for your records and banking.</Card>
<Card title="You Use an Attorney" index={3}>List your lawyer as the designee. Attorneys often file the SS-4 alongside the LLC formation documents. The attorney receives the EIN and includes it in your entity records.</Card>
<Card title="You File Yourself" index={4}>Leave Line 18 blank. The IRS sends the EIN directly to the responsible party named on Line 7a. This works for US residents using the online tool. Non-residents without an SSN cannot use the online tool.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The IRS online EIN application is restricted to applicants with a valid SSN, ITIN, or existing EIN. Non-US residents who lack all three must file by fax or mail. The fax method, filed through a designee, returns the EIN in 4-7 business days. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) for the complete non-resident path.

## How Do You Complete the Third Party Designee Section on <Accent>SS-4?</Accent>

You complete the third party designee section by entering the designee's name, phone number, and fax number on Line 18. The designee does not sign the form. The responsible party signs the signature block below Line 18. The fields are short and require no identification numbers.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Enter the Designee Name">Write the full name of the person or organization receiving the EIN. If you use ein.so, this is ein.so. The name must match the party that will receive the IRS fax.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Add the Designee Phone Number">Enter a phone number the IRS can call with questions about the application. This is the designee's number, not yours. ein.so supplies a US-reachable number so the IRS never needs to call you internationally.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Add the Designee Fax Number">Enter the fax number where the IRS sends the EIN assignment letter. The IRS faxes the EIN to this number, which is why fax filing returns the EIN faster than mail.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Sign as the Responsible Party">The responsible party signs the signature block, not the designee. The signature certifies that every entry on the form is correct. ein.so prepares the form and you sign as responsible party before submission.</Step>
</Steps>

### Line 18 Field Reference

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Designee name | The party receiving the EIN (e.g., ein.so) |
| Designee phone | A number the IRS can call during US hours |
| Designee fax | The fax number for the EIN assignment letter |
| Signature block | Signed by the responsible party only |

The designee fields require no SSN, ITIN, or EIN. Only the responsible party section (Line 7b) carries an identification number, which for non-US residents is the passport number. See the [EIN by fax](/ein-by-fax/) process for how the IRS returns your EIN to the designee fax line.

## What Can the Third Party Designee <Accent>Not Do?</Accent>

The third party designee cannot file your taxes, change your address, or act on any IRS matter beyond the single EIN application. The authorization is one of the narrowest the IRS grants. It ends the instant the IRS assigns your EIN.

These limits protect you. The designee role is built to receive an EIN and nothing more.

<Alert type="warning" title="Limits of the Designee Authorization">
<ol>
<li>The designee cannot represent you in an audit or tax dispute.</li>
<li>The designee cannot file your annual returns or information returns.</li>
<li>The designee cannot change your responsible party or business address with the IRS.</li>
<li>The designee cannot access your tax account or transcripts.</li>
<li>The authorization expires the moment the IRS issues the EIN.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

For ongoing IRS authority, you file a separate form. Form 2848 grants power of attorney for representation. Form 8821 grants tax information access. Neither is part of the SS-4 designee role. ein.so files the SS-4 and acts as designee only. We do not provide tax advice. Confirm any tax representation needs with a CPA.

After you receive your EIN, your real compliance work begins. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Most LLCs also file a [BOI report](/boi-filing/) with FinCEN.
