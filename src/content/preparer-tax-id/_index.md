<Section>
<Capsule>**A PTIN (Preparer Tax Identification Number) is required for anyone who prepares or helps prepare federal tax returns for compensation.** Apply online at irs.gov/ptin. The annual fee is $19.75. PTINs must be renewed each year. The IRS issues approximately 750,000 active PTINs.</Capsule>
</Section>

<Section surface>
<Label>What Is PTIN</Label>
## What Is a <Accent>PTIN?</Accent>

| Detail | Information |
|--------|------------|
| Full Name | Preparer Tax Identification Number |
| Format | P + 8 digits (e.g., P12345678) |
| Issued By | IRS |
| Cost | $19.75/year |
| Required? | Yes, for paid tax preparers |
| Renewal | Annual |

PTIN is different from an EIN or SSN. It identifies the individual tax preparer, not the business.
</Section>

<Section>
<Label>How to Apply</Label>
## How to Get <Accent>a PTIN</Accent>

1. Go to irs.gov/ptin
2. Create an account
3. Complete the online application
4. Pay $19.75
5. Receive PTIN immediately

You need: SSN, date of birth, personal information, and previous year's tax return info for identity verification.
</Section>

<Section wide>
<Label>Who Needs</Label>
## Who Needs a PTIN?

- CPAs who prepare tax returns
- Enrolled agents
- Tax attorneys
- Seasonal tax preparers
- Anyone paid to prepare federal tax returns

Unpaid volunteers (VITA program) do NOT need a PTIN.
