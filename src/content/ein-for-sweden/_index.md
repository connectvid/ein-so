## How Do Swedish Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Swedish citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Swedish passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Swedish applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Swedish founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Sweden.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Swedish passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Swedish address. No Organisationsnummer or Skatteverket registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Swedish address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Swedish passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

| SS-4 Field | Swedish Entry |

## Can Swedish Applicants Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Yes. Swedish applicants get an EIN without an SSN by entering their passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b and faxing the form to the IRS. The IRS does not require an SSN or ITIN from a non-resident responsible party. The online IRS tool is the only channel that demands an SSN.

Many Swedish founders assume they need an ITIN first. They do not. The EIN belongs to your US LLC, not to you personally, so the IRS accepts a foreign responsible party with a passport identifier. An ITIN matters later only if you have a personal US tax filing requirement. ein.so offers an [ITIN service](/ein-without-ssn/) at $197 standard and $297 express for founders who do need one, but it is not a prerequisite for the EIN.

## What Should Swedish Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Swedish Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Swedish resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. Your US compliance still requires annual information filings, and you continue to declare income in Sweden.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties for late or missing reports.</Card>
<Card title="Swedish Reporting" index={3}>Swedish residents declare worldwide income to Skatteverket and may need to report foreign company interests and foreign accounts. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Swedish revisor.</Card>
<Card title="Avoid Double Taxation" index={4}>A US-Sweden income tax treaty exists to allocate taxing rights and reduce double taxation. It does not remove your Swedish obligations. Confirm treaty treatment of your specific income with a CPA.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
