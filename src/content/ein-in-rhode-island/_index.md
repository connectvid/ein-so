## Is a Rhode Island EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Rhode Island EIN is the same as a federal EIN, because the EIN is a federal number from the IRS. Rhode Island does not issue its own EIN. One 9-digit EIN serves your business nationwide for federal taxes, banking, and payroll.

The confusion comes from a separate document: state tax registration. A Rhode Island business may also register with the Rhode Island Division of Taxation for state accounts such as sales and use tax or employer withholding. That state registration is distinct from your federal EIN.

| Identifier | Issued by | Used for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form SS-4 |
| RI state tax account | RI Division of Taxation | Rhode Island sales tax, withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state registration, and state registration does not replace your EIN. A US business often needs both. Confirm your Rhode Island state obligations with a CPA. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the federal process that applies in every state.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Rhode Island LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for a Rhode Island LLC without an SSN or ITIN. You enter your passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents file by fax to 855-215-1627. ein.so handles this for $49.

Non-residents form a Rhode Island LLC through a registered agent, then apply for the EIN against that entity. The federal EIN process does not depend on your country of residence or your immigration status.

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Rhode Island Business?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 by entering your LLC details, your responsible party, and your reason for applying. Non-residents put a passport number on Line 7b. US residents put an SSN or ITIN. The form is identical for a Rhode Island business and any other state.

| SS-4 Field | What to enter |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Rhode Island LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home or business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/passport) | SSN/ITIN, or passport number for non-residents |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | Limited liability company |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The most common rejection cause for non-residents is address formatting. International addresses with postal codes that do not match the US layout can be silently rejected by the IRS. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style and reviews every field before faxing. A clean SS-4 is the difference between a 4-7 day approval and a multi-week resubmission. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the complete walkthrough.
