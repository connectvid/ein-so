## Why Do Non-Residents Need an <Accent>EIN Service at All?</Accent>

Non-residents need an EIN service because the IRS online tool requires an SSN, which most non-residents do not have. The fallback is faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. An EIN service prepares the SS-4 correctly and manages the fax filing so the application is not rejected.

A US LLC cannot open a US bank account, accept Stripe or PayPal payments, or file US taxes without an EIN. Non-resident founders form an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico, then need the EIN to activate it. Here is why the fax route matters for each group.

<Card title="No US Visit Required" index={2}>You apply entirely from your home country. The IRS does not require you to be in the United States to receive an EIN. ein.so files the SS-4 by fax and emails your EIN letter. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).</Card>
<Card title="Banking and Payments" index={3}>Mercury, Relay, Stripe, and PayPal each require an EIN before they activate a US business account. The EIN links your foreign-owned LLC to the US financial system. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="US Tax Filing" index={4}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The EIN identifies the LLC on those filings. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. See [Form 5472 guide](/form-5472-guide/).</Card>

### Key SS-4 Fields for Non-Residents

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is a malformed foreign address. The IRS rejects addresses that do not map to its field layout and rarely explains why. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and the [complete EIN application process](/how-to-get-ein/).
