## Does a Barber Shop or Salon <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

A barber shop or salon needs an EIN if it operates as an LLC or corporation, hires any employee, or pays booth renters who receive a 1099. A solo barber with no staff and no LLC can use an SSN, but an EIN is recommended to keep that SSN private.

The line is simple: the moment a second person earns money through your shop, or you create a legal entity, the IRS expects an EIN. The table below maps common salon situations.

| Situation | EIN Needed? |
|-----------|------------|
| Salon or barber shop LLC | Yes (required) |
| Salon corporation (S-corp / C-corp) | Yes (required) |
| Shop with W-2 employees | Yes (required) |
| Shop paying booth renters (1099) | Yes (required) |
| Solo barber, sole proprietor, no staff | Recommended |
| Non-US resident with a US salon LLC | Yes (required) |
| Hobby income, no business | No |

### Why Barber Shops and Salons Get an EIN

1. **Business license and permits** — Most cities require an EIN on the cosmetology establishment license application.
2. **Payroll** — Required to report wages on Form 941 and issue W-2s to barbers and stylists.
3. **Booth rental** — Required to issue 1099-NEC forms to independent chair renters.
4. **Business banking** — Required to open a [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) and accept card payments.
5. **Privacy** — Keeps your SSN off W-9s, vendor accounts, and product distributor forms.
6. **Commercial lease** — Landlords reference the EIN on the lease and credit application.

## How Does a Barber Shop or Salon <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

A barber shop or salon gets an EIN by filing IRS Form SS-4 online, by fax, or by phone. US residents with an SSN use the free online tool for an instant EIN. Non-US residents file by fax to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

The application is the same regardless of industry. Your method depends on whether you have a US Social Security Number.

## How Does a Non-Resident Salon Owner <Accent>File Form SS-4?</Accent>

A non-resident salon owner files Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, entering a passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool is blocked for applicants without an SSN, so the fax method is the only path. The IRS issues most error-free EINs in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Salon LLC">Register an LLC for the salon through a registered agent in a state such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico. This creates the legal entity the EIN attaches to. You can form it online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, your formation date, and your mailing address. No SSN or ITIN is required for the EIN itself.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. List the salon activity on Line 16. ein.so completes this to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for a Salon

| SS-4 Field | Salon Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your salon LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, then number of members |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "Personal care services" or "barber/salon" |

The IRS silently rejects malformed addresses and mismatched names. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) and our [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/) for the details that prevent rejection.

## What Tax Forms Does a Salon File <Accent>With an EIN?</Accent>

A salon files payroll forms for staff, 1099s for booth renters, and an annual income tax return tied to its structure. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC also files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year, with a $25,000 penalty for not filing.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Payroll Forms (Form 941, W-2)" index={1}>A salon with employees files Form 941 quarterly to report withheld taxes and issues W-2s in January. State payroll filings reference the same EIN. Confirm state rules with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="1099-NEC for Booth Renters" index={2}>Issue a 1099-NEC to each independent booth renter paid $600 or more in the year. Collect a W-9 from each renter first. The salon EIN appears as the payer ID.</Card>
<Card title="Form 5472 + 1120 (Foreign-Owned LLC)" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The report is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>

## Does a Salon Owner Need an ITIN <Accent>or an EIN?</Accent>

A salon needs an EIN for the business; the owner may also need an ITIN for personal US tax filing. The EIN identifies the salon entity to the IRS. An ITIN identifies an individual non-resident who must file a personal US return but has no SSN.

These are different numbers for different purposes. A salon LLC gets an EIN to operate, hire, and bank. A non-resident owner who must file a personal US tax return uses an ITIN. ein.so files both.
