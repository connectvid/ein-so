## What Do You Do After You Get Your <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

After you receive your EIN, you open a US business bank account, connect payment processors, and meet your annual filing duties. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file Form 5472 plus a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.

The EIN is the start of US compliance, not the end. The same steps apply whether you used ein.so or another service, because the IRS issues the same number to everyone. Confirm all tax specifics with a US CPA, because ein.so files EINs and does not give tax advice.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Open a US bank account">Use your EIN, LLC documents, and passport to open an account with a remote-friendly bank. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Step>
<Step step="2" title="File your BOI report">Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="File Form 5472 each year">A foreign-owned single-member LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The penalty for not filing is $25,000.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Confirm tax duties with a CPA">ein.so does not give tax advice. Confirm your US and home-country obligations with a qualified US CPA.</Step>
</Steps>
