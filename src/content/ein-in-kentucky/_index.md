## Is a Kentucky EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Kentucky EIN is not different from a federal EIN — they are the same number. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN in the format XX-XXXXXXX. Kentucky does not issue its own EIN. Your federal EIN works for every state.

Many founders assume each state issues its own employer ID. That is incorrect. The EIN is a single federal identifier from the IRS. Kentucky instead issues a separate state tax registration through the Kentucky Department of Revenue. You may need both numbers depending on your activity in the Commonwealth.

| Number | Issuer | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal tax filing, payroll, banking |
| Kentucky state tax account | Department of Revenue | State income tax, sales tax, withholding |

The federal EIN does not replace state registration. A Kentucky business that hires employees or sells taxable goods registers with the Department of Revenue separately. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the distinction and [EIN cost](/ein-cost/) for a full price breakdown.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN <Accent>in Kentucky?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN in Kentucky by faxing Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627 with a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online application requires an SSN, so non-residents cannot use it. The fax method issues an EIN in 4-7 business days.

The process is federal and identical in all 50 states. Forming the LLC happens at the Kentucky Secretary of State; the EIN happens at the IRS. ein.so completes both the form preparation and the IRS submission so the application is not silently rejected.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Kentucky LLC">Register your LLC with the Kentucky Secretary of State for the $40 filing fee, or use a registered agent. Kentucky does not require members to be US citizens or residents. You complete this step online from outside the US.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your foreign address. No SSN, ITIN, or US address is required to obtain the EIN as a non-resident.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Kentucky LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so fills this out to prevent IRS rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

## What Does Kentucky's 4.5% State Tax Mean <Accent>for Your EIN?</Accent>

Kentucky's 4.5% flat state income tax applies separately from your federal EIN obligations. Your federal EIN handles IRS reporting. The Kentucky Department of Revenue handles the 4.5% state income tax, sales tax, and employer withholding through a separate state account.

The EIN does not pay or report Kentucky state tax. A Kentucky business links its federal EIN to a state tax account when it registers with the Department of Revenue. The two systems run in parallel.

## What Does a Foreign-Owned Kentucky LLC <Accent>File Each Year?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Kentucky LLC files IRS Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 every year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. The LLC also files a Kentucky annual report and registers with the Department of Revenue if it has state activity.

Form 5472 is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill. It reports transactions between the LLC and its foreign owner. The IRS requires it for every foreign-owned disregarded LLC.
