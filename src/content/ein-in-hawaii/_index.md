## Is a Hawaii EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Hawaii EIN is the same as a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) that works in all 50 states. Hawaii does not issue a separate EIN. The number you receive in Honolulu is identical in format to one issued anywhere in the US.

People search "Hawaii EIN," but the EIN is always federal. Hawaii does run its own separate state-level registration through the Hawaii Department of Taxation. Many Hawaii businesses need both numbers for different purposes.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, Form SS-4 |
| Hawaii Tax ID | Hawaii Dept. of Taxation | General excise tax (GET), state withholding |

Your federal EIN does not replace Hawaii state tax registration. You apply for the EIN first, then register with the state if your activity requires it. See our [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) guide for the distinction. Confirm which Hawaii registrations apply to your business with a Hawaii CPA.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Hawaii?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Hawaii through the IRS by one of four methods. US residents with an SSN apply online at irs.gov for an instant EIN. Non-residents without an SSN file Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627 and receive the EIN in 4-7 business days.

## Can a Non-US Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Hawaii LLC?</Accent>

Yes. A non-US resident gets an EIN for a Hawaii LLC by filing Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627. No SSN or ITIN is required. The applicant enters a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

Non-residents form US LLCs in many states. Hawaii is one option, with a $50 state filing fee paid to the Department of Commerce and Consumer Affairs. The EIN process is identical no matter which state holds your LLC. The federal SS-4 procedure does not change.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Hawaii LLC">Register your LLC with the Hawaii Department of Commerce and Consumer Affairs through a registered agent. The state filing fee is $50. You can complete formation online from outside the US. A registered agent provides the required Hawaii address.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation details, and your mailing address. No SSN, ITIN, or US Social Security card is required. Your passport number is the only identification the IRS needs on Line 7b.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent silent rejections. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575. Use it to open a bank account.</Step>
</Steps>

## What Goes on Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Hawaii Business?</Accent>

Form SS-4 for a Hawaii business uses standard federal fields. You enter the LLC legal name, the mailing address, the responsible party, and the reason for applying. Non-residents enter a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. The form is one page.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Hawaii LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your mailing address (foreign address accepted) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC / sole proprietor / corporation |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "tourism services" |

The IRS uses Line 7b to identify the responsible party. US residents enter an SSN; non-residents enter a passport number. This single difference is why non-residents cannot use the online tool, which validates an SSN. The fax method accepts the passport number. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for line-by-line detail.

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these SS-4 errors">
<ol>
<li>Leaving Line 7b blank — non-residents must enter a passport number.</li>
<li>Using a malformed foreign address that the IRS silently rejects.</li>
<li>Selecting the wrong entity type on Line 9a for a single-member LLC.</li>
<li>Listing a responsible party name that does not match the passport.</li>
</ol>
