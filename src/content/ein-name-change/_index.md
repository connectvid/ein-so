**Change the business name on your EIN by notifying the IRS in writing. LLCs send a signed letter to the IRS in Cincinnati, OH 45999. Corporations check the name change box on Form 1120. Sole proprietors enter the new name on the next Schedule C.** Your 9-digit EIN number never changes. The federal process costs $0. The IRS takes about 4 to 6 weeks to record the change.

A business name change updates the entity name tied to your EIN without changing the EIN itself. The same 9-digit number stays linked to the same legal entity. The method depends on your entity type: a letter to the IRS for LLCs, the name change box on the annual return for corporations and partnerships, and the next tax return for sole proprietors. This guide covers each path, the state filing that comes first, the documents you send, and how a name change affects banking, Form 5472, and BOI reporting for non-resident owners.

## How Do You Change the Business Name on an EIN by <Accent>Entity Type?</Accent>

You change the EIN business name by the method matched to your entity type. LLCs mail a signed letter to the IRS. Corporations and partnerships check the name change box on the annual return. Sole proprietors enter the new name on the next tax return. The EIN number stays the same in every case.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="LLC: Letter to the IRS" index={1}>File amended Articles of Organization with your state first. Then mail a signed letter to the IRS in Cincinnati, OH 45999 listing your EIN, old name, new name, and address. Attach the state-stamped amendment. The responsible party signs. See [EIN for LLC](/ein-for-llc/).</Card>
<Card title="Corporation: Form 1120" index={2}>File amended Articles of Incorporation with your state. Then check the name change box at the top of your next Form 1120 corporate return. The IRS updates the EIN record when it processes the return. A separate letter also works.</Card>
<Card title="Partnership: Form 1065" index={3}>Update the partnership agreement and any state filing. Check the name change box on your next Form 1065 return. The IRS records the new partnership name from the return. No new EIN is issued for a name change.</Card>
<Card title="Sole Proprietor: Next Return" index={4}>Enter the new business name on your next Schedule C attached to Form 1040. A sole proprietor does not file a separate IRS notification for a DBA or trade-name change. The EIN remains tied to the individual owner.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

A non-resident owner of a foreign-owned single-member US LLC uses the LLC letter method. The IRS treats most foreign-owned single-member LLCs as disregarded entities, but the EIN record still carries the LLC legal name. Update that name with the Cincinnati letter so it matches your state amendment.
</Section>

## How Do You Send an EIN Name Change Letter to the <Accent>IRS?</Accent>

Send the EIN name change letter to the Internal Revenue Service, Cincinnati, OH 45999. The letter lists your EIN, old business name, new business name, and address. Attach the state-stamped amended Articles. The responsible party signs and dates the letter. The IRS processes it in about 4 to 6 weeks.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="File the State Amendment First">Change the legal name at the state level before you write to the IRS. File amended Articles of Organization (LLC) or Articles of Incorporation (corporation) with your Secretary of State. The state returns a stamped copy. This copy is your proof for the IRS letter.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Write the Letter">Include your EIN, the exact old name, the exact new name, the business mailing address, and a clear statement that you are changing the business name. Reference the attached state amendment. Keep the letter to one page.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Sign as the Responsible Party">The responsible party or an authorized member signs and dates the letter. For a non-resident single-member LLC, the owner signs. No SSN or ITIN is required to sign a name change letter.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Mail and Track">Mail the signed letter and the attached amendment to the IRS, Cincinnati, OH 45999. Keep a copy. The IRS takes about 4 to 6 weeks to update the record. You will not always receive a written confirmation for an LLC letter.</Step>
</Steps>

## When Do You Need a New EIN Instead of a <Accent>Name Change?</Accent>

You need a new EIN only when the legal entity changes, not when the name changes. Converting a sole proprietorship to an LLC, a single-member LLC to a multi-member partnership, or an LLC to a corporation creates a new entity that requires a new EIN. A pure name change keeps the same EIN.

| Situation | New EIN needed? | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Same entity, new name | No | Letter or name change box |
| Sole prop becomes LLC | Yes | File new [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) |
| Single-member LLC adds members | Yes | New EIN for the partnership |
| LLC elects corporation status | Sometimes | Confirm with a CPA |
| Owner changes (sale) | Sometimes | Depends on structure |
| DBA / trade name added | No | Register DBA, keep EIN |

A name change preserves your banking history, your payment processor records, and your tax filing continuity. A new EIN starts those records over. Confirm any structural change with a CPA before you decide. ein.so files [Form SS-4](/how-to-get-ein/) for a new EIN when the entity itself is new, with Standard at $49 (4 to 7 business days) and Express at $97 (2 to 3 business days). The IRS charges $0 for the EIN.

## How Does an EIN Name Change Affect Your Bank Account and <Accent>Payments?</Accent>

An EIN name change forces you to update your bank account, payment processors, and platform accounts to the new name. Banks match the EIN name to the account name and your formation documents. A mismatch triggers account freezes, especially for non-resident owners. Update each record after the state amendment is stamped.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Business Bank Account" index={1}>Send your bank the amended Articles and your EIN confirmation showing the new name. Mercury and Relay update the account name from these documents. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) for non-resident requirements.</Card>
<Card title="Payment Processors" index={2}>Update Stripe, PayPal, and Payoneer to the new legal name so payouts and 1099-K records match the EIN. A name mismatch can hold funds. Update the business profile and re-verify with the amended documents.</Card>
<Card title="Selling Platforms" index={3}>Update Amazon, Shopify, and Etsy seller accounts to the new name. Amazon re-runs the tax interview when the legal name changes. Keep the EIN the same and only change the displayed legal name.</Card>
<Card title="Registered Agent and State" index={4}>Notify your registered agent and confirm the Secretary of State record shows the new name. Your annual report must use the current legal name to stay in good standing.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Sequence matters. File the state amendment first, notify the IRS second, then update banking and platforms third using the stamped documents. Doing the bank update before the state amendment is a common reason non-resident accounts get flagged.

## How Does a Name Change Affect Form 5472 and <Accent>BOI Reporting?</Accent>

A name change requires the new legal name on every federal filing after the change. A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for non-filing is $25,000. Use the new name on these forms and update your FinCEN BOI report within the deadline.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Form 5472 + Pro-Forma 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. Report the new legal name once the change is effective. The $25,000 penalty applies to late or missing filings, not to name updates.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. After a name change, update the BOI report so the entity name matches the state record. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and confirm the deadline that applies to your LLC.</Card>
<Card title="Tax Returns" index={3}>Use the new name on the annual return that records the change. Corporations check the name change box on Form 1120. Partnerships check it on Form 1065. The IRS updates the EIN record from the processed return.</Card>
<Card title="Confirm With a CPA" index={4}>ein.so handles EIN filing and does not provide tax advice. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/) and [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/).</Card>
</NumberedCards>

## What Mistakes Should You Avoid When Changing an <Accent>EIN Name?</Accent>

Avoid applying for a new EIN when you only need a name change. A name change keeps the same EIN. Applying for a new one fragments your tax history and confuses banks. The other common errors involve sequence, signatures, and mismatched documents.

<InfoBox title="Avoid these name change errors">
<ul>
<li>Requesting a new EIN for a simple name change — the EIN stays the same.</li>
<li>Notifying the IRS before filing the state amendment — file the state amendment first.</li>
<li>Sending an unsigned letter — the responsible party must sign and date it.</li>
<li>Using a name on the letter that differs from the stamped Articles — match them exactly.</li>
<li>Updating the bank before the state record is amended — the bank flags the mismatch.</li>
<li>Forgetting to update Form 5472, the BOI report, and platform accounts to the new name.</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

For a non-resident owner, the highest-risk error is a name mismatch between the EIN record and the US bank account. Banks freeze accounts when the EIN name and account name diverge. File the state amendment, notify the IRS, then present the stamped Articles and the EIN confirmation to the bank in that order.
