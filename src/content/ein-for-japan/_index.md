## How Do Japanese Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Japanese citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Japan passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Japanese applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Japanese founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Japan.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Japan passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Japan address. No My Number, Corporate Number, or Japan registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Japan address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Japan passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### Japan-Specific SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Japanese Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your Japan address (banchi, city, prefecture, postal code) |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your Japan passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

A common Japanese rejection cause is address formatting. The Japan address order runs from large to small (prefecture first), which does not map to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed international addresses. ein.so reformats your Japan address to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Should Japanese Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US and Japan Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Japan resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The US-Japan income tax treaty helps prevent double taxation and can reduce US withholding on certain passive income.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="US-Japan Tax Treaty" index={2}>The treaty allocates taxing rights and provides relief from double taxation. It can lower US withholding on dividends, interest, and royalties. It does not remove your obligation to declare income in Japan. Confirm rates with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Japan Reporting" index={3}>Japan residents generally must declare foreign company interests and foreign income to the National Tax Agency. Confirm your reporting duties for a foreign-held US LLC with a Japan tax accountant (zeirishi).</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
