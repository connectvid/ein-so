## Is a Nebraska EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

A Nebraska EIN is the same as a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN that works in all 50 states. Nebraska does not assign its own EIN. The state issues a separate tax registration through the Nebraska Department of Revenue for state-level taxes.

Many new business owners confuse the federal EIN with a state tax ID. They serve different purposes and come from different agencies. The EIN identifies your business to the IRS for federal tax, banking, and payroll. The Nebraska state tax ID identifies your business to the Nebraska Department of Revenue for sales tax and withholding.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payroll, Form 5472 |
| Nebraska state tax ID | Nebraska Department of Revenue | State sales tax, state withholding |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. A business with employees or taxable sales in Nebraska may need both. Get the federal EIN first, because banks and the state often ask for it during registration. See the [state tax ID guide](/state-tax-id/) for how the two interact. Confirm your exact state registrations with a CPA, because requirements depend on your activity.

## How Do You Get an EIN in <Accent>Nebraska?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Nebraska by filing IRS Form SS-4. Applicants with an SSN use the IRS online tool for an instant EIN. Non-residents without an SSN fax Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b, and the IRS issues the EIN within 4-7 business days.

## How Do You Fill Out Form SS-4 for a <Accent>Nebraska LLC?</Accent>

You fill out Form SS-4 with your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Non-residents leave the SSN field blank and write the passport number instead. The IRS accepts a passport as identification for the responsible party.

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Nebraska LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country or US mailing address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, with member count |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "consulting," "software" |

The most common rejection cause is address formatting. International addresses do not map cleanly to the SS-4 layout, and the IRS rejects malformed entries without a clear error message. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style so your application is not silently rejected. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) for line-by-line instructions.

## What Taxes Apply to a <Accent>Nebraska LLC?</Accent>

A Nebraska LLC faces federal taxes through the IRS and state taxes through the Nebraska Department of Revenue. A single-member US LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. A foreign-owned single-member LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year.

The EIN is used for federal tax reporting. A separate Nebraska state tax ID is used for state-level taxes such as sales tax and withholding. The two systems are independent, and you may need to register with both.
