<Capsule>**The IRS issues EINs completely free of charge. There is no government fee for an EIN application.** You can get your EIN for $0 by applying online at irs.gov (requires SSN/ITIN), faxing Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627, calling the IRS at 267-941-1099, or mailing Form SS-4 to Cincinnati, OH. The only reason to use a paid service is if you cannot access the free methods — which is the case for most non-US residents.</Capsule>

Every year, the IRS processes over 5 million EIN applications. Every single one is free. The IRS does not charge a filing fee, processing fee, or any other fee for issuing an Employer Identification Number. This has been true since the EIN system was created in 1974 and remains true in 2026.

Despite this, dozens of websites charge $50 to $300+ for EIN applications. Some of these services are legitimate (they handle paperwork for people who cannot use the free online tool). Others are misleading, implying that a government fee exists when it does not. This guide explains every free method available and when paying for a service actually makes sense.

## 4 Free Ways to Get <Accent>an EIN</Accent>

The IRS offers 4 methods to obtain an EIN. All 4 are free.

<ProcessSteps>
<ProcessStep step="1" title="Online — IRS EIN Assistant (Instant)">Visit irs.gov and use the EIN Assistant. Available Monday-Friday 7:00 AM to 10:00 PM Eastern Time. Your EIN is issued immediately upon completion. **Requirement:** You must have a valid SSN or ITIN. This method is NOT available to non-US residents without an SSN. See our guide on the [IRS EIN online application](/ein-online-application/).</ProcessStep>
<ProcessStep step="2" title="Fax — Form SS-4 (4-7 Business Days)">Complete [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) and fax it to the IRS at [855-215-1627](/irs-fax-number-for-ein/). The IRS processes fax applications within 4-7 business days and returns your EIN by fax. This method is available to everyone, including non-residents. No SSN required. See our complete guide on [applying by fax](/ein-by-fax/).</ProcessStep>
<ProcessStep step="3" title="Phone — IRS Business Line (Same Day)">Call the IRS at [267-941-1099](/irs-ein-phone-number/) (international) or 800-829-4933 (domestic). Hours: Monday-Friday 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM Eastern Time. The IRS can issue your EIN during the call. You must have all Form SS-4 information ready. See [apply by phone](/ein-by-phone/).</ProcessStep>
<ProcessStep step="4" title="Mail — Form SS-4 (4-6 Weeks)">Mail your completed Form SS-4 to: Internal Revenue Service, Attn: EIN Operation, Cincinnati, OH 45999. Processing takes 4-6 weeks. This is the slowest method. See [apply by mail](/ein-by-mail/).</ProcessStep>
</ProcessSteps>

## When Does Paying for an EIN <Accent>Make Sense?</Accent>

Paying for an EIN service makes sense in exactly one scenario: **you cannot or do not want to navigate the free methods yourself.**

### Who Cannot Use the Free Online Tool

The IRS online EIN Assistant requires a valid SSN or ITIN. If you do not have either, you are locked out of the fastest free method. This affects:

- Non-US residents without an SSN or ITIN
- Foreign nationals forming US LLCs
- International Amazon sellers, Shopify merchants, and freelancers
- Anyone who has never had a US tax identification number

These applicants must use fax, phone, or mail. While these methods are still free, they require:
- Correctly completing [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) (21 fields, several with IRS-specific requirements)
- Access to a fax machine or international calling
- Knowledge of IRS procedures and terminology
- Patience to navigate IRS hold times (often 30-60+ minutes)

## Avoid EIN <Accent>Scams</Accent>

### Red Flags

1. **"Government filing fee" claims** — There is no government fee. If a website says the IRS charges a fee, it is lying.
2. **Prices above $150 for EIN only** — An EIN-only service should not cost $150+. That price should include additional services (LLC formation, registered agent).
3. **"Official" or "authorized" EIN sites** — The IRS does not authorize third-party EIN providers. Any site claiming "official IRS authorization" for EIN services is misleading.
4. **Domains that look like irs.gov** — The only official IRS website is irs.gov. Lookalike domains are scams.

### Legitimate Services

A legitimate EIN service is transparent about three things:
1. The EIN itself is free from the IRS
2. The service fee covers form preparation and filing
3. The exact price is stated upfront (no hidden fees)
