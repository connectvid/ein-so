## How Do Egyptian Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Egyptian citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using an Egyptian passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path for Egyptian applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Egyptian founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Egypt.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Egyptian passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Egyptian address. No Tax Card Number or Egyptian registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Egyptian address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Egyptian passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575) by email.</Step>
</Steps>

## Can Egyptians Get an EIN <Accent>Without an SSN?</Accent>

Egyptians get an EIN without an SSN by writing "Foreign" or their passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. The IRS issues EINs to foreign responsible parties who hold no Social Security Number. The online application tool blocks these applicants, so the fax route is required.

The IRS designed the SS-4 fax process for exactly this situation. A non-US resident with no SSN and no ITIN still qualifies for an EIN as the responsible party of a US LLC. Your Egyptian passport number is the identifier the IRS accepts.

## What Should Egyptian Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Tax Compliance?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by an Egyptian resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The owner still files an annual US information return. Confirm your exact position with a CPA.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC must file [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) and submit your report on time. The federal BOI filing itself is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
<Card title="Egyptian Reporting" index={3}>Egyptian residents declare worldwide income to the Egyptian Tax Authority under Egyptian rules. Your US LLC income may be subject to both US and Egyptian rules. Confirm your reporting duties with an Egyptian accountant.</Card>
