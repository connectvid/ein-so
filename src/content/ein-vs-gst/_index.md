## What Is an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is a 9-digit federal tax ID issued by the US Internal Revenue Service to businesses operating in the United States. The format is XX-XXXXXXX, for example 12-3456789. The IRS assigns each EIN permanently to one entity.

Every US business entity uses its EIN for federal tax filing, opening a bank account, hiring employees, and accepting payments. The IRS charges $0 for an EIN. ein.so files Form SS-4 for non-residents for $49 (Standard) or $97 (Express). For the full explanation, read [what is an EIN](/what-is-ein/).

### Key EIN Facts

<InfoBox title="EIN at a Glance">
<ul>
<li>**Issuer:** US Internal Revenue Service (IRS)</li>
<li>**Format:** XX-XXXXXXX (9 digits, one hyphen)</li>
<li>**Cost:** $0 from the IRS; $49 through [ein.so](/apply/)</li>
<li>**Purpose:** US federal business tax identification</li>
<li>**Application:** [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/) by online, fax, phone, or mail</li>
<li>**Processing:** 4-7 business days by [fax](/ein-processing-time/) for non-residents</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

Non-residents cannot use the IRS online EIN tool, because that tool requires an SSN. Applicants without an SSN file by fax instead. They enter a passport number on Form SS-4 Line 7b. No SSN and no ITIN are required. See [EIN without SSN](/ein-without-ssn/) for the full method.

## What Is a <Accent>GST Number?</Accent>

A GST number is a consumption-tax registration number issued by a national tax authority in countries that operate a Goods and Services Tax. The number identifies a business for GST collection and reporting. The format and length vary by country.

GST is a value-added consumption tax used in India, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, Singapore, Malaysia, and other countries. Each system has its own registration number. In India, the GSTIN is 15 characters derived from the state code and PAN. In Australia, GST attaches to the 11-digit ABN. In Canada, the GST/HST number is a 9-digit Business Number plus an RT suffix. The United States has no federal GST. US states impose their own sales taxes, which sit entirely outside the EIN system.

### GST Number Formats by Country

| Country | Number name | Format | Tax authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| India | GSTIN | 15 characters (state code + PAN) | GST authority of India |
| Australia | GST / ABN | 11 digits | Australian tax authority |
| Canada | GST/HST number | 9 digits + RT suffix | Canada tax authority |
| Singapore | GST registration | Prefixed alphanumeric | Singapore tax authority |
| New Zealand | GST number | Tied to IRD number | New Zealand tax authority |

A GST number reports tax a business collects on sales. An EIN does not. The EIN identifies the entity itself, not a consumption-tax obligation. This is why one number cannot substitute for the other.

## EIN vs GST Number: How Do They <Accent>Compare?</Accent>

An EIN identifies a business to the US federal government, while a GST number registers a business for consumption tax in its home country. They differ in issuer, format, jurisdiction, and purpose. One never replaces the other.

| Question | EIN | GST Number |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What does it identify? | A US business entity | A GST-registered business |
| What is it for? | Federal tax filing, banking, payroll | Collecting and reporting consumption tax |
| Who issues it? | US IRS | National tax authority |
| Where is it valid? | United States only | Issuing country only |
| What does it cost? | $0 (IRS) / $49 (ein.so) | Set per country |
| How do you apply? | Form SS-4 to the IRS | Country GST portal |
| Does the US use this system? | Yes | No (US has no GST/VAT) |

### The Core Difference

An EIN tells the IRS that your US company exists and files taxes. A GST number tells your home tax authority that your business collects consumption tax. The first is an entity identifier. The second is a tax-collection registration. A founder with a US LLC and a home-country GST registration holds both, and each works only inside its own border.

## How Do Founders From GST Countries <Accent>Get a US EIN?</Accent>

Founders from India, Australia, Canada, and other GST countries get a US EIN by submitting IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path. Enter a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS issues most correct filings in 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register a US LLC or corporation with a US state. Wyoming, Delaware, and New Mexico are common choices for non-residents. You can form the entity online from your home country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US entity name on Line 1, your home address on Line 4, your full name on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Your GST number is not requested.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Submit by Fax">Fax Form SS-4 to the IRS at 855-215-1627. ein.so prepares and files the form for $49 (Standard, 4-7 business days) or $97 (Express, 2-3 business days).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Receive Your EIN">The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575). ein.so delivers your EIN to you by email so you can open a bank account and accept payments.</Step>
</Steps>

Your existing GST number has no bearing on the EIN application. The IRS does not request or verify foreign tax IDs. Your passport is sufficient identification on Line 7b. ## Do You Need <Accent>Both Numbers?</Accent>

You need an EIN when you operate a US entity and a GST number when you operate in a GST country. You need both when you run businesses in both jurisdictions. The two numbers coexist and never conflict.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="You Need a US EIN If" index={1}>You formed a US LLC or corporation, sell on US platforms such as Amazon.com or a Shopify US store, want a US [business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/), hire US contractors, or file US tax returns.</Card>
<Card title="You Need a GST Number If" index={2}>You run a business in India, Australia, Canada, or another GST country, collect consumption tax on sales there, employ workers there, or cross your country's GST registration threshold for turnover.</Card>
<Card title="You Need Both If" index={3}>You operate businesses in both the US and a GST country, you are a GST-country resident who owns a US LLC, or you earn income in both jurisdictions. Most cross-border founders hold both numbers.</Card>
<Card title="The Order Does Not Matter" index={4}>Get the number for whichever country you start in first. A GST-country founder forming a US LLC adds an EIN to the GST number they already hold. Neither registration cancels or affects the other.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Most founders expanding to the US already hold a GST number at home. They add a US EIN when they form their US entity. The EIN unlocks US banking, US payment processors, and US tax filing, none of which the GST number can do.

## What US Tax Filings Follow a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year after receiving its EIN. The penalty for not filing Form 5472 is $25,000. The EIN is the start of US compliance, not the end.

<Alert type="warning" title="Annual Filing for Foreign-Owned US LLCs">
<ol>
<li>File [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year.</li>
<li>The penalty for failure to file Form 5472 is $25,000.</li>
<li>Form 5472 is an information return, not always a tax bill.</li>
<li>Most US LLCs also file a [BOI report](/boi-filing/) with FinCEN.</li>

The US has no GST or VAT, so there is no US GST return to file. State sales tax is the closest US equivalent, and it registers separately from your EIN, state by state. Your home-country GST obligations continue under your home rules regardless of your US EIN. ein.so files the EIN and does not provide tax advice. Work with a local accountant and a US CPA.
