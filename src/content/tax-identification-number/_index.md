A Tax Identification Number is not a single number. It is a category. The IRS uses "TIN" on official forms because several different numbers can fill the same field. A US citizen writes an SSN. A business writes an EIN. A non-resident writes an ITIN. Knowing which TIN you need prevents rejected forms, blocked bank applications, and missed tax filings. ## What Are the Five Types of <Accent>Tax Identification Number?</Accent>

The five types of TIN are the EIN, SSN, ITIN, PTIN, and ATIN. Each serves a distinct purpose. The EIN identifies a business. The SSN identifies a US citizen. The ITIN identifies a non-resident person. The PTIN identifies a paid tax preparer. The ATIN covers an adopted child awaiting an SSN.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="EIN — Employer Identification Number" index={1}>The EIN is a 9-digit business TIN formatted XX-XXXXXXX. Every US business entity uses one to open a bank account, hire staff, and file taxes. Non-residents get an EIN by faxing Form SS-4. The IRS charges $0. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/).</Card>
<Card title="SSN — Social Security Number" index={2}>The SSN is a 9-digit personal TIN formatted XXX-XX-XXXX. The Social Security Administration issues it to US citizens and work-authorized residents using Form SS-5. Non-residents abroad cannot get an SSN, so they use an ITIN or EIN instead.</Card>
<Card title="ITIN — Individual Taxpayer ID" index={3}>The ITIN is a 9-digit personal TIN that starts with 9. The IRS issues it to non-residents who must file a US tax return but cannot get an SSN. You apply with Form W-7. The ein.so ITIN service costs $197 Standard. See the [ITIN guide](/itin-number/).</Card>
<Card title="PTIN — Preparer Tax ID" index={4}>The PTIN is an 8-digit number prefixed with P. Every paid US tax preparer must hold one before signing a return. Preparers register at irs.gov. The PTIN protects the preparer's SSN from appearing on client returns. See [preparer tax ID](/preparer-tax-id/).</Card>
</NumberedCards>

The fifth type, the ATIN, is rare. The IRS issues an Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number to families adopting a US child when the child's SSN is not yet available. It lets adoptive parents claim the child on a return. Most readers of this page need the EIN, which is the business TIN.
</Section>

## Which Tax Identification Number <Accent>Do You Need?</Accent>

You need the TIN that matches your role. A US business needs an EIN. A US citizen filing personal taxes uses an SSN. A non-resident person filing US taxes needs an ITIN. A paid tax preparer needs a PTIN. Match your situation in the table below.

| US citizen filing personal tax | SSN | File Form SS-5 |

| Paid US tax preparer | [PTIN](/preparer-tax-id/) | Register at irs.gov |

Most non-resident entrepreneurs need the EIN, not the ITIN. The EIN belongs to the business. The ITIN belongs to a person. A foreign owner of a US LLC gets an EIN for the company and does not need an ITIN unless a US tax return requires a personal number. Confirm your personal filing needs with a US CPA. ## How Does an EIN Differ From Other <Accent>Tax Identification Numbers?</Accent>

An EIN differs from other TINs because it identifies a business, not a person. The SSN and ITIN identify individuals. The PTIN identifies a tax preparer. The EIN is the only TIN that attaches to a company, which is why every US LLC and corporation needs one.

The distinction matters when forms ask for a TIN. A bank opening a business account wants the EIN. A US client sending you a W-9 wants the EIN of your LLC. A payment processor like Stripe or PayPal collects the EIN during its tax interview. You never enter a personal SSN or ITIN where a business TIN belongs.

| Question | EIN | SSN | ITIN |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identifies a... | Business | Person | Person |
| Available to non-residents | Yes | No | Yes |
| Requires SSN to apply | No | N/A | No |
| Used on W-9 / bank forms | Yes | Personal only | Personal only |
| IRS fee | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| ein.so price | $49 | N/A | $197 |

The biggest practical difference for non-residents is access. The IRS online EIN tool requires an SSN. Non-residents cannot use it. Instead, they fax Form SS-4 and enter a passport number on Line 7b. This is why the fax method, which ein.so handles, is the standard route for foreign founders. See [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).

## How Do Non-Residents Get a Business <Accent>TIN (EIN)?</Accent>

Non-residents get a business TIN by faxing IRS Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627, using a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days. The online tool is blocked without an SSN, so fax is the path.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an LLC in a state like Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This creates the legal entity that the EIN attaches to. You can form the LLC entirely online from abroad.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US residency is required to get the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent errors. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter, the CP 575.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for the Business TIN

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC name |
| Line 4 (Address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is a malformed international address. The IRS silently drops applications with addresses that do not fit the SS-4 layout. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. ## Where Do You Use Your Business <Accent>Tax Identification Number?</Accent>

You use your business TIN, the EIN, anywhere a US form asks for a company tax ID. This includes bank applications, the W-9, the W-8BEN-E, payment-processor tax interviews, and your annual IRS filings. The EIN is the single number that links your US LLC to every US financial and tax system.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Bank Account" index={1}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise require your EIN and passport to open a US business account. The EIN appears on the application as your business TIN. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Payment Processors" index={2}>Stripe, PayPal, and Payoneer collect your EIN during their tax interview. Without a valid business TIN, payouts are held or capped. The EIN unlocks full USD processing for your LLC.</Card>
<Card title="Annual IRS Filing" index={3}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The EIN identifies the filer. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Confirm your duties with a US CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Marketplace Selling" index={4}>Amazon, Shopify, and Etsy require a US TIN for their seller tax interview. Sellers enter the EIN of their US LLC to complete onboarding and receive payouts in dollars.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

Beyond these, most US LLCs must file a Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report with FinCEN, which is free. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) after you receive your EIN. Keep your CP 575 assignment letter safe, because banks and platforms ask for it as proof of your business TIN.
