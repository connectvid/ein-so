## Is a Utah EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Utah EIN is the same as a federal EIN. The IRS issues one 9-digit EIN (XX-XXXXXXX) that works in Utah and every other state. Utah does not issue its own EIN. The number is federal and nationwide.

Many founders confuse the federal EIN with Utah state tax registration. They are two separate numbers issued by two separate agencies for two separate purposes.

| Identifier | Issued By | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, payment processors |
| Utah State Tax ID | Utah State Tax Commission | Utah sales tax and state withholding |
| LLC registration | Utah Division of Corporations | Forms the legal entity |

Your federal EIN does not replace Utah state registration. A Utah business that sells taxable goods or hires employees registers with the Utah State Tax Commission in addition to holding an EIN. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for the difference, and [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the full federal process.

## How Do Non-Residents Apply for an EIN <Accent>in Utah?</Accent>

Non-residents apply by faxing IRS Form SS-4 to 855-215-1627 using a passport number on Line 7b. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the only viable path. The IRS issues most error-free EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Utah LLC">Register your LLC with the Utah Division of Corporations through a registered agent with a Utah address. You can complete this online from abroad. This gives you a legal entity and a US business address to attach the EIN to.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your passport (valid and unexpired), your Utah LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US visit is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Utah LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent the errors that cause IRS rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for a Utah LLC

| SS-4 Field | Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Utah LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is malformed international address formatting. The IRS silently rejects addresses that do not map to the SS-4 layout. ein.so reformats your address to an IRS-accepted style. ## What Taxes Apply to a Utah Business <Accent>With an EIN?</Accent>

A Utah business faces both federal and state taxes. Utah applies a 4.65% flat state income tax, separate from federal taxes. Your EIN handles federal reporting with the IRS, while Utah State Tax Commission registration handles state obligations.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Federal Filing: Form 5472 + 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member Utah LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for non-filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not necessarily a tax bill. Confirm your liability with a CPA.</Card>
<Card title="Utah State Income Tax" index={2}>Utah charges a 4.65% flat state income tax. How this applies to a non-resident-owned LLC depends on US-source income and your entity treatment. Confirm with a CPA before you assume any rate applies to you.</Card>
<Card title="Utah Sales Tax" index={3}>Businesses selling taxable goods in Utah register with the Utah State Tax Commission for sales tax. This registration is separate from your federal EIN. Confirm whether your activity creates a Utah sales-tax obligation.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={4}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. This is a federal requirement, free to file at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
</NumberedCards>

ein.so handles the EIN application and does not provide tax advice. Work with a US CPA familiar with non-resident matters to confirm your Utah and federal obligations. Your EIN is the starting point for US tax compliance.

## What Should a Utah Business Do <Accent>After Getting an EIN?</Accent>

After getting an EIN, a Utah business registers for state taxes, files federal reports, and opens banking. The EIN unlocks the next steps, but it does not complete your compliance on its own. Follow this order.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Register With the Utah State Tax Commission">If you collect sales tax or hire employees in Utah, register with the Utah State Tax Commission. This state registration is separate from your federal EIN.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Open a US Business Bank Account">Use your EIN, passport, and Utah LLC documents to open an account with Mercury, Relay, or Wise. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Step>
<Step step="3" title="File Your BOI Report">Most Utah LLCs file a beneficial ownership report with FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/). Filing is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Plan Annual Federal Filing">A foreign-owned single-member LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The non-filing penalty is $25,000.</Step>
</Steps>

If you also need an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number for personal US filing, ein.so offers an ITIN service for $197 Standard and $297 Express. The ITIN is for individuals; the EIN is for the business entity. They serve different purposes and you may need both.
