## Is a Tennessee EIN Different From a <Accent>Federal EIN?</Accent>

No. A Tennessee EIN is the same federal EIN issued by the IRS to businesses in every state. The IRS assigns one 9-digit number (XX-XXXXXXX) that works nationwide. Tennessee does not issue a separate state version of an EIN.

The confusion comes from two different numbers a Tennessee business may hold. The federal EIN comes from the IRS through [Form SS-4](/ss4-form-guide/). A separate Tennessee state tax registration comes from the Tennessee Department of Revenue. These serve different purposes and you may need both.

| Identifier | Issued By | Used For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Federal EIN | IRS | Federal taxes, banking, hiring, payment processors |
| Tennessee state tax ID | TN Department of Revenue | Sales tax, franchise tax, excise tax (if applicable) |

Your EIN does not replace state tax registration. A Tennessee LLC that sells taxable goods registers separately with the Department of Revenue. A non-resident LLC with no Tennessee-based operations may have different obligations. Confirm your exact state registration requirements with a CPA. See [state tax ID](/state-tax-id/) for more detail.

## How Do You Get an EIN <Accent>in Tennessee?</Accent>

You get an EIN in Tennessee through the IRS by one of four methods: online, fax, phone, or mail. The method depends on whether you have an SSN. US residents with an SSN apply online for instant approval. Non-residents without an SSN use the fax method.

| Method | Speed | Cost | Who Uses It |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Online](/ein-online-application/) | Instant | $0 | US residents with an SSN |
| [Fax](/ein-by-fax/) | 4-7 business days | $0 / $49 | Non-residents without an SSN |
| [Phone](/ein-by-phone/) | Same call | $0 | International applicants only |
| [Mail](/ein-by-mail/) | 4-6 weeks | $0 | Applicants who skip fax |

The federal process is identical in every state. Forming your LLC in Tennessee versus another state does not change the EIN steps. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for full instructions and [EIN processing time](/ein-processing-time/) for timing detail.

### Tennessee Business Steps After Your EIN

1. Register with the [Tennessee Department of Revenue](/state-tax-id/) if your activity requires state tax accounts.
2. Obtain any local business licenses for your county or city.
3. File your [BOI report](/boi-filing/) with FinCEN (a federal requirement for most LLCs).
4. Open a [US business bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/) using your EIN.

## How Does a Non-Resident Get an EIN for a <Accent>Tennessee LLC?</Accent>

A non-resident gets an EIN for a Tennessee LLC by filing Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627. You enter your passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN or ITIN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so non-residents must use the fax route.

The IRS does not require US residency, an SSN, or an ITIN to issue an EIN. Foreign founders form a Tennessee LLC through a registered agent, then attach the EIN to that entity. ein.so prepares the SS-4, files it by fax, and emails the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your Tennessee LLC">Register an LLC with the Tennessee Secretary of State through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity. You can complete formation online from outside the United States. Confirm the current state filing fee with the Secretary of State.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need a valid passport, your LLC name and formation date, and your home-country address. No SSN, ITIN, or US residency is required. A foreign national uses only the passport number for identification.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your Tennessee LLC name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this to prevent the address errors that cause silent IRS rejection.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days. Express delivery is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter by email through ein.so.</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for Non-Residents

| SS-4 Field | Entry for a Non-Resident |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your Tennessee LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your home-country address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "e-commerce," "software," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause is malformed international address formatting. See the full [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/), the [EIN without SSN guide](/ein-without-ssn/), and [EIN for non-residents](/ein-for-non-residents/).

## What Tax Filings Does a Tennessee LLC With an EIN <Accent>Require?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member Tennessee LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year with the IRS. The penalty for failing to file is $25,000. This is a federal information return, not necessarily a tax bill. State and federal obligations are separate.

<Card title="Form 5472 + Pro-Forma 1120" index={1}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 annually. The non-filing penalty is $25,000. The EIN identifies the LLC on these returns.</Card>
<Card title="BOI Report to FinCEN" index={2}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to confirm your deadline and avoid penalties. This is a federal requirement, not a Tennessee one.</Card>
<Card title="Tennessee State Taxes" index={3}>Tennessee has no broad personal income tax. It does impose franchise and excise taxes on certain entities and sales tax on taxable goods. Confirm whether your LLC owes these with a CPA and the Department of Revenue.</Card>
<Card title="Federal Income Tax" index={4}>A single-member LLC owned by a non-resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax. Your federal liability depends on whether income is US-sourced. Confirm your situation with a CPA.</Card>
