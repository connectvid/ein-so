## Where Do You Mail Form SS-4 to Get an <Accent>EIN by Mail?</Accent>

Mail Form SS-4 to Internal Revenue Service, Attn: EIN Operation, Cincinnati, OH 45999. This is the only IRS address for international EIN applications by mail. No street number is required; the ZIP code 45999 routes the envelope to the EIN unit.

Non-US residents and applicants with no legal residence or principal place of business in the United States all use this single Cincinnati address. Domestic applicants with a US business address use a different IRS service center, but that case does not apply to most non-resident readers. Write the address exactly as shown, with "Attn: EIN Operation" on its own line, so internal IRS sorting routes the form to the correct desk.

**Internal Revenue Service**
**Attn: EIN Operation**
**Cincinnati, OH 45999**

## How Do You Apply for an EIN by Mail as a <Accent>Non-Resident?</Accent>

Complete Form SS-4 with your passport number on Line 7b, then mail it to the IRS EIN Operation in Cincinnati, OH 45999. The IRS issues the EIN in 4-6 weeks and mails back the CP 575 confirmation letter. No SSN or ITIN is required.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US Entity">Register your US LLC or corporation through a registered agent in states such as Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico. This gives you a legal entity name and a US business address to attach the EIN to. You complete this online from your home country.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your valid passport, your entity formation details (name, state, formation date), and your foreign address. The passport replaces the SSN requirement. No US visa or in-person visit is needed for an EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your entity name on Line 1, your foreign address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. Sign and date the form. The [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/) walks through every line.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Mail the Form to Cincinnati">Send the completed SS-4 to Internal Revenue Service, Attn: EIN Operation, Cincinnati, OH 45999. Use certified or tracked mail to confirm delivery. Keep a copy of the signed form for your records.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Wait for the CP 575 Letter">The IRS processes the form in 4-6 weeks and mails the CP 575 EIN confirmation letter to your Line 4 address. International return mail adds 1-3 weeks. Your nine-digit EIN appears in the XX-XXXXXXX format.</Step>
</Steps>

### Non-Resident SS-4 Field Guide

| SS-4 Field | Non-Resident Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your US LLC or corporation name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your foreign home or business address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC, corporation, or as formed |
| Line 10 (Reason for applying) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | E.g., "software," "e-commerce," "consulting" |

The most common rejection cause for mailed forms is a malformed foreign address on Line 4. The IRS sometimes returns or shelves applications with addresses that do not fit the form layout. A wrong entry on Line 7b is the second-most common error. ein.so completes and faxes SS-4 to prevent both failures. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/) for the complete process.

## What Do You Do With Your EIN After It Arrives by <Accent>Mail?</Accent>

After the CP 575 letter arrives, use your EIN to open a US business bank account, connect payment processors, and meet your annual US tax filings. The EIN is the federal tax ID that links your foreign-owned entity to the US financial system.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="Open a US Bank Account" index={1}>Mercury, Relay, and Wise accept non-resident LLC owners with an EIN and a passport. You receive US account and routing numbers. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="File Form 5472 Annually" index={2}>A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/) with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. This is an information return, not always a tax bill.</Card>
<Card title="File Your BOI Report" index={3}>Most US LLCs report beneficial ownership to FinCEN. Review the [BOI filing rules](/boi-filing/) to avoid penalties. The filing itself is free at fincen.gov/boi.</Card>
