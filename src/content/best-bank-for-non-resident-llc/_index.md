## Why Does a Non-Resident LLC Bank Account Require an <Accent>EIN?</Accent>

A non-resident LLC bank account requires an EIN because the EIN is the federal tax ID that links your company to the US banking and tax system. Banks must verify the business and report account activity to the IRS. No EIN means no business account, with no exceptions across US banks.

The EIN is a 9-digit number in the format XX-XXXXXXX, issued by the IRS at no cost. Non-residents cannot use the IRS online EIN tool, because that tool requires a Social Security Number. Instead, you submit Form SS-4 by fax to the IRS at 855-215-1627, entering your passport number on Line 7b in place of an SSN. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter, called CP 575, which is the exact document banks ask for.

## What Tax Filings Apply After a Non-Resident LLC <Accent>Opens a Bank Account?</Accent>

A foreign-owned single-member US LLC files Form 5472 with a pro-forma Form 1120 each year after it opens a bank account. The penalty for not filing is $25,000. Opening a bank account does not change these obligations. Confirm your specific filings with a CPA.
