## Do HVAC Businesses <Accent>Need an EIN?</Accent>

An HVAC business needs an EIN if it is an LLC, is a corporation, or hires even one employee. A sole-proprietor technician with no employees can file under an SSN, but an EIN is recommended to protect that SSN. The EIN is free from the IRS.

The table below maps common HVAC setups to the EIN requirement. Use it to confirm your situation before you file.

| HVAC Situation | EIN Needed? |
|----------------|-------------|
| HVAC LLC (single-member or multi-member) | Yes (required) |
| HVAC corporation (C-corp or S-corp) | Yes (required) |
| HVAC business with employees | Yes (required) |
| HVAC sole proprietor, no employees | Recommended |
| Non-US resident with a US HVAC LLC | Yes (required) |
| Hobby repair / side income only | No |

An HVAC business uses its EIN for licensing applications, EPA-related compliance records, employee payroll, supplier credit accounts, and service-contract billing. The EIN is the single federal identifier that ties all of these activities to one legal entity. Contractors, manufacturers, and banks all key off this number.

<NumberedCards>

## How Does an HVAC Business <Accent>Get an EIN?</Accent>

## How Does a Non-Resident HVAC Owner <Accent>File Form SS-4?</Accent>

A non-resident HVAC owner files Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, entering a passport number on Line 7b instead of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax route is the only path for non-residents. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Register an HVAC LLC in a US state through a registered agent. This creates the legal entity that the EIN attaches to and gives the business a US address. Non-residents complete this online.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">Collect your valid passport, your LLC name and formation state, your LLC formation date, and your mailing address. No SSN and no ITIN are needed for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter the LLC name on Line 1, your address on Line 4, your full legal name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes the form to prevent rejections.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 goes to the IRS by fax. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns the EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

### SS-4 Field Guide for an HVAC Business

| SS-4 Field | HVAC Entry |
| --- | --- |
| Line 1 (Legal name) | Your HVAC LLC name |
| Line 4 (Mailing address) | Your address |
| Line 7a (Responsible party) | Your full name as on your passport |
| Line 7b (SSN/ITIN/EIN) | Your passport number (non-residents) |
| Line 9a (Entity type) | LLC or corporation |
| Line 10 (Reason) | Started new business |
| Line 16 (Principal activity) | "HVAC contractor" or "heating and air conditioning" |

A common rejection cause is an address that does not match IRS formatting rules. ein.so reformats international addresses to an IRS-accepted style. The EIN connects the company to every part of the US financial and tax system. Banking and compliance both start the moment the EIN arrives.
