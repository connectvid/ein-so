## What Is Form W-7 and the <Accent>ITIN It Creates?</Accent>

Form W-7 is the IRS form that assigns an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a 9-digit number that starts with the digit 9. The IRS issues ITINs to people who have a US tax filing requirement but do not qualify for a Social Security Number.

The ITIN exists so the IRS can process tax returns for non-residents, dependents, and others outside the SSN system. An ITIN does not grant work authorization. An ITIN does not provide immigration status. An ITIN does not make you eligible for Social Security benefits. It serves one purpose: identifying you on US federal tax documents.

The current Form W-7 revision is dated August 2019. The form runs 4 pages, and the IRS instructions run 12 pages. You download both free from irs.gov. The IRS charges $0 for the ITIN itself.

<InfoBox title="Who Needs an ITIN">
<ul>
<li>A non-resident who must file a US federal tax return</li>
<li>A non-resident claiming a tax treaty benefit that requires a US tax ID</li>
<li>A spouse or dependent of a US taxpayer who is being claimed on a return</li>
<li>A foreign person receiving US-source income subject to reporting</li>
<li>A non-resident partner in a US partnership with a filing obligation</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

## How Does Form W-7 Differ From <Accent>Form SS-4 (EIN)?</Accent>

Form W-7 creates a personal tax ID for an individual, while Form SS-4 creates a business tax ID for a company. A non-resident who owns a US LLC files Form SS-4 to get an EIN. That same person files Form W-7 only when they personally must appear on a US tax return.

This distinction matters because many non-residents apply for the wrong number. They form a Wyoming or Delaware LLC, then assume they need an ITIN to open a bank account or run Stripe. They do not. The EIN handles those tasks. The ITIN attaches to you as a human taxpayer, not to your business.

<Card title="Run a US LLC: You Need an EIN" index={1}>An EIN lets your LLC open a US bank account, accept payments, hire contractors, and file the annual [Form 5472](/form-5472-guide/). Apply for it on Form SS-4. ein.so files SS-4 by fax to the IRS for $49 standard.</Card>
<Card title="File a Personal US Return: You May Need an ITIN" index={2}>If you personally must file Form 1040-NR, claim a treaty benefit, or be claimed as a dependent, you need an ITIN. Apply for it on Form W-7 with the return attached.</Card>
<Card title="Open a Bank Account: EIN Is Enough" index={3}>Mercury, Relay, and most non-resident-friendly banks open accounts using the LLC's EIN and your passport. They do not require a personal ITIN for a non-resident-owned LLC. See [EIN for a bank account](/ein-for-bank-account/).</Card>
<Card title="Both at Once: EIN First" index={4}>When you need both, get the EIN first because it is faster (4 to 7 business days) and unblocks banking. Add the ITIN later if a personal filing requirement appears.</Card>

## How Do You Complete <Accent>Form W-7 Line by Line?</Accent>

You complete Form W-7 in 6 sections covering your reason for applying, personal details, foreign address, birth information, and supporting documents. Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport. A name mismatch is the most common rejection cause.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Reason for Submitting (Top of Form)">Check one box, a through h. Box a covers a nonresident alien required to get an ITIN to claim a treaty benefit. Box b covers a nonresident filing a US tax return. Box h covers renewals. The reason box must match your situation exactly.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Name (Line 1a and 1b)">Enter your legal name on Line 1a exactly as printed on your passport. Use Line 1b only if your birth name differs from your current legal name.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Mailing and Foreign Address (Lines 2 and 3)">Enter your mailing address on Line 2 and your foreign country address on Line 3. The IRS mails your CP565 notice to the Line 2 address. International addresses must follow the country's standard format.</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Birth Information and Citizenship (Lines 4, 5, 6a)">Enter your date of birth, country of birth, and country of citizenship. Line 6a asks for your country of citizenship. If you hold dual citizenship, list both countries.</Step>
<Step step="5" title="Foreign Tax ID and Visa (Lines 6b, 6c)">Enter your foreign tax ID number on Line 6b if your country issues one. Enter US visa details on Line 6c only if you hold a US visa. Non-residents abroad usually leave 6c blank.</Step>
<Step step="6" title="Documents and Signature (Line 6d, Bottom)">List the identity documents you are submitting on Line 6d, including the passport number and expiration date. Sign and date the form. Unsigned forms are rejected immediately.</Step>
</Steps>

### Form W-7 Field Reference

| W-7 Field | What to Enter |
| --- | --- |
| Reason box (a-h) | Single box matching your filing reason |
| Line 1a | Legal name exactly as on passport |
| Line 2 | Mailing address for the CP565 notice |
| Line 3 | Foreign (home country) address |
| Line 4 | Date of birth and country of birth |
| Line 6a | Country of citizenship |
| Line 6b | Foreign tax ID number (if issued) |
| Line 6d | Passport number, country, expiration date |

The most common W-7 rejection causes are an unsigned form, a name that does not match the passport, and a missing federal tax return. Attach Form 1040-NR (or the applicable return) unless an exception applies.

## What Documents Does <Accent>Form W-7 Require?</Accent>

Form W-7 requires original identity documents or copies certified by the issuing agency. A valid passport is the only standalone document the IRS accepts. Without a passport, you submit 2 documents from a list of 13, and at least one must show your photograph.

The IRS does not accept notarized copies for ITIN applications. Copies must be certified by the agency that issued the original, such as the passport authority in your home country. A Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) can verify your passport in person and submit copies, which lets you keep your originals during the 7 to 11 week processing window.

<InfoBox title="Acceptable Form W-7 Documents">
<ul>
<li>Passport (standalone, preferred by the IRS)</li>
<li>National identification card showing photo, name, and date of birth</li>
<li>US driver license</li>
<li>Foreign driver license</li>
<li>US state identification card</li>
<li>Foreign voter registration card</li>
<li>Civil birth certificate (required for dependents under 18)</li>
<li>US military identification card</li>
<li>Visa issued by the US Department of State</li>
</ul>
</InfoBox>

<Alert type="warning" title="Document Submission Risks">
<ol>
<li>Mailing an original passport to the IRS leaves you without it for up to 60 days.</li>
<li>The IRS returns originals by standard mail, which can be slow internationally.</li>
<li>Notarized copies are rejected. Only agency-certified copies are accepted.</li>
<li>Using a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) lets you keep your passport during processing.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>

## How Do You Submit <Accent>Form W-7 to the IRS?</Accent>

You submit Form W-7 by mail, through a Certifying Acceptance Agent, or in person at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. The IRS does not accept Form W-7 online or by fax. You attach your federal tax return to the W-7 unless you qualify for a documented exception.

Mailing sends your W-7, tax return, and original documents to the IRS ITIN Operation in Austin, Texas. A CAA verifies your identity, certifies your passport, and forwards the package, so you keep your originals. An IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center reviews documents in person but requires an appointment and a US location.

| Method | You Keep Originals | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mail to IRS Austin | No | Applicants comfortable mailing a passport |
| Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) | Yes | Non-residents protecting their passport |
| IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center | Yes | Applicants already inside the US |

After the IRS approves your application, it mails a CP565 notice containing your assigned ITIN. Processing takes 7 to 11 weeks. Applications filed between January and April sit at the longer end because of peak tax season volume. Compare this to an EIN, which ein.so delivers in 2 to 7 business days. ## Does an ITIN From Form W-7 <Accent>Expire?</Accent>

Yes, an ITIN expires if you do not use it on a US federal tax return for 3 consecutive years. The IRS also retires ITINs in scheduled batches based on their middle digits. An expired ITIN must be renewed on Form W-7 before it can appear on a return again.

To renew, you complete a fresh Form W-7 and check the renewal reason box (Reason h). You submit the same identity documents the original application required. You do not attach a tax return for a renewal-only filing in some cases, but a return is still required when you renew while filing.

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="3-Year Non-Use Rule" index={1}>An ITIN not used on a return for 3 straight years expires automatically. The IRS does not send a reminder. You discover the expiration when a return is delayed.</Card>
<Card title="Scheduled Retirement by Middle Digits" index={2}>The IRS has retired ITINs in batches grouped by middle digits. Check the current IRS schedule before assuming your ITIN is still active.</Card>
<Card title="Renewal on Form W-7" index={3}>Renew by checking Reason box h and resubmitting identity documents. Renew before tax-filing season to avoid return processing delays of several weeks.</Card>
<Card title="EINs Do Not Expire" index={4}>Unlike an ITIN, an EIN never expires. Once the IRS assigns your LLC an EIN, it stays valid for the life of the business. See [how to get an EIN](/how-to-get-ein/).</Card>
</NumberedCards>

An expired ITIN delays your refund and can disallow certain credits until renewal completes. Confirm your ITIN status and renewal timing with a US CPA. ein.so handles ITIN filings and renewals but does not provide tax advice.
