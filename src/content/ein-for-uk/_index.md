## What Should UK Residents Know About <Accent>US-UK Tax Obligations?</Accent>

The UK corporation tax rate is 25% for profits over GBP 250,000, while a US single-member LLC owned by a non-resident is a disregarded entity. The UK-US tax treaty eliminates double taxation and reduces withholding rates to as low as 0% on certain income types. Here are the basics:

### HMRC and IRS Dual Filing Requirements

<NumberedCards>
<Card title="US Tax Filing" index={1}>A single-member LLC owned by a non-resident is a disregarded entity for US tax purposes. You may need to file Form 5472 and Form 1120 (pro forma) annually with the IRS. If your LLC has US-sourced income, you may owe US taxes, subject to treaty relief.</Card>
<Card title="HMRC Self-Assessment" index={2}>UK residents must report worldwide income, including US LLC income, on their self-assessment tax return. You can claim foreign tax credits for any US taxes paid to avoid double taxation under the UK-US tax treaty.</Card>
<Card title="VAT Considerations" index={3}>Your US LLC is not subject to UK VAT for sales made to US customers. However, if your US LLC sells to UK customers, VAT registration may be required. US sales tax is separate from UK VAT and is handled at the state level.</Card>
</NumberedCards>
