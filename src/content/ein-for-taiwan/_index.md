## How Do Taiwanese Citizens Apply for a <Accent>US EIN?</Accent>

Taiwanese citizens submit IRS Form SS-4 by fax to 855-215-1627, using a Taiwan passport number in place of an SSN. The IRS online tool requires an SSN, so the fax method is the path for Taiwanese applicants. The IRS issues most correctly filed EINs within 4-7 business days.

<Steps>
<Step step="1" title="Form Your US LLC">Most Taiwanese founders register an LLC in Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico through a registered agent. This gives you a US business address and a legal entity to attach the EIN to. You form the LLC entirely online from Taiwan.</Step>
<Step step="2" title="Gather Your Documents">You need your Taiwan passport (valid and unexpired), your LLC formation details (name, state, formation date), and your Taiwan address. No UBN or Taiwan business registration is required for the EIN.</Step>
<Step step="3" title="Complete Form SS-4">Enter your US LLC name on Line 1, your Taiwan address on Line 4, your full name as responsible party on Line 7a, and your Taiwan passport number on Line 7b. ein.so completes this for you to prevent errors. See the [SS-4 form guide](/ss4-form-guide/).</Step>
<Step step="4" title="Submit by Fax and Receive Your EIN">The SS-4 is faxed to the IRS at 855-215-1627. Standard delivery is 4-7 business days; Express is 2-3 business days. The IRS returns your EIN assignment letter (CP 575).</Step>
</Steps>

| SS-4 Field | Taiwanese Entry |

## What Should Taiwanese Entrepreneurs Know About <Accent>US Taxes?</Accent>

A single-member US LLC owned by a Taiwan resident is usually a disregarded entity for US federal income tax, meaning the LLC itself pays no US federal income tax on non-US-sourced income. The foreign owner still has annual US information-reporting duties. Confirm your exact filings with a US CPA.

## What Mistakes Do Taiwanese Applicants Make on <Accent>Form SS-4?</Accent>

Taiwanese applicants most often fail by leaving Line 7b blank, entering a UBN where the passport number belongs, or formatting the Taiwan address in a way the IRS rejects. Each error restarts the 4-7 business day timeline.

<Alert type="warning" title="Avoid these four SS-4 errors">
<ol>
<li>Leaving Line 7b empty because you have no SSN. Enter your Taiwan passport number instead.</li>
<li>Entering your 8-digit UBN on Line 7b. The UBN has no US standing and the IRS does not accept it.</li>
<li>Using the IRS online tool, which blocks every applicant without an SSN. Use the fax method to 855-215-1627.</li>
<li>Submitting a Taiwan address in district-first order without a clean postal code, which triggers a silent rejection.</li>
</ol>
</Alert>
