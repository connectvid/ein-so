# Skill 14: Link Building

## Core Principle

"Links are like votes. When other websites link to your page, it tells Google that your page is somehow important." -- Tim Soulo, Ahrefs CMO

"Links are a very strong ranking signal and have a very direct influence on search rankings." -- Ahrefs

This is not theory. Backlinko found that a site's overall link authority (measured by DR) strongly correlates with higher rankings. Ahrefs confirmed: the more backlinks a page has, the more organic traffic it gets from Google. Semrush studies reached the same conclusion.

In the AI era, links serve a dual purpose:
1. **Google rankings:** Direct influence on PageRank and search position
2. **AI visibility:** Mentions and links from authoritative sources act as citation signals for LLMs [Ahrefs, Jan 2026]

## The Four Types of Link Building (Ahrefs Framework)

### Type 1: Adding Links (Foundational -- Start Here)

Manual placement on directories, social profiles, communities.

Ahrefs: "Professional link builders prefer to start with these for brand-new websites. They refer to it as building 'foundational links.'"

These links are mostly nofollow or weak, but they:
- Create brand entity signals Google recognizes
- Create AI "mention" signals [Ahrefs Jan 2026]
- Make paid link building look natural by comparison
- Build the entity stack that tells Google your brand exists [Floate, 2025-07]

Ahrefs caution: "Don't go crazy listing in every imaginable directory. A few dozen where it is natural for your business to be listed. Anything beyond that wastes your time."

### Type 2: Asking for Links (Outreach)

Ahrefs: "The reasoning behind outreach tactics seems fair, but you'll be surprised how low the success rate is. If you get 5 links out of 100 outreach emails, be proud."

Best outreach tactics:
- **Resource page link building** -- find "best free tools" or "recommended resources" lists and request inclusion
- **Broken link building** -- find dead pages, offer yours as replacement
- **Unlinked mentions** -- someone mentioned your brand without linking; ask for the link
- **Guest posting** -- offer to write free content with a link back
- **The Fixer Upper** (Diggity) -- find competitor articles with many backlinks that are outdated, pitch your updated version as a replacement to everyone linking to the old one

Key insight from Ahrefs: "Build relationships BEFORE you need links. A cold email from someone you've talked to on Twitter is 10x more likely to be answered."

Key insight from Gotch [2025-03]: "The number one thing you need to understand is when you reach out to someone, you need to be thinking about a win-win. Every person is thinking about what's in it for me."

### Type 3: Buying Links

Ahrefs is explicit: "Exchanging money for links is quite risky. Google considers it a manipulation of its algorithm and may punish you."

Reality check: link buying is widespread. The entire link marketplace ecosystem exists because it works. Google's approach has shifted toward neutralization over toxicity [Floate, 2025-07] -- bad links are more likely to be ignored than to tank your site. But page-level penalties still happen.

Keep it clean if you do buy:
- Mixed anchor texts (branded, naked URL, generic, partial match, exact match)
- Mix of homepage and inner page link targets
- Reasonable price points that don't signal obvious spam
- Natural-looking link velocity -- not 200 links in one month
- Vet the selling site thoroughly (see vetting section below)

### Type 4: Earning Links

Ahrefs: "You earn links when people link naturally without you asking. This requires something truly noteworthy."

Gotch's "Reverse Silo" approach [2025-03]: Instead of pointing links directly at money pages (risky footprint), acquire backlinks to linkable assets. Those assets internally link to your commercial pages. A rising tide lifts all ships.

Proven linkable asset frameworks [Gotch, 2025-03]:
- **Free tools / calculators** -- the #1 proven framework across industries
- **Data-driven content** -- original research, curated statistics pages
- **Rankings / lists** -- "Top 50 X" with filtering mechanisms
- **Animated infographics** -- high effort, massive return
- **Glossaries / term databases** -- proven to attract links at scale
- **Definitive guides** -- epic length implies authority

Diggity's linkable asset framework [2022-10]:
- Know your audience (what do they share?)
- Reverse engineer what's already working in your niche
- Collect data from multiple sources for credibility
- Make it visually beautiful -- looks matter for linkability
- Share it everywhere, then rinse and repeat annually

## Link Quality Factors (5 Factors)

Five factors that make a backlink valuable [Ahrefs]:

1. **Authority of the linking site** -- Higher DR/DA = more link equity passed. "Authority is king." [Floate, 2025-07]
2. **Relevance of the linking page** -- A relevant DR 40 site can outperform an irrelevant DR 80 site. "One good link from a relevant website can be more impactful than millions of low-quality links." -- Google's John Mueller [cited by Gotch, 2024-10]
3. **Placement within main content** -- Links in body copy pass more equity than sidebar, footer, or author bio links. Higher placement in the article = more value [Authority Hacker, 2024-10]
4. **Anchor text** -- Descriptive anchors that vary naturally. Over-optimization triggers Penguin penalties.
5. **Follow vs nofollow status** -- Follow preferred, but nofollow still provides value for entity recognition and traffic

### The Authority vs. Relevance Axis (Floate)

Floate's framework [2025-07]: Plot every link opportunity on a graph where X-axis is relevance and Y-axis is authority. The sweet spot is high on both, but that's rare. The rule:
- **The more relevant a link is, the lower DR it needs to be effective**
- **The less relevant a link is, the higher DR it needs to be effective**
- Never go below the threshold on both axes simultaneously

### The Relevancy Pyramid (Gotch)

Gotch's framework [2024-10, 2025-03]: Prioritize link opportunities by relevance, starting at the top:

1. **100% match** -- Same niche + same location (for local). Tiny pool but most powerful.
2. **Industry match** -- Same broad industry, different specific niche (e.g., plumber getting link from electrician)
3. **Topic-adjacent** -- Related industries (home services getting link from real estate)
4. **Location match** -- Same city/region but different industry (local chamber, softball league)
5. **General authority** -- High-authority sites with no specific relevance (Forbes, university .edu)

Start at the top, exhaust each tier, then move down. Create a "Dream 100" list of your most valuable link prospects.

## Anchor Text Strategy

### Anchor Text Types

1. **Branded** -- Your business/brand name (e.g., "Diggity Marketing")
2. **Naked URL** -- The actual URL (e.g., diggitymarketing.com/guide)
3. **Generic** -- "click here," "go here," "this website," "learn more"
4. **Partial match** -- Contains one or more words from target keyword (e.g., "learn about dog food")
5. **Exact match** -- Exactly your target keyword (e.g., "best dog food")
6. **LSI/Topical** -- Related keywords one topic level up (e.g., "pet diet" for a dog food page)
7. **Brand + keyword** -- Combination (e.g., "WebMD creatine guide")
8. **Title anchor** -- The exact title of the target page
9. **Image anchor** -- Google uses the ALT tag as anchor text

### Natural Distribution (Gotch's Safe Ratios)

Gotch's anchor text ratios [2017-07, still used and validated]:

| Type | Percentage |
|------|-----------|
| Branded | 70%+ |
| Naked URL | ~20% |
| Generic | ~5% |
| LSI / Partial match / Title | 1-5% |
| Exact match | Below 1% |

Gotch: "If you follow these ratios, I can assure you you'll never have to deal with any type of algorithmic penalties or even manual penalties."

### Diggity's Data-Driven Approach

Diggity [2023-04] rejects cookie-cutter ratios entirely: "Every niche is different. You must find a distribution that looks natural for YOUR niche."

His process:
1. Plug top 5 competitors' pages into Ahrefs Site Explorer
2. Go to Anchors tab, export all anchors
3. Categorize each anchor by type (target, branded, URL, topical, misc, NA)
4. Create pivot table showing distribution percentages
5. Average across all 5 competitors
6. **Match that distribution** -- it's what Google already considers natural for your niche

### Critical Anchor Text Rules

**Never over-optimize exact match.** This is the #1 cause of Penguin penalties.

**Never repeat the same target anchor twice.** "How likely is it that 110 different people linking to you use the exact same target anchor? Not very likely at all." [Diggity, 2023-04]

**Cycle your anchors** [Gotch, 2017-07]: After using an exact match anchor, cycle through branded > partial match > naked URL > generic > branded before using exact match again.

**Match anchor to link type** [Diggity, 2023-04]:
- Blog articles = descriptive/target anchors
- Link lists = branded or naked URLs
- Sidebars = topical or empty anchors
- Forum posts = URL or SEO title
- Blog comments = author name
- Directories / profiles / donations = always branded or naked URL

**Place your best anchors on your best links** [Gotch, 2017-07]: If you land a Forbes link, use a keyword-rich anchor. For directories, profiles, and lower-value links, always use branded or naked URL.

**Watch capitalization** [Floate, 2025-07]: Google flags unnatural capitalization patterns. "Stake Is A New Cryptocurrency Online Casino" is a dead giveaway that an SEO placed the link. Use natural lowercase: "stake is a new cryptocurrency online casino." Seven out of nine links with capitalized anchors going to one site = over-optimization signal.

**Use co-occurrence** [Gotch, 2017-07]: Put your target keyword in close proximity to your anchor text even when using generic anchors. "If you are looking for more information about creatine, go here." Google reads the surrounding context.

**Use synonyms** [Diggity, 2023-04]: When you've maxed out on target anchors, use synonyms. Google understands "car," "automobile," and "vehicle" mean the same thing.

### Anchor Text Analysis (Competitive Analysis Process)

When stuck on a keyword, analyze competitor anchor text distribution [Osborne/Gotch method]:

1. Export backlinks for top 3 competitors from Semrush/Ahrefs
2. Categorize every anchor: exact match > partial match > brand > naked > random
3. Calculate percentages for each category
4. Compare your percentages to competitor averages
5. Identify where you're over-optimized (reduce) or under-optimized (build more)
6. Work on percentages, not raw counts -- a site with 80 links and 3% exact match needs different handling than one with 200 links and 3%

Deploy this analysis when:
- You're stuck on a competitive keyword and can't break through
- It's a highly competitive niche (attorneys, roofing, insurance)
- You need to diagnose why rankings plateaued despite building more links

## Link Velocity

### What is Natural?

Link velocity = the rate at which you acquire new backlinks over time.

There is no universal "safe" number. Floate [2025-07] on why linear advice is impossible: "What is your existing link profile? How many pages do you have? How many are you publishing? What type of links? How old is your domain? What niche? What geo? There's so many contributing factors that it is really hard to give any sort of linear advice."

### General Guidelines

- **New sites (Month 1-3):** Focus on foundational links only. 5-15 new referring domains per month is natural for a new site that's actively being promoted.
- **Growing sites (Month 3-6):** 10-25 new referring domains per month. Mix of directories, guest posts, and earned mentions.
- **Established sites (6+ months):** Scale based on content publishing velocity. Floate's rule of thumb: "If you've got a hundred pages and you're publishing a new blog post every week, then maybe look at only doing five links a week."

### Red Flags

- 200 links appearing in one month (obvious Fiverr DR inflation)
- All links from the same IP range or registrar
- Sudden spike followed by nothing -- Google watches velocity patterns over time
- Building 7+ links to the same page in the same week

### Floate's Velocity Rules [2025-07]

- Do not count entity stacking links (citations, social profiles) toward your velocity calculations -- unless you're building 100+ in one day
- Randomize delivery timing. Don't have all your monthly links go live on the same day.
- Common sense wins. If your site has 10 pages and you build 50 links in a week, that's suspicious regardless of any formula.

## Tier-1 Backlinks in the AI Era

### What Moves the Needle in 2026

The link building landscape has shifted. Low-authority "mommy blog" links no longer move rankings [Gotch, 2025-03]. What works:

1. **Digital PR links** -- Forbes, Inc., HubSpot, NYT, industry publications. These are the most powerful links and cannot be bought.
2. **Editorial mentions on authoritative sites** -- When a journalist or content creator naturally references your work.
3. **Links from companies in your niche** -- Authority Hacker [2024-10]: "The most valuable links you can build are from the brands selling products in your niche."
4. **Expert roundup / quote links** -- HARO, Quoted, Source of Sources placements.
5. **Podcast appearance links** -- Show notes link back to your site with organic mentions.

### Homepage Links are Underrated

Gotch [2024-10]: "Don't underestimate the power of homepage backlinks. If you study the link profiles of the most successful websites online, you see that most of their backlinks point to their homepages."

If your site is structured well with proper internal linking, homepage backlink equity flows to all pages.

Floate [2025-07]: "If unsure where to point a link, point it at the homepage. Do it as a quote, an interview, something that merges two websites and powers up your entity stacking."

## How Links Affect AI Visibility

Links have always been votes for Google. In the AI era, they serve an additional function:

1. **Mentions on high-authority sites = citation signals for LLMs.** When ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude answers a question, they draw from content that is widely referenced. Links from trusted sources increase the probability your brand appears in AI responses.
2. **Entity stacking across trusted platforms** creates consensus signals that LLMs use to validate your brand's existence and expertise.
3. **Digital PR placements** on major publications are the most likely to surface in AI-generated answers because LLMs weight authoritative sources heavily.
4. **Foundational links** (directories, profiles) contribute to entity recognition across AI training data.

The dual strategy: Build links that satisfy Google's ranking algorithm AND create the citation/mention patterns that LLMs use for their responses.

## Link Building for Different Site Types

### Play 1: Lead-Gen Sites (wyomingllc.co, ein.so, itin.so)

Risk tolerance: LOW. These pay the bills.

- Focus on white-hat earned links and foundational links
- Digital PR through expert quotes (HARO, Quoted)
- Create one definitive linkable asset per site (calculator, statistics page, definitive guide)
- Build internal link equity from tools/content to money pages
- Guest posting on business formation / entrepreneur blogs
- Target: DR 30-40 within 6 months through clean link building

### Play 2: Link Network Sites (backlinky.io, maillead.io, suparank.co)

Risk tolerance: MEDIUM. These sites ARE the link building product.

Link selling readiness requirements:
- 3+ months since first indexing (not registration)
- 500+ monthly organic visitors on Ahrefs
- At least 5 tools/pages live and working
- Mixed backlink profile (directories + social + editorial mix)
- No suspicious link velocity spikes
- About page with real identity
- Contact page with real email address
- No GSC manual actions

Niche relevance matters for link selling:

| Site | Primary Link Buyers |
|------|-------------------|
| suparank.co | SEO tools, content marketing, link building services |
| serpapis.com | SEO tools, web dev tools, API products |
| maillead.io | Email verification services, outreach tools, B2B SaaS |
| backlinky.io | SEO tools, content tools, marketing SaaS |

Never sell links to clearly irrelevant niches -- one irrelevant sale tanks your reputation.

Marketplace listings (when ready):
- **Tier 1:** Adsy.com, Collaborator.pro, WhitePress (30% commission each)
- **Tier 2:** Direct outreach to SEO agencies (no commission), SEO Facebook groups, Twitter/X with Ahrefs screenshots

Pricing by DR + Traffic:

| DR | Traffic | Price |
|----|---------|-------|
| 20-30 | 500-1K | $50-80 |
| 30-40 | 1K-2K | $80-150 |
| 40-50 | 2K-5K | $150-250 |
| 50-60 | 5K-10K | $250-400 |
| 60+ | 10K+ | $400-600+ |

Add 30% for commercial niches (SEO, finance, ecommerce). Subtract 20% for entertainment/fun niches.

### Play 3: SaaS Sites (timetrackingsoftware.io, youtubetranscripts.co)

Risk tolerance: MEDIUM. Competing against well-funded incumbents.

- Build links from competitor ecosystems (the companies whose products you review)
- Comparison pages and alternative pages naturally attract links from people searching for options
- G2, Capterra, GetApp listings are foundational links AND trust signals
- AppSumo / Product Hunt launches generate high-authority links
- Target: Get DR into the ballpark of small blogs ranking in your SaaS niche (typically 30s-50s)

## Guest Posting

### When It Works

Guest posting works when you:
- Offer genuinely useful content the host site doesn't have
- Pitch content they can actually rank for (Diggity's rankable content approach)
- Provide value beyond just "free article" (link surplus, social shares, expertise)

### How to Find Opportunities

1. Search operators: `"write for us" + [niche]`, `"guest post" + [niche]`, `"contribute" + [niche]`
2. Find competitor guest posts in Ahrefs (check referring domains for blog posts)
3. Use ChatGPT to identify top brands in your niche, then check if they accept contributors [Authority Hacker, 2024-10]
4. Use Ahrefs Content Gap to find keywords competitors rank for but the target site doesn't -- pitch content to fill those gaps [Diggity, 2022-10]

### The Rankable Content Pitch (Diggity)

"If you wanna get a link from HubSpot, figure out their weaker competitors. Use Ahrefs' content gap tool to find keywords those sites rank for but HubSpot doesn't. Pitch to write that content for them, free of cost."

This converts because you're offering SEO value, not just content.

### Guest Post Quality Requirements

Floate [2025-07] on what makes a guest post actually pass equity:
- The page must rank for at least some keywords -- orphan/isolated pages get neutralized
- It needs internal links pointing to it from the host site
- It needs social signals and ideally some tier-2 links
- The host site should not be publishing 100+ posts per day
- Content should not be obviously AI-generated at scale

"Rank your damn guest posts. Give them the ability to rank, get traffic, have signals from Google itself." [Floate, 2025-07]

## HARO / Journalist Strategies

### Platforms

- **HARO (Help A Reporter Out)** -- journalists request expert quotes. Free to sign up.
- **Quoted** -- similar platform, growing in use
- **Source of Sources** -- newer alternative
- **Connectively** -- HARO's successor platform

### How to Get Featured [Diggity, 2022-10]

1. **Speed is everything.** Respond to queries within hours, not days. Journalists have deadlines.
2. **Don't give half-assed answers.** Answer every question as if you know you're getting the backlink.
3. **Cast a wide net.** Don't wait for queries in only your exact niche. You have expertise in business, management, productivity, and more.
4. **Include your credentials.** Tell them exactly how to cite you: "Adam Enfroy, founder of adamenfroy.com"
5. **Use a reusable template** [Authority Hacker, 2024-10]: Pre-build a HARO template in HubSpot Sales or Gmail so you only need to customize the expert quote for each response.

### What Makes You PR-Ready

- About page with real name, photo, credentials
- A clear one-sentence expertise statement
- Previous media mentions (even small ones help)
- A professional headshot
- Active social media presence
- Awards or certifications (optional but helpful)

## Niche Edits

### What They Are

A niche edit (also called "curated link" or "link insertion") is when you pay to have your link added to an already-published, already-indexed page on another site. Unlike a guest post, no new content is created.

### Why They Can Be Effective

- The page already has authority, backlinks, and rankings
- The link appears in established content (more natural than a brand-new guest post)
- Floate [2025-07] recommends: "Niche edits on ranking posts and pages that have direct exact match keywords -- that's totally fine and encouraged."

### Risks

- Over-reliance on one tactic creates a detectable pattern
- Some niche edit providers inject links into pages the site owner doesn't know about (hacked/injected links = toxic)
- Quality varies wildly -- always vet the host page yourself

### How to Evaluate a Niche Edit Opportunity

- Does the host page rank for relevant keywords?
- Does it have its own referring domains?
- Is the site still actively maintained?
- Is the page in a normal category (not /sponsored/ or /paid/)?
- Would this link drive relevant referral traffic? [Gotch, 2025-03: "The referral traffic principle"]

## Link Audit

### How to Check Your Backlink Profile

1. **Export all backlinks** from Ahrefs or Semrush
2. **Review anchor text distribution** -- look for over-optimization
3. **Check link velocity** -- any unnatural spikes?
4. **Evaluate referring domain quality** -- DR distribution, relevance
5. **Identify toxic patterns** -- same IP range, same registrar, same template

### Toxic Link Identification

Red flags for toxic links:
- Links from sites with Chinese or Indonesian spam in their history [Floate, 2025-07]
- Links from sites that were previously redirected to spam
- Links from hacked sites with injected JavaScript
- Links from sites faking traffic through login page keywords or error code keywords [Floate, 2025-07]
- Links from PBNs with obvious footprints (same template, same hosting, same registrar)
- Links in orphaned pages with no internal links, no social signals, no other external links

### Disavow Process

Floate [2025-07]: "Disavow takes 30-90 days to kick in." During that time, negative links still count. Use Google's Disavow Tool for clearly toxic links, but don't disavow everything that looks slightly off -- Google ignores most spam signals automatically (Telegram spam, mass directory spam, etc.).

## Council Expert Insights

### Nathan Gotch (Gotch SEO) -- Systems-Based Link Building

Key principles [2024-10, 2025-03]:

1. **Identify your expert.** "A strong personal brand can be a huge backlink driver. Gary Vaynerchuk has over 16,000 referring domains to his personal website." Build a personal brand around a subject matter expert.

2. **The Relevancy Pyramid.** Prioritize the most relevant link opportunities first. "Relevance is king when it comes to link building."

3. **The Merger Technique.** Find undervalued websites with great link profiles, acquire them, and 301 redirect to your site. "Must 301 redirect based on link distribution" -- map redirects page-by-page based on where the backlinks hit.

4. **The Dream 100.** Identify the top 100 websites you want links from. Treat them like gold. Develop relationships slowly.

5. **Use AI for prospecting.** Deep Research in ChatGPT can find link opportunities in minutes that used to take hours. Upload your SOP and let it prospect at scale.

6. **It's a marathon, not a sprint.** "GotchSEO.com is over 10 years old and has a DR of 73. Just stay the course."

### Charles Floate -- Aggressive Link Building + Anti-Spam

Key principles [2025-07]:

1. **Authority wins.** "It doesn't matter about intent. It doesn't matter about content. It just matters about the authority behind the website."

2. **Google neutralizes before penalizing.** "Google is just preferring to neutralize links over making them toxic. You won't see an increase or decrease. That is going to be 99% of the case if you're not doing proper vetting."

3. **Stop building orphan links.** A page with no internal links, no social signals, no tier-2 links will be automatically neutralized by Google.

4. **Diversification is not just pillow links.** Diversify: link types (guest posts, niche edits, PR, infographics), geo locations, meta tags (follow, nofollow, sponsored, UGC), CMS platforms (not all WordPress blogs), content types, relevancy levels, and velocity.

5. **Entity stacking is mandatory for YMYL.** Build branded profiles, citations, and references across trusted platforms. "Jason Barnard recommends 30-60 different types of these before Google will validate that entity."

6. **PBN risks in 2025+.** Google comes after link networks, not marketplaces. But PBN footprints (same template, same hosting, same registrar) are detectable. PBNs still work for some operators but carry real risk for money sites.

### Matt Diggity -- Data-Driven Link Building

Key principles [2022-10, 2023-04]:

1. **Reverse engineer competitor anchor text.** Don't use cookie-cutter ratios. Every niche has its own natural distribution.

2. **Never repeat target anchors.** Each target anchor should be unique.

3. **The best links on the planet are the ones you can't pay for.** Digital PR, earned editorial links, and HARO placements outperform everything else.

4. **Link exchanges work if not excessive.** Google says "excessive" link exchanges are a scheme. Moderate, relevant exchanges are fine. Pro tip: respond to inbound link requests from relevant sites and propose exchanges.

5. **Link surplus strategy.** Build enough outgoing link relationships that you can offer links TO others, which makes them far more likely to link back. "The ability to give links will increase your ability to get them."

6. **Guest posts should rank.** Use the rankable content pitch -- find content gaps on the target site and offer to fill them.

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Do

### PBN Risks

PBNs (Private Blog Networks) can still work but carry real deindex risk [Floate, 2025-07]:
- Google targets link networks with obvious footprints
- Same template across sites = detectable
- Same hosting provider / IP range = detectable
- Same registrar / registration patterns = detectable
- PBNs on hacked domains = extremely risky

For Play 1 sites: DO NOT use PBNs. Risk is too high.
For Play 2 sites: Test carefully if at all. Remember: deindexed network site = worthless product.

### Link Farms

- Mass-produced links from the same IP block
- Fiverr "500 backlinks for $5" packages
- Automated link building tools (GSA, etc.) for anything other than tier-3 [Floate, 2025-07]

### Obvious Footprints [Floate, 2025-07]

- All links using exact match anchors with capitalized words
- All guest posts in a "/sponsored/" or "/paid/" subfolder
- All links from the same CMS (only WordPress blogs)
- All links going live on the same day
- Building links only to money pages (use the reverse silo)
- Every guest post targeting the same "reverse swim" keyword as your money page

### Over-Optimization Signals

- Exact match anchor text above 1-3% of total profile
- Building 90+ links to a page when no competitor has more than 5
- Ignoring consensus: if competitors have zero referring domains to their category pages, building 50 links to yours is a red flag [Floate, 2025-07]
- All links from one geographic region when your business is local

### Other Mistakes

- Building links before on-page is solid (fix the foundation first)
- Ignoring internal linking (pages with exact-match anchor internal links have 5x more traffic -- Ahrefs data)
- Never auditing your backlink profile
- Building links without a strategy (random links < systematic campaign)

## Vetting Sites Before Buying/Building Links [Floate, 2025-07]

Before spending money on any link, check:

1. **Current publishing quality.** Is recent content relevant to the site's niche? Random casino/solar panel articles on a home & garden blog = run.
2. **Bad topic neighborhoods.** Viagra, unregulated casinos, diet pills = stay away unless you're in those niches.
3. **Publishing frequency.** 100+ articles/day = your link gets diluted. One per day = much better equity transfer.
4. **Do recent posts rank?** Check last 10-20 posts -- if none have any keywords in Ahrefs after 72+ hours, the site's equity transfer is weak.
5. **Wayback Machine history.** Was it always this site? Any parking pages, hacked periods, Indonesian/Chinese spam, or redirects to spam?
6. **Ahrefs/Semrush profile.** Natural traffic trajectory? Any suspicious spikes?
7. **Fake traffic detection.** Login pages (Disney Plus login), software error codes, and other tricks inflate Ahrefs traffic numbers artificially.
8. **Relevant traffic.** Translate top keywords if in another language. Make sure no toxic topics are hiding.
9. **DR powered by redirects?** Check if they're using domain redirects to inflate DR and reselling the authority.
10. **Mass AI content.** All posts have AI images, identical structure, no internal linking, no schema = low quality.
11. **Pages vs. traffic ratio.** 2,000 posts with 2,000 traffic = 1 visit per post = poor quality. 19 posts with 10K traffic = excellent.

## How Link Building Drives LLM Visibility

Link building in 2026 serves a dual purpose: Google rankings AND LLM citation signals.

### Links = Mentions = AI Citations

Ahrefs (Jan 2026): "AI assistants value 'mentions' -- the number of times your brand and products are mentioned on third-party websites."

Every backlink is also a brand mention. Every mention trains LLMs to associate your brand with your topic. The more high-quality sites mention you, the more confidently LLMs recommend you.

### Which Link Types Impact LLMs Most

| Link Type | Google Impact | LLM Impact | Priority |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Editorial mention in news/PR | High (authority) | **Very High** (high-trust training data) | #1 |
| Guest post on relevant blog | Medium-High | **High** (topical association) | #2 |
| Resource page inclusion | Medium | **High** (listicle citations are gold for AI) | #3 |
| Podcast/YouTube mention | Low-Medium (link) | **Very High** (transcript citations) | #4 |
| Reddit discussion mention | Low (nofollow) | **Very High** (Reddit is top LLM source) | #5 |
| Directory listing | Low | **Medium** (entity recognition signal) | #6 |
| Review site profile | Low-Medium | **High** (ratings influence AI recommendations) | #7 |

### Key Insight: Nofollow Links Matter for AI

Google may discount nofollow links for PageRank. But LLMs don't care about follow/nofollow status. A nofollow link from Reddit, a podcast transcript, or a G2 review is just as valuable for AI citation as a dofollow editorial link. This means:

- Reddit posts (nofollow) = high LLM value despite low Google link value
- Forum discussions (often nofollow) = LLM training data
- Social media mentions (nofollow) = entity signals for AI systems

**Build for both.** The ideal link building strategy earns both dofollow links (Google) AND nofollow mentions (LLMs).
