# Skill 13: Third-Party Signals for LLM Visibility

## Why Third-Party Signals Matter More Than Ever

LLMs trust external validation more than your own claims. This is the single most important shift from traditional SEO to AI search optimization.

> "AI trusts external validation more than your website. If you're making claims without any evidence, it's no bueno." -- Kevin Indig, Discovered Labs (Oct 2025)

> "AI assistants value 'mentions' -- the number of times your brand and products are mentioned on third-party websites." -- Ahrefs (Jan 2026)

In traditional SEO, the game was: create content on your site, buy backlinks to it, improve page rank. In AI search, the game has expanded. LLMs perform reasoning chains before citing you. They check: can this claim be corroborated by others? If your website says you are the best, but nobody else on the internet agrees, you will not appear in AI answers.

**The fundamental principle:** If your claims cannot be supported by external sources -- reviews, Reddit discussions, news coverage, community conversations -- you will never make it into AI answers. Period.

### What the research shows

- Reddit is consistently the #1-2 most cited domain across LLMs (Indig, Oct 2025)
- ChatGPT biases toward Wikipedia and Reddit (Indig, Oct 2025)
- Gemini biases toward YouTube because Google owns YouTube (Indig, Oct 2025)
- Tier-one publication links double as brand mentions AND algorithmic SEO value (Chris Pantelli / Income School, Oct 2025)
- Brand mentions may become the new backlink for LLM visibility (Pantelli, Oct 2025)
- Reddit signed partnerships with both OpenAI and Google -- every post and comment feeds AI training data (Ahrefs, Mar 2026)

## Reddit Strategy (Detailed)

### Why Reddit Is Priority #1

Reddit is the single most impactful third-party platform for LLM visibility because:

1. **Data partnerships:** Reddit signed data-licensing deals with both OpenAI and Google. Every post, every comment, every brand mention feeds AI systems directly. (Ahrefs, Mar 2026)
2. **Citation frequency:** Reddit is consistently the #1-2 most cited domain in LLM answers. (Indig, Oct 2025)
3. **Google SERP presence:** Google has been aggressively surfacing Reddit content in SERPs, especially under the "Discussions and forums" filter. Even Tom's Hardware lost visibility to forum threads in recent core updates. (Aleyda/Lily Ray, Jul 2025)
4. **Deleted content still cited:** Even deleted Reddit posts and accounts still get cited by LLMs. The content persists in training data. (Indig, Oct 2025)
5. **Real human signal:** Both Google and LLMs prioritize authentic human conversations over polished marketing content. Reddit is the largest indexable source of this.

### Account Management

You cannot post effectively on Reddit with fresh accounts. Primary subreddits (the ones with your ICP) have automod rules that filter:

- Accounts below a certain karma threshold
- Accounts younger than a certain age (often 30-90 days minimum)
- Accounts with no prior activity in the subreddit

**Account infrastructure requirements:**
- Use aged accounts (minimum 6-12 months old)
- Accounts must have legitimate karma from genuine participation
- Each account needs a distinct posting history and personality
- Multiple accounts needed for the discussion seeding framework (see below)
- Never use a single account to repeatedly mention the same brand

**What NOT to do:**
- Do NOT use brand new accounts to post about your product
- Do NOT use automated tools that mass-generate comments -- this is getting detected and banned
- Do NOT be overtly promotional -- Reddit communities will destroy you
- Do NOT use the same account to both post and recommend your own product
- Do NOT spam the same message across multiple subreddits
- Do NOT buy software that "auto-finds subreddits and auto-creates comments" -- this produces detectable, low-quality output (Indig, Oct 2025)

### Subreddit Targeting

Identify primary subreddits where your ICP lives:

1. **AI visibility audit approach:** Run your target queries through ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity. Scrape which Reddit posts/subreddits are being cited. Target those subreddits. (Indig, Oct 2025)
2. **Google SERP approach:** Search your target queries in Google, switch to the "Forums" tab. Note which subreddits rank. (Indig, Oct 2025)
3. **Direct research:** Search your product category + "reddit" in Google. Find the 5-10 subreddits where real discussions happen.

Primary subreddits (tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands of members) are harder to post in but deliver the most impact. These are the ones LLMs cite most frequently.

### Content Seeding Framework

This is the framework Indig uses to naturally bridge to product recommendations without triggering Reddit's spam detection. It requires multiple accounts working together:

**Account A -- The Problem Poster:**
Posts a genuine question or describes a problem that your ICP faces. Does NOT mention any product. Just describes the pain point authentically.

**Account B -- The Resonator:**
Replies to Account A's post, resonating with the problem. "Yeah, I had the same issue." Shares their own experience struggling with the problem. Hints that they found a solution but does not name it yet.

**Account C -- The Curious Asker:**
Replies to Account B: "How did you solve it?" or "What ended up working for you?" This creates the natural opening.

**Account D -- The Natural Recommender:**
Replies with a nuanced comparison: "I tried [Competitor A] and [Competitor B]. Competitor A had [specific feature] but Competitor B had [specific feature] which I preferred. I ended up going with [your product/client] because [specific reason]."

Key principles of this framework:
- The recommendation must feel neutral, not promotional
- Include real competitor names and honest assessments
- Mention specific features, pricing, use cases -- not generic praise
- The recommender account should have a posting history that makes sense
- The entire thread should look like a natural conversation

> "It's like a neutral bridge to the solution and it's not blatant promotion which is just not going to work on Reddit." -- Indig (Oct 2025)

### Entity Richness in Reddit Content

Whether posting or commenting, ensure your Reddit content is entity-rich for LLM retrieval:

- **3-6 concrete entities per 100 words** -- tools, standards, metrics, capabilities, integrations, use cases (Indig, Oct 2025)
- Don't just say "buy this product" -- talk about specific features, specific integrations, specific pricing, specific use cases
- Mention alternatives and competitors by name (this satisfies the LLM query fan-out process)
- Include specific numbers: "I sent 10,000 cold emails," "we have 50 employees," "pricing starts at $29/month"

**Why entity richness matters:** When LLMs process a user query, they perform "query fan-out" -- splitting the query into sub-queries with additional constraints (company size, budget, integrations, use cases). Your Reddit content needs to contain those entities so it gets matched during the retrieval process.

### Ranking Comments

You can influence which comment appears as "Best" (sorted by default) under high-traffic posts:

- The top comment position gets the most impressions and is most likely to be scraped by LLMs
- Upvotes are relative to the current best performer -- you only need to outperform the current top comment
- Early timing matters -- commenting early on a post that will get traction gives you a head start

## Review Site Strategy

### Why Reviews Matter for AI

LLMs use review sites as external validation signals. When ChatGPT recommends a product, it often checks whether that product has reviews on trusted platforms.

> "The number of reviews and average rating has a stupid amount of control over your AI SEO rankings." -- Matt Diggity (Jul 2025)

### Platform Priority

| Platform | Best For | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| G2 | B2B SaaS | Most cited review platform in B2B LLM answers |
| Capterra | B2B SaaS | High trust, frequently cited alongside G2 |
| Trustpilot | B2C, Services | Broad coverage, well-known trust signal |
| Google Business Profile | Local, Services | Directly feeds Google AI and Gemini |
| Yelp | Local, Consumer | Still relevant for local business queries |

### Review Collection Framework

1. **Ask at the moment of delight:** Trigger review requests immediately after a positive outcome (successful onboarding, first win, support resolution)
2. **Make it frictionless:** Direct links to the exact review form, not the platform homepage
3. **Spread across platforms:** Don't concentrate all reviews on one platform. LLMs cross-reference multiple sources
4. **Respond to every review:** Both positive and negative. This shows active engagement
5. **Pull reviews into owned content:** When creating blog posts or landing pages, embed real user quotes from G2/Trustpilot/Reddit with links to the original source. This lets LLMs verify your claims by following the citation. (Indig, Oct 2025)

### Maintaining Star Ratings

- Aim for 4.0+ stars minimum across all platforms
- A drop below 4.0 can cause LLMs to deprioritize or negatively frame your product
- Volume matters: 50+ reviews on G2 carries more weight than 5 reviews at 5.0 stars
- Recency matters: Reviews from the last 6 months carry more weight than reviews from 3 years ago

## YouTube Strategy

### Why YouTube Matters for AI

- **Gemini bias:** Google owns YouTube. Gemini and Google AI Mode draw heavily from YouTube content and transcripts. (Indig, Oct 2025)
- **Transcript indexing:** YouTube transcripts are text that LLMs can parse, extract passages from, and cite
- **Multimodal signals:** Content that includes video gives AI "more to work with" and increases citation likelihood (Diggity, Aug 2025)

### Tactical Approach

1. **Always enable transcripts:** Auto-generated or manual, transcripts must be available. LLMs read transcripts, not video content
2. **Include key entities in spoken content:** When discussing your product/topic on video, speak specific names, features, comparisons, pricing -- these become searchable/extractable text
3. **Structured video content:** Use clear sections (visible in YouTube chapters) that mirror H2/H3 structure. Each section should be a self-contained passage that can be extracted independently
4. **Embed video on your site pages:** Then add a transcript section below the video on the same page. This gives both Google and LLMs the video context + text context together. Diggity showed a page where the video transcript content was directly cited in AI overviews. (Diggity, Aug 2025)
5. **YouTube for competitor comparisons:** "Product A vs Product B" videos rank well both in YouTube search and get cited in LLM comparison queries

## Wikipedia Strategy

### Why Wikipedia Matters

ChatGPT biases heavily toward Wikipedia for entity understanding. If your brand/product has a Wikipedia page, LLMs treat it as a high-confidence verified entity.

### Notability Requirements

You cannot just create a Wikipedia page. Wikipedia requires "notability" -- evidence that reliable, independent sources have covered the subject. (Indig, Oct 2025)

**What builds notability:**
- Press coverage in tier-one publications (New York Times, Forbes, TechCrunch, BBC, etc.)
- Funding announcements covered by media
- Founders/executives who have spoken at notable conferences
- Podcast appearances on established shows
- Awards from recognized industry bodies
- Published research or reports cited by others

**Reality check:** Most startups and small businesses cannot get a Wikipedia page. If you don't have multiple independent media sources covering your company, focus on Wikidata first (lower notability threshold) and build toward Wikipedia eligibility over 12-24 months.

### Building Toward Eligibility

1. Accumulate 5+ independent media mentions in notable publications
2. Ensure founders/executives have their own professional visibility (LinkedIn, speaking events, published content)
3. Create a Wikidata entry first (lower barrier, still feeds LLM entity understanding)
4. Document all media coverage systematically -- you'll need it for Wikipedia citations
5. Do NOT create your own Wikipedia page -- have a neutral third party do it, or it will get flagged and removed

**Warning about Wikipedia moderators:**
> "There are a fun group of people who will actually report your page and take it down like unofficial moderators." -- Indig (Oct 2025)

## Digital PR Strategy

### Why Digital PR Is the Golden Era Play

> "Because of the onset of AI and how easy it is now to mass-produce low-grade content, the true editorial news houses are still looking to produce great editorially written journalist-first content. And the way they bolster the credibility is by featuring and quoting experts. Therein lies the opportunity." -- Chris Pantelli / Linkify (Oct 2025)

Digital PR links from tier-one publications serve double duty:
- **SEO value:** High-authority backlink (one DR70+ link > 100 DR20 links)
- **AI value:** Brand mention in a trusted source that LLMs cite

### Platforms for Sourcing PR Opportunities

| Platform | Type | Cost |
|----------|------|------|
| HARO (Help a Reporter Out) | Journalist request matching | Free (relaunched by Brett Farmiloe / Featured.com) |
| Source of Sources (SOS) | Journalist requests from HARO's original founder Peter Shankman | Free |
| Quoted (spelled with Q) | Journalist request matching | Free tier available |
| Featured.com | Expert matching to journalist queries | Paid tiers |

### How to Earn Tier-One Links

1. **Be PR-ready first:** Your About page must be prominent (not buried 4 clicks deep). Journalists will check your website to verify you are who you say you are. (Pantelli, Oct 2025)
2. **Detailed credentials page:** Qualifications, certifications, awards, accolades, speaking history -- "sing from the rooftops" about credentials (Pantelli, Oct 2025)
3. **Use dictation tools for authenticity:** Use Whisper Flow, VoiceInk, or similar tools to dictate expert responses in your own voice. Then use AI to clean up grammar/structure while preserving your authentic tone. This passes AI detection because it IS your voice. (Pantelli, Oct 2025)
4. **Build journalist relationships:** Follow journalists who cover your industry. Engage with their content on social media. Provide value before asking for anything. Relationships compound over time.
5. **Track mentions:** Set up Google Alerts and Talkwalker Alerts for your name, brand name, and variations (with and without spaces). When you're quoted without a link, send a polite email asking for the link addition. (Pantelli, Oct 2025)

### Custom GPT for PR Responses

Build a custom GPT/Claude project trained on:
- Hours of your own YouTube/podcast transcripts
- Your brand voice and tone
- Topics you will and will not discuss (especially important for YMYL)
- Words to avoid (AI slop words: "robust," "delve," "landscape," etc.)

Use this to draft PR responses that sound authentically like you. (Pantelli, Oct 2025)

## Brand Reputation Signals

### Controlling Your Brand Narrative Across Platforms

LLMs learn about your business from public profiles. Inaccuracies or inconsistencies across platforms confuse LLMs and reduce citation confidence.

> "LLMs learn about your business and its reputation from: Google Business Profile, Yelp, LinkedIn, Trustpilot, and so forth. Make sure you've claimed these listings and their information matches your website's About and FAQ pages." -- Matt Diggity (Jul 2025)

### Platform Checklist

| Platform | Action | Priority |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Google Business Profile | Claim, complete all fields, match website info exactly | Critical |
| LinkedIn (company page) | Complete profile, match messaging, active posting | High |
| LinkedIn (founder/CEO personal) | Detailed experience, matches company About page | High |
| Yelp | Claim listing, respond to reviews | Medium |
| Trustpilot | Claim profile, solicit reviews | Medium |
| Crunchbase | Complete profile (especially for SaaS/funded companies) | Medium |
| Industry directories | Toolify.ai, Product Hunt, There's An AI For That, G2, Capterra | High |

### Entity Consistency

All platforms must present consistent information about:
- What your company does (same core description)
- Who founded/runs it
- Key products/services
- Pricing (if public)
- Contact information
- Industry/category

Inconsistencies across platforms reduce LLM confidence in recommending you. If LinkedIn says you do X but your website says you do Y, the LLM may skip you entirely rather than risk providing inaccurate information.

## Podcast Strategy

### Why Podcasts Are a High-Value Signal

> "LLMs love transcripts and they're going to -- that's real. Me and you have not used any AI to say anything that's come out of our mouths today. This is all authentic, brand new content and information." -- Chris Pantelli (Oct 2025)

Podcasts deliver multiple signals simultaneously:
1. **Authentic human voice:** Transcripts of real conversation are nearly impossible to fake at scale
2. **Cross-platform backlinks:** One podcast episode generates links from YouTube, Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and dozens of other platforms
3. **Transcript indexing:** Podcast transcripts are crawled and cited by LLMs
4. **Relationship building:** Podcast hosts become allies who may mention you in future episodes

### Finding Podcast Opportunities

1. Go to **listennotes.com**
2. Search your keywords in quotes (e.g., "SEO marketing")
3. Change sort from "relevance" to "date" to find recently active shows
4. Find shows that recently discussed your topic
5. Reach out: "Hey, I saw you recently covered [topic]. I have a perspective on [angle] -- would you be interested in having me on?"

(Pantelli, Oct 2025)

### Maximizing Podcast Impact

- Mention specific entities during conversation: product names, specific metrics, competitor names, use cases
- Share original data/research if you have it -- this becomes quotable/citable
- Ask hosts to include your website link in show notes
- Request the transcript be published alongside the episode
- Repurpose key quotes on LinkedIn, your blog, and email newsletter

## E-E-A-T Signals for Third-Party Validation

### Author Credentials

Every content creator associated with your brand needs visible credentials:

- **About page:** Who built the site/product, why it exists, background of founders (Ahrefs, Oct 2025)
- **Author bios on articles:** Include credentials, years of experience, relevant certifications
- **Qualifications and certifications:** Display them prominently. "If your business has any industry certifications or awards, list them on your site. If you're a Michelin star restaurant, you'd have to be smoking crack not to display it." (Diggity, Jul 2025)
- **Case studies and original research:** "Show firsthand experience and proven results. This not only adds credibility but signals to AI systems." (Diggity, Jul 2025)

### Trust Page Checklist (Do Once, Verify Quarterly)

- [ ] About page with founder/team background and why the company exists
- [ ] Contact page with working email or form
- [ ] Privacy policy page
- [ ] HTTPS enabled
- [ ] No placeholder content anywhere on the site
- [ ] Industry certifications/awards displayed
- [ ] "Last updated" dates on time-sensitive content
- [ ] Transparent methodology where applicable
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure if earning commissions
- [ ] Social profiles linked from site with real engagement

## Community Building

### Indexable Communities Beat Dark Forests

> "Unfortunately there are so many websites and brands that rely on Discords and these platforms that are the so-called dark forest. But I have SaaS clients that actually have their forums. They have done a great job integrating the user experience and leveraging conversations." -- Aleyda Solis (Jul 2025)

**Indexable platforms that LLMs can crawl and cite:**
- Your own forum (Discourse, NodeBB, etc.) -- highest value because you own it
- Reddit (public subreddits)
- LinkedIn posts and articles
- Threads (getting more Google traffic with recent updates)
- YouTube comments
- Public GitHub discussions
- Stack Overflow / Stack Exchange
- Quora

**Non-indexable "dark forests" that LLMs CANNOT cite:**
- Discord (most servers are private)
- Slack communities
- Private Facebook groups
- WhatsApp groups
- Telegram channels

If your community lives in Discord, you are invisible to LLMs. Consider mirroring key discussions to an indexable platform.

### Community Manager Presence

> "You can see community managers disclosing they are community managers, by the way, helping the community out there not only in their own websites but also in third-party platforms and it's paying off." -- Aleyda Solis (Jul 2025)

Having identified community managers (who disclose their role) engaging helpfully on Reddit, forums, and other platforms is a legitimate, sustainable third-party signal.

## Monitoring What AI Says About Your Brand

### Regular Brand Audits Across Models

> "Start to regularly check AI platforms for what it knows and thinks about your brand." -- Matt Diggity (Jul 2025)

### Test Prompts to Use

Run these prompts across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity:

**Discovery prompts:**
- "What can you tell me about [brand name]?"
- "What does [brand name] do?"
- "Is [brand name] a good [product category]?"

**Comparison prompts:**
- "Compare [your brand] vs [competitor A] vs [competitor B]"
- "What's the best [product category] for [use case]?"
- "Best [product category] for [persona] with [constraint]?"

**Problem prompts:**
- "I'm a [persona] looking for [solution]. What do you recommend?"
- "What are the top [product category] tools for [specific use case]?"

### What to Check For

1. **Accuracy:** Is the information about you correct? If not, you need to update source platforms
2. **Framing:** Are you positioned favorably, neutrally, or negatively?
3. **Presence:** Do you appear at all? If not, third-party signals are insufficient
4. **Position:** Where in the recommendation list do you appear? (Indig tracks "average position in AI answers")
5. **Citations:** What sources are being cited when you are (or aren't) mentioned?

### Account for Variables

When testing AI answers, account for factors that skew results:

- **Geography:** If you're in the US but your ICP is in Europe, answers will differ. Use VPN or testing tools
- **Persona memory:** ChatGPT builds a knowledge graph of you. Testing in your own workspace skews results toward your profile. Use incognito/fresh sessions or tools like Scrunch
- **Model differences:** Each model has biases. Test all major models, not just one. (Indig, Oct 2025)

### Tools for AI Monitoring

| Tool | What It Does |
|------|-------------|
| Ahrefs Brand Radar | Shows mentions of your brand vs competitors across AI answers |
| Scrunch | AI visibility monitoring across models (agency partner tool) |
| Profound | Citation tracking across ChatGPT and other LLMs |
| Peak | AI visibility monitoring |
| Google Alerts | Free brand mention monitoring (traditional web) |
| Talkwalker Alerts | Free brand mention monitoring |

## Key Principle: Integrated Strategy, Not Isolated Tactics

> "This is just an integrated strategy. Just focusing on one channel was really never a good strategy. If you're creating a blog, you should also do a YouTube video of it. You should do a summary of it on LinkedIn. You should send a summary to your newsletter." -- Indig (Oct 2025)

Every piece of content you create should ripple across multiple platforms. A blog post becomes a YouTube video becomes a LinkedIn post becomes a Reddit discussion point becomes a podcast talking point. Each ripple creates another third-party signal that LLMs can discover and cite.

The brands that win in AI search are not the ones doing one thing well. They are the ones creating a web of interconnected, verifiable signals across every platform that LLMs trust.
